## TL;DR

R3 trains compact rubric-agnostic reward models on point-wise, pair-wise, and binary evaluation data with generated rubrics and explanation traces.

The useful object is not just a reward score: it is a heterogeneous judgment record with rubric text, score or preference label, and generated explanation. Benchmark gains should not be read as proof that the generated rubric or explanation is correct.

## 1. What is this work?

The paper builds reward models that can operate across evaluation formats: scalar scoring, pairwise preference, and binary or classification-style judgments.

For this atlas, it belongs in Preference & Reward Feedback Data because it exposes a reusable rubric-conditioned feedback format rather than only reporting evaluator accuracy.

## 2. What data object does it expose?

The relevant record can include:

- instruction,
- task description,
- input,
- response or response pair,
- evaluation rubric,
- score,
- pairwise choice,
- classification label,
- reasoning or explanation.

Prompt sources are public datasets across general chat, reasoning, classification, factuality, summarization, natural-language inference, and preference tasks.

## 3. What is the verifier / reward / judge / environment?

The verifier is model-judged and mixed. R3 produces reasoned score assignments across point-wise, pair-wise, and binary evaluation formats.

The feedback contract is judgment required: candidates are scored, ranked, or classified according to an associated rubric and feedback format.

## 4. How is the data constructed?

Reported construction details include:

- sample from a 4M+ raw pool into 20K diverse examples,
- generate rubrics, negative answers, and explanation traces,
- summarize traces over 4096 tokens,
- filter incorrect predictions and trivial examples by small-model agreement,
- train Qwen3-based reward models with LoRA and LLaMA Factory.

Reported teachers include GPT-4.1 mini for rubric and negative-answer generation, plus reasoning distillation from DeepSeek-R1.

## 5. How can it enter post-training?

Recorded use:

- reward_modeling,
- evaluation.

Use it as a reward-modeling recipe or evaluator artifact. Keep the rubric and explanation fields attached; collapsing the record into only a scalar score would lose much of the audit surface.

## 6. What should readers audit?

- Explanation traces may rationalize a score after the fact.
- Rubric-agnostic training can still fail on unseen rubric wording or missing preference dimensions.
- Heterogeneous source labels can encode incompatible notions of quality.
- The public model/data links should be pinned to exact revisions before reuse.
- Benchmark gains are not evidence that the feedback labels are clean.

## 7. What is missing or risky?

- Exact GitHub and Hugging Face revisions.
- Licenses for each source dataset in the mixture.
- Whether train/evaluation splits are recoverable from the released data.
- How generated rubrics and generated explanations were filtered.
- Whether local tasks match the original source mixture.

## 8. Why it matters for post-training reasoning data

R3 makes rubric text and generated explanation first-class parts of the reward-data object. That is useful for comparing reward data that looks similar as scalar labels but differs substantially in feedback provenance.
