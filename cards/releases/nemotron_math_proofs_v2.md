## TL;DR

Nemotron-Math-Proofs-v2 is an NVIDIA release of AoPS-derived synthetic proof, verification, and meta-verification conversations generated by DeepSeek-V4-Pro. Its embedded natural-language scores are not formal proof checks; release completeness, filtering, failure prevalence, and AoPS source rights remain unresolved.

## 1. What is this work?

- Year / venue: 2026 · NVIDIA Hugging Face dataset release.
- Atlas roles: data release, process supervision, and construction recipe.
- Domain / use: mathematical proofs, SFT, and evaluation.
- Status: partial.

It replaces only the Cascade-2 natural-language-proof subset, not the associated proof-refinement data.

## 2. What data object does it expose?

The record is a two-turn proof, verification, or meta-verification conversation. Its public fields are `messages`, `problem`, `subset`, and `uuid`; seeds are an AoPS-derived subset of Nemotron-Math-Proofs-v1, and traces are generated in DeepSeek-V4-Pro Max inference mode. The release does not expose a row-level formal proof artifact, accepted/rejected flag, or documented schema for score extraction.

## 3. What is the verifier / reward / judge / environment?

Verification is natural-language teacher judgment using DeepSeekMath-V2-style prompts, with boxed `0`, `0.5`, or `1` signals embedded in text. There is no formal checker, calibration study, false-positive/false-negative analysis, or disclosed terminal predicate. A natural-language verification trace must not be treated as a theorem-prover certificate.

## 4. How is the data constructed?

1. Take the AoPS-derived v1 problem subset.
2. Use DeepSeek-V4-Pro Max inference mode to generate proof, verification, and meta-verification conversations.
3. Apply DeepSeekMath-V2-style natural-language verification prompts.
4. Release the resulting train JSONL.

Filtering rule, temperature, inference budget, base model, rollout count, and optimizer are `unknown`.

## 5. How can it enter post-training?

It can support proof SFT and evaluation only after parsing the embedded scores, retaining the exact dataset revision, and performing independent proof-quality validation. It is unsuitable as formal-process supervision without an external proof checker or an independently audited annotation protocol.

## 6. What should readers audit?

- Pin the JSONL revision `7665d7f1d006fd89aa852a9dab8060c60b63f814` and independently enumerate its records.
- Reconcile the card's 82,737-record claim with the Dataset Server's partial 23,799-record conversion.
- Audit score parsing, score distribution, verifier prompt, teacher configuration, and agreement with an external checker or experts.
- Establish AoPS source provenance, redistribution rights, train/evaluation overlap, and decontamination.
- Determine filtering/retention logic and whether failed or low-score traces are represented.

## 7. What is missing or risky?

The Dataset Server is partial relative to the declared total. Filtering, failed-trace prevalence, decontamination, and independent correctness evidence are `unknown`; scores are embedded in natural language and may be ambiguous to parse. The release claims CC-BY-4.0, but AoPS source rights are not established by that claim. No benchmark result is evidence that these individual proof traces are correct.

## 8. Why it matters for post-training reasoning data

It makes proof plus self-verification traces available while retaining the boundary between a model's natural-language judgment and a formal proof contract. That boundary is central when reusing the records as training labels.
