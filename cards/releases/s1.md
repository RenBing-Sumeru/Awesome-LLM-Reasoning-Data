# s1: Simple Test-Time Scaling

> Curation level: L5_audit_ready
> Category: construction_recipes_open_reasoning_data, scaling_test_time_compute_rlvr
> Links: [📄 Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.19393)

## TL;DR

s1 curates a small s1K reasoning dataset and studies budget forcing as a simple way to scale test-time reasoning compute.

It is a useful counterpoint to massive-data recipes: careful small-set curation plus inference-budget control can materially change reasoning performance.

## 1. What is this work?

- Year / venue: 2025 · arXiv.
- Atlas role: construction_recipe, scaling_study.
- Domains: math.
- Current status: verified.
- Why it belongs: Core small-data and test-time-scaling reference for studying s1K curation, reasoning traces, and budget forcing.

## 2. What data object does it expose?

- Prompt/source: 1,000 curated reasoning questions selected for difficulty, diversity, and quality.
- Trace/action author: reasoning traces paired with selected questions.
- Answer/artifact format: question, reasoning trace, final answer.
- Process fields: selection criteria, trace, answer, budget-forcing intervention.
- Environment or substrate: offline SFT plus inference-time decoding control.
- Terminal predicate: benchmark answer correctness under controlled test-time compute.

## 3. What is the verifier / reward / judge / environment?

- Verification contract: benchmark answer scoring and test-time behavior analysis.
- Recorded verifier/reward/environment: final-answer evaluation with budget forcing.
- Supervision granularity: trace_level for SFT and answer_level for evaluation.

## 4. How is the data constructed?

- Base model: Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct in the reported setup.
- Teacher: curated reasoning traces.
- Generator: model response under budget forcing at inference time.
- Filtering rule: difficulty, diversity, and quality criteria for s1K.
- Sampling protocol: small curated set rather than broad web-scale corpus.
- Inference budget: explicitly controlled by terminating or extending thinking.
- Optimizer/scaffold: SFT on s1K plus budget forcing at test time.

## 5. How can it enter post-training?

Recorded training/evaluation use: sft, test_time_compute.

Use it to ask whether a claimed data gain is really a data-selection effect, an inference-budget effect, or both.

## 6. What should readers audit?

- Are s1K selection criteria reproducible?
- Where did reasoning traces come from?
- How sensitive are results to budget forcing prompts?
- Are benchmark improvements stable across tasks?
- Does small-set curation overfit visible benchmarks?

## 7. What is missing or risky?

- Small curated sets can overfit benchmark style.
- Budget forcing changes inference behavior without changing data.
- Trace provenance and selection criteria must stay visible.

## 8. Why it matters for post-training reasoning data

It is a useful counterpoint to massive-data recipes: careful small-set curation plus inference-budget control can materially change reasoning performance.

The reusable lesson is to identify the feedback-bearing record: prompt, trace, label, preference, reward, verifier, environment state, benchmark item, or audit evidence.
