The paper reports that DataPRM improves Best-of-N policy performance by 7.21% on ScienceAgentBench and 11.28% on DABStep, and that its RL setting reaches 78.73% on DABench and 64.84% on TableBench.

These results support the environment-aware feedback design in the reported data-analysis benchmarks. They do not establish that a reward model can safely generalize to a new notebook runtime, a different database, unavailable tools, or a scientific task whose ground truth cannot be probed through execution.
