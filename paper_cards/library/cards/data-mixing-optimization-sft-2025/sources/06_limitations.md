The method depends on validation sets that represent the desired downstream domains. Its own Orca results show that lower held-out loss can fail to improve downstream averages when the source-defined domains and evaluation tasks are misaligned. It operates on coarse domains, while a source such as FLAN may internally contain many heterogeneous tasks; repeated sampling of a small favored domain can also cause overfitting, which the paper links to an IFEval drop. The paper releases its PMLR record but does not declare a dedicated implementation or new standalone dataset, so its perturbation, fitting, and data-processing choices must be reconstructed and upstream dataset terms checked independently.
