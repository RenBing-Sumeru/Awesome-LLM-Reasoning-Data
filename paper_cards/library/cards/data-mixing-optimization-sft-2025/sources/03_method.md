The method begins with a small balanced SFT run, then perturbs one domain at a time through four alternative data-size ratios while holding the remaining domains fixed. It records validation loss after every run and fits a domain-specific formula with Huber loss and a constrained trust-region procedure. The formula contains a scaling term for the selected domain and an effective-data-from-transfer term for the other domains, with constraints preventing the transfer estimate from exceeding the available out-of-domain data.

For a target budget, the fitted curves are summed and optimized with sequential least-squares programming under nonnegative weights that add to one. Controlled experiments use instruction following, math, and code, with ratios one-half, one-third, two, and three around a 660,000-token unit size. The paper also reweights six skill domains of Tulu3 and source-defined domains in Orca, and evaluates model-specific results for Llama3.1-8B, Llama3.1-70B, and Qwen2.5-32B.
