In the controlled three-domain study, weights predicted from small perturbation experiments approach the best mixtures found by exhaustive grid search over 21 combinations. The abstract reports that the predicted mixture's per-domain loss is on average only 0.66% above the best per-domain loss achieved by grid search. This is the direct evidence for the claimed extrapolation: the fitted loss surface is useful enough to select a full-budget mixture without retraining every possible weight combination.

For Tulu3 reweighting, the reported average validation perplexity improves from 2.162 to 2.150 for Llama3.1-8B and from 2.127 to 2.080 for Llama3.1-70B; Qwen2.5-32B improves from 2.165 to 2.133. On downstream Tulu3 tasks, the learned mixture gives averages of 50.4 and 63.8 for the two Llama models, compared with 50.1 and 61.1 for the original mixture. The paper also reports a counterexample: on Orca, held-out loss and downstream performance need not agree, so validation alignment is a condition of the method rather than a universal guarantee.
