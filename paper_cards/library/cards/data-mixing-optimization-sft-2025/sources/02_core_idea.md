The paper represents the value of a domain by a fitted validation-loss curve. For a target domain, its effective data is the amount of its own data plus an estimated transfer contribution from all other domains; the resulting effective size is inserted into a fine-tuning scaling law. This separates two effects that a static mixture hides: diminishing returns from more in-domain tokens and transfer from the rest of the mixture.

After fitting five parameters per domain from small perturbation runs, the method solves for weights on the probability simplex that minimize the sum of estimated per-domain validation losses. Equal domain weighting in the objective targets a broadly capable model, while user-selected domain multipliers can instead target a specialty. The proposed mixture is therefore an explicit optimization result tied to the data budget, evaluation domains, and base model, rather than a fixed heuristic.
