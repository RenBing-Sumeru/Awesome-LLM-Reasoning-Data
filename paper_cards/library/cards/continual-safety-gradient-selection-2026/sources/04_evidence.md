The paper compares low-, moderate-, and high-gradient selections, full-data fine-tuning, clipping, and related baselines across several model families and task orders. It measures attack success and safety-basin preservation alongside task performance.

The authors report that moderate-gradient selection preserves 83–88 percent of alignment in their reported setting and is robust across tested selection ratios. The evidence is empirical and task-specific, not a formal guarantee for arbitrary safety policies.
