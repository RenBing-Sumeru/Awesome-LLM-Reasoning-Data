COPR requires historical optimal-policy information and a choice of constraint strength, both of which can add storage, bookkeeping, and tuning burden as the task stream grows. Preserving old policies is not inherently desirable when old and new preferences conflict or when earlier labels contain harmful bias. The reported benchmark sequences may also be simpler than real feedback streams with drifting users, unclear task boundaries, and missing historical data. A deployment should test order sensitivity, subgroup behavior, old-new conflict cases, and the cost of retaining the necessary policy state.
