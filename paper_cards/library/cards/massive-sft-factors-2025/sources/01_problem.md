SFT data selection is often guided by topical relevance, dataset reputation, or a small number of model-task comparisons. Those rules are hard to trust when a base model, a corpus, the tuning method, sample count, and the benchmark all interact. A data source that improves one model or task can have little effect, or a negative effect, elsewhere; uncontrolled public checkpoints cannot separate those causes.

This paper asks which data and training factors actually predict SFT gains under controlled comparisons. Its intervention is a source-specific SFT dataset, with either 1,000 or 20,000 sampled records, consumed by full-parameter tuning or LoRA. The study is an audit of training-data compatibility and downstream response, not a proposal for a new answer verifier or a new instruction corpus.
