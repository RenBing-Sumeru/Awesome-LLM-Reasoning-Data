The practical lesson is that compatibility is a relation between a base model and a training record, not an intrinsic property of the record. Low perplexity is a strong empirical screen in this study, but the paper also shows dataset effects remain model-specific and that more data is not reliably better. Read the mid-layer analysis as an interpretability observation, not a prescription to update only those layers. For data curation, the most reusable artifact is the controlled evaluation matrix: it makes selection, tuning, and assessment assumptions visible enough to challenge or reproduce them.
