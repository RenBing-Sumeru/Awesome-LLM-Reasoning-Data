The experiments focus on 7–9B models, approximately ten popular English training datasets, and English SFT even when the base models include Chinese and Japanese variants. The authors explicitly leave larger models, mixture-of-experts architectures, wider multilingual instruction data, and specialized domains for future work. Perplexity is described as a proxy for compatibility rather than a causal explanation, and correlations can vary by model family. The public release contains evaluation results and nearly all trained models, but reproducing the full experiment requires large storage—about 80 TB for all models according to the repository—and separate access to every upstream dataset.
