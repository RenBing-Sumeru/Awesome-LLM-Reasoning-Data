The study fine-tunes twelve approximately 7B-scale English, Chinese, and Japanese base models on ten English SFT datasets in four groups: general tasks, coding, mathematics, and classic NLP. Overlong examples are removed under a shared tokenizer constraint, then each source is sampled to 1,000 examples; a focused three-model experiment also compares 1,000 with 20,000 examples. Both full-parameter and LoRA training are run, producing 1,070 models in total.

The models are evaluated with OpenCompass on twelve benchmarks covering mathematics, coding, knowledge, examinations, and instruction following. The analysis compares changes from each base model, correlates training-data properties with benchmark gains, measures layer-wise weight deltas, and maps 757 checkpoints through log-likelihood vectors. The public repository supplies the consolidated evaluation dataframe and downloads for nearly all SFT models; it does not claim to republish the ten upstream training datasets as one new dataset.
