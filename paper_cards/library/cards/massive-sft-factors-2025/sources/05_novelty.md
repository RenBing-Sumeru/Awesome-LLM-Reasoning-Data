Prior SFT papers often isolate one model family, one training corpus, or one factor such as dataset size or LoRA. This work changes the evidence surface by crossing models, source datasets, sample counts, tuning methods, and benchmarks under shared preprocessing and evaluation. It thereby asks a more operational data question: not whether a corpus resembles a benchmark, but whether the base model can absorb that corpus in a way that predicts a useful downstream change.

The novelty is the released, controlled SFT landscape and its connection among corpus perplexity, performance change, and layer-wise adaptation. It does not invent perplexity or LoRA. Its contribution is showing, across the reported grid, why a selection pipeline should record the base model used for scoring, the exact sampled data count, tuning method, and benchmark contract, since no source ranking is model-independent.
