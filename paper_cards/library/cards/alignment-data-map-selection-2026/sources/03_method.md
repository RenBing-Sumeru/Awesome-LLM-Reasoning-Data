For each record containing an instruction and two or more responses, the method obtains a response-level alignment score. It takes the mean as quality and the variance as variability. To form three non-overlapping regions, it assigns the highest-variability 33% to High Variance, then divides the remaining records by quality into Low Average and High Average. For a labeled record, it additionally compares the label vector with the score vector through cosine similarity.

The experiments select 33% subsets from UltraFeedback and Preference-Dissection, train Mistral-7B and LLaMA-3-8B with DPO or SimPO, and evaluate MT-Bench, Evol-Instruct, and AlpacaEval. They repeat selection with GPT-4 labels, ArmoRM scores, and GPT-4o-reference similarity. Reproduction requires the scoring model or reference, response set, region thresholds, label encoding, dataset revision, training hyperparameters, and evaluator versions; changing any score source changes the map and therefore the training data.
