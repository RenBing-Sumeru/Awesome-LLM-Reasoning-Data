With LLaMA-3-8B and SimPO on UltraFeedback, the 33% High Average subset reports an AlpacaEval win rate of 25.24%, compared with 17.54% for full-data training, 4.90% for a random 33% subset, and 4.23% for the High Variance region. The same subset attains 50.9% MT-Bench win rate and 47.9% Evol-Instruct win rate, providing a concrete example that selection by both quality and variability can outperform data volume in this setting.

The paper also checks the selection criterion across three scoring methods and reports that the High Average region remains best among compared regions. Its label-diagnosis experiment finds that high label-score correlation subsets generally train better than low-correlation subsets; a qualitative example shows an exact answer receiving a lower original label score than irrelevant responses but the highest alignment score. This supports diagnosis as a targeted review signal, not proof that the scoring source is itself a ground-truth annotator.
