Prior selection rules commonly rely on a pairwise margin or on response quality alone. Alignment Data Map changes the selection object into a two-dimensional record characterization: variability captures how difficult a preference comparison is, and average quality captures whether the candidates are worth learning from. The label-score correlation extends the same scored record into a diagnostic surface rather than treating selection and annotation review as separate pipelines.

The contribution is not a new human-feedback dataset, a new judge, or a new DPO objective. Its distinctive reusable artifact is the decision rule that locates a record before training and makes the scoring provenance part of that decision. Before reuse, compare against quality-only and variability-only selections, audit cases that the score source flags as low correlation, and check whether the preferred region underrepresents difficult prompts, languages, or safety-critical behavior.
