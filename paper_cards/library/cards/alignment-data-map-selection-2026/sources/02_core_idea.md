The map represents response quality by the mean alignment score of a prompt's candidates and ambiguity by the variance of those scores. Alignment scores may come from an LLM judge, a reward model, or similarity to a strong reference answer. Low variance generalizes the familiar small-margin condition, while the mean separates genuinely good supervision from uniformly weak candidate sets.

The preferred region is high average quality and low variability. The paper calls it High Average and selects it after first removing the high-variability portion; it also uses the cosine correlation between collected labels and response scores to flag suspicious annotations. Thus the same representation controls two practical decisions: which unlabeled records deserve labeling or training, and which labeled records deserve review.
