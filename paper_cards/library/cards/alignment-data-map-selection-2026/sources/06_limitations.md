The map inherits bias and error from the LLM judge, reward model, or reference answer used to score responses, and its reported evaluations rely largely on automatic and LLM-based metrics. Its region boundaries are design choices, while a low label-score correlation can indicate annotation noise but can also expose a scoring model's blind spot. The paper evaluates selected subsets in the reported alignment datasets and benchmarks, so a new use should add human spot checks, subgroup coverage checks, and tests for whether selection removes rare but important preference cases.
