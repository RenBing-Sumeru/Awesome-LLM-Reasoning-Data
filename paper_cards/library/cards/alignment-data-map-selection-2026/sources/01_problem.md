Preference-data selection often uses a reward margin: pairs whose candidate scores are close are treated as informative, while pairs with a large gap are treated as easy. That signal cannot distinguish two low-margin records in which both responses are excellent from two in which both are poor. It also gives no direct way to identify whether a collected preference label agrees with the quality evidence available for its candidates.

Alignment Data Map asks which preference records should be sent to optimization when both absolute response quality and the ambiguity among responses matter. It maps each instruction and its candidate set by mean alignment score and score variability, then uses the map for selection and label diagnosis. The paper changes a record-selection decision before DPO or SimPO training; it is not a new preference loss or a general-purpose benchmark.
