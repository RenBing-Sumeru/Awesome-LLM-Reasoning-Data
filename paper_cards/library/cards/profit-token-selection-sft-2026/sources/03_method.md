For a reference sequence, ProFit detaches the model probability at position t and defines a binary mask that retains the cross-entropy term only when that probability exceeds a static threshold τ. The stop-gradient gate prevents the discrete masking rule from being optimized through directly. All retained token losses are averaged over the response, whereas standard SFT applies cross-entropy to every reference token. The method uses current online probabilities and needs neither an external reference model nor multiple reference responses.

The main SFT experiments curate 2,000 high-reward samples from BAAI-InfinityInstruct and train Qwen3, Llama-3.1, and OLMo-2 families. They compare full-token SFT with entropy-based tuning and Dynamic Fine-tuning, and evaluate GPQA-Diamond, GSM8K, MATH-500, AIME'24, and IFEval. The paper additionally examines threshold and LoRA-rank behavior, re-fine-tunes Qwen2.5-Instruct models, and initializes GRPO on DeepScaleR from ProFit, standard SFT, or DFT checkpoints.
