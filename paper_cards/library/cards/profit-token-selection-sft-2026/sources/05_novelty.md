Existing curation commonly accepts or rejects an entire instruction-response pair, and existing token objectives may reweight tokens or rely on external reference models. ProFit changes the granularity of the training decision: it uses an online, hard probability gate to suppress part of a retained response. Thus the data record is not discarded; its supervision surface changes at token level according to the current policy's confidence.

The distinctive claim is that high predicted probability can proxy core semantic content in the reported tasks, and that hard masking of the complementary tokens reduces gradient interference. It is not a general theorem that probability equals importance. The semantic annotation, threshold ablation, and comparison with entropy and DFT are necessary parts of the contribution because they test whether the particular direction of masking—not merely token weighting—accounts for the reported gains.
