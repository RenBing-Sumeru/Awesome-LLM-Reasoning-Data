The authors restrict their central assumption to logic-intensive reasoning and mathematics: in creative generation, low-probability tokens may carry valuable stylistic diversity rather than disposable expression. The fixed threshold is shared by all samples, so it cannot adapt to instance difficulty or a model's changing calibration. The semantic analysis uses a Gemini-3-Pro judge, while the training method itself uses model probabilities; neither establishes a universal ground truth of token importance. Code is publicly linked, but the paper does not release a new standalone training dataset, and its curated 2,000-sample slice and upstream InfinityInstruct terms must be reconstructed and checked before reuse.
