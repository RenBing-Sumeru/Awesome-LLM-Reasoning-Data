The important distinction is between token confidence as a training gate and token confidence as a verifier. ProFit does not establish that a high-probability token is correct; it argues that, within a correct reference in its studied regime, high-probability tokens are often the more stable semantic anchors for SFT. The most useful replication checks are the semantic-label analysis, fraction of tokens retained at each threshold, low-probability-only control, and whether a masked SFT initialization helps or harms the next training consumer such as GRPO.
