ProFit assumes that, for the logic-heavy tasks it studies, high-probability reference tokens tend to carry core semantics and low-probability tokens are more often replaceable phrasing. It uses the model being trained to compute token probabilities online, then masks losses for tokens below a fixed threshold. The remaining high-probability tokens define the supervised target, so one response supplies a selective signal rather than an all-token imitation target.

The paper frames this as avoiding interference rather than generating more answers. A semantic study labels core and trivial tokens in multiple answers with Gemini-3-Pro, then finds their probability distributions differ significantly: the reported mean probability is 0.768 for core tokens and 0.485 for trivial ones, with a hypothesis-test p-value of 1×10⁻⁶. The authors also derive a gradient lower bound showing why low-probability tokens can dominate updates even when they are not the desired semantic signal.
