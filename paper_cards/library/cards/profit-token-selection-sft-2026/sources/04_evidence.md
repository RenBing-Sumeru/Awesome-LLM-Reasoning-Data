On Qwen3-4B-Base, ProFit reaches a five-benchmark average of 52.33, compared with 41.39 for standard SFT and 50.30 for Dynamic Fine-tuning. On Qwen3-14B-Base, standard SFT is 1.88 points below the vanilla model's average, whereas ProFit changes that to a 5.64-point gain; on Qwen3-0.6B, ProFit averages 31.49 versus 28.51 for SFT. The threshold ablation also reports that retaining only low-probability tokens performs below standard SFT, while retaining high-probability tokens beats it across the shown thresholds.

The evidence goes beyond base-model SFT. For Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, ProFit reaches 54.88 average versus 54.71 for the original instruction model and 52.88 for standard SFT, although it is not uniformly better at every scale. As an RL initialization, it reaches MATH-500 Avg@4 57.3 and Pass@4 76.4, compared with 53.1 and 70.6 for the baseline. These are reported results on a 2,000-sample, reasoning-oriented setup; they do not prove that masking low-probability tokens preserves semantic value in creative or open-ended generation.
