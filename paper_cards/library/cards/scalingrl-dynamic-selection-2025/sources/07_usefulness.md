Use this paper when an RL pipeline must decide continuously how to spend scarce rollout and update budget across a known prompt pool. Its most transferable lesson is to separate coverage-preserving pool construction from model-state-dependent sampling, then make the sampling score depend on signals that predict useful optimization rather than on generic quality alone. It is especially useful as a design reference for a mathematical RL curriculum, but any reuse should replace its source-specific filters, external-model estimators, and reward thresholds with audited choices for the target domain.
