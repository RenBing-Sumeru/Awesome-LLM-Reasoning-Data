Large-scale reasoning RL commonly treats a prompt pool as static: every retained mathematical question is repeatedly available for policy updates. That wastes computation on questions already solved by the current policy, on questions so hard that they yield nearly uniform failure, and on long responses whose optimization dynamics may be unhelpful. A smaller fixed subset does not solve the second problem, because the usefulness of a prompt changes as the policy changes.

The paper asks how to decide which mathematical prompts should be used at each stage of RL, while preserving enough coverage for generalization. Its target is not a universal data-quality ranking. It is a stage-dependent training surface that selects questions with an appropriate current difficulty, a useful reasoning-path complexity, and a reward history capable of supplying a learning signal.
