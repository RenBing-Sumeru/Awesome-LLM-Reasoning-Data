The contribution is not simply that fewer examples can work. The paper turns a prompt's current training value into a three-part, time-varying decision: difficulty avoids both trivial and unreachable tasks, reasoning-path complexity controls the response-length dimension, and reward adaptability removes examples at reward saturation. This makes sample selection part of the learning schedule rather than a preprocessing choice made once.

It also couples broad distribution control to local adaptation. The 8K pool keeps a prescribed easy, medium, and hard composition, whereas the dynamic sampler chooses particular records within those groups as the policy evolves. Compared with a static curriculum or a reward-only filter, the design explicitly aims to preserve coverage while changing which records consume PPO rollout and update budget.
