The initial construction stage applies structural and mathematical-syntax checks, language-model clarity checks, domain stratification, and model-based difficulty bounds. Problems solved by Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B are removed as trivial; overly long solution traces are also excluded. The paper describes an 8K candidate set with 4.9K easy, 1.4K medium, and 2.1K hard examples, preserving those broad difficulty proportions while changing the sampled members over epochs.

During PPO training, difficulty is estimated from repeated DeepSeek-R1 answers, complexity from sampled solution length, and reward adaptability from recent mean reward. These terms form a learned weighted score. At every epoch, a temperature-controlled probability distribution and threshold choose the prompts used for rollouts; PPO then gives correct boxed answers a reward of 1, well-formatted wrong answers -0.5, and formatting failures -1.
