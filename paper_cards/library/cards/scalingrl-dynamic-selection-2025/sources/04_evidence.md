On a Qwen2.5-MATH-7B-Instruct backbone, the reported dynamic 1.5K-sample run reaches 84.2 on MATH500, 22.5 on AIME 2024, and 67.5 on AMC 2023, for a 58.1 average in the paper's comparison table. The same table reports 81.4, 22.5, and 63.3 for the static 8K filtered set, and 81.4, 20.3, and 58.6 for a random 1.5K subset. The paper also reports that the full three-term score outperforms every one- or two-term ablation on MATH500.

The efficiency claim is substantial but should be read as a reported experimental configuration, not a universal cost law. The authors contrast four hours on eight A800 GPUs for the 1.5K dynamic regime with thirteen days for the 220K regime, while also disclosing an approximately fourteen-to-fifteen-hour one-time construction pipeline. Evaluation covers MATH500, AIME 2024 and 2025, AMC 2023, and OlympiadBench, but all are mathematical reasoning settings.
