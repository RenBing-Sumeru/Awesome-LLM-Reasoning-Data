ScalingRL separates one-time pool construction from repeated training-time selection. It first removes malformed, ambiguous, trivial, excessively long, and distributionally redundant records from a 220K mathematical pool, leaving an 8K candidate set with controlled topic and difficulty composition. The later decision is made within that candidate set rather than by committing to all of it for every update.

For each candidate, the Data Effectiveness Score combines relative difficulty, sampled chain-of-thought complexity, and recent reward adaptability. The preferred region moves through training: early updates favor questions near the pool's typical difficulty, while later updates move toward harder and more complex questions, excluding reward extremes that provide little gradient information. A decaying-temperature sampler converts the score into the next batch composition.
