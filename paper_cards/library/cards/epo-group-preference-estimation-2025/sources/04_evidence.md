The reported results show gains across arithmetic and commonsense reasoning settings. For Llama-2-13B, EPO improves accuracy over SFT by 5.43 percentage points on GSM8K and 3.29 on the MetaMath subset; for Qwen2.5-7B, the corresponding improvements are 2.64 and 2.05. The paper also reports increases of 3.58 on SocialIQA for Llama-3-8B, 4.47 on ARC-Easy for Mistral-7B, 6.94 on ARC-Challenge for Llama-2-13B, and 6.29 on MathQA for Mistral-7B.

Several analyses test the construction choices. The study finds that EPO consistently exceeds the listed preference-optimization baselines across its tasks, while ordinary DPO can damage performance in some mathematical settings. It reports that longer group-response limits generally improve estimation until a limit below 20 harms performance, and that response sampling distribution matters: selecting groups from high-probability correct and low-probability incorrect responses is not equivalent to random construction. A false-positive analysis finds roughly 3.52 to 6.00 percent of positive samples may be mislabeled as sample count rises.
