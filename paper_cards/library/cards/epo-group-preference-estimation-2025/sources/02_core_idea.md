EPO interprets the reward of a response group as the expectation of the rewards of its members. Rather than deciding that one sampled correct rationale is the whole preferred signal, it asks whether the preferred group has higher expected reward than the dispreferred group. Each response's contribution is weighted by its reference-model probability, so a response that is more likely under the sampling reference affects the optimization direction more strongly.

This turns multi-sampling into an explicit data-construction decision. The model first generates several candidate answers for the same question; correctness places them in groups; EPO consumes the group statistics rather than collapsing them to one arbitrary pair. When both groups contain one response, the objective reduces to ordinary DPO. The paper therefore frames DPO as a limiting case of a broader record that retains uncertainty and variation among sampled solutions.
