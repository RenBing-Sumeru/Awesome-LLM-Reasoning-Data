The demonstrated construction relies on tasks with binary verifiable answers, and the authors do not provide a method for calculating comparable group expectations in subjective alignment settings. Rule-based answer checks create false positives and the rate grows somewhat as more candidates are sampled. The choice of response length limit, sample count, smoothing, and group sampling distribution can change the resulting signal. Evidence is limited to the reported open models and datasets, and the authors note non-commercial restrictions for the models they use.
