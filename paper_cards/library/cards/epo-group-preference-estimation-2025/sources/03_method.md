After supervised fine-tuning, the model samples 20 responses per training query at temperature 0.7 and adds the original rationale to the candidate pool. A rule-based verifier checks each final answer. Candidates matching the target answer enter the preferred group, and the others enter the dispreferred group. Prompts with no incorrect sampled answer are discarded because they cannot form both groups. The group-level data record retains all of these responses rather than choosing one winner and one loser.

EPO calculates a probability-weighted mean of policy/reference log ratios inside each group and applies a logistic loss to the difference between preferred and dispreferred expectations. A smoothing coefficient controls instability caused by incomplete probability sums. The method may truncate responses in both groups to a fixed length, because the authors find that late tokens can destabilize optimization. It is evaluated after SFT on arithmetic and commonsense reasoning datasets against RFT, DPO, batched DPO, RPO, and Step-DPO.
