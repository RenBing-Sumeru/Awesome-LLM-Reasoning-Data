The central novelty is to elevate a collection of rollouts to the unit of preference supervision. Prior pairwise methods decide a learning direction from two individual responses. EPO instead preserves multiple correct and incorrect trajectories, computes a weighted group expectation, and sends that aggregate to the loss. This changes the training record itself: the response label is no longer a binary edge between two texts but a structured partition of a prompt's sampled behavior.

The approach also links a simple verifiable answer signal to a richer reward estimate. Correctness is not claimed to describe every quality of a reasoning trace; it supplies a partition from which EPO estimates a less sample-sensitive direction. The optional length limitation is another explicit decision about which part of each grouped trace enters training. Together, grouping, probability weighting, and truncation define a training consumer distinct from merely increasing DPO batch size.
