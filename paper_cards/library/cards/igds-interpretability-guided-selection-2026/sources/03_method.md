The method separates a small feature-identification set, a larger candidate pool, and a final test set. For an instruction-tuned model, a sparse autoencoder converts hidden states at a task-specific position into sparse features. High-frequency features are only candidates: IGDS adds each feature's influence vector to the residual stream on validation examples and ranks it by the resulting task-performance gain.

It then freezes the validated feature set, computes a Feature-Resonant Score for each candidate record, and retains the top fraction for SFT of the corresponding base model. The reported math pool has 93.7K OpenR1-Math-220k examples and is evaluated on MATH-500 with pass@8; summarization and translation use separate DialogSum and WMT24/WMT24++ splits. Reuse requires the exact SAE, layer and token position, intervention protocol, split boundaries, and selection ratio.
