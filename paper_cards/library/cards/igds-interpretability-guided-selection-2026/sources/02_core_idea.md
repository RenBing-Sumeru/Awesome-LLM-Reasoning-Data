The contribution is a bridge from mechanistic evidence to a data decision. IGDS first uses sparse autoencoders to identify frequently active candidate features, then keeps only features whose amplification produces a positive change on a small validation set. It scores every training record by the combined activation of that causal feature set and fine-tunes on the highest-ranked subset.

The relevant object is not a newly released dataset but an existing instruction-response record plus its feature-resonant score. The feedback contract is task performance under intervention during feature validation, followed by ordinary SFT; this differs from selectors that trust an external judge, a low loss, or semantic coverage without asking whether the chosen examples reinforce a causal task mechanism.
