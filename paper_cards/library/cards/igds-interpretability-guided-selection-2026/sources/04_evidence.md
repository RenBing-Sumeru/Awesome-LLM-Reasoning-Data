The decisive math comparison fine-tunes Gemma-2-2B on selected OpenR1-Math records and evaluates MATH-500. With a 50% sampling rate, IGDS reaches 37.8 accuracy, whereas removing frequency recall gives 29.2 and removing causal filtering gives 33.0; the paper also reports that IGDS exceeds full-data fine-tuning by 17.4% on this setting. These comparisons isolate both stages of the proposed selector rather than merely comparing a small subset with a full corpus.

The paper further evaluates Gemma-2, LLaMA-3.1, and Qwen3 across math, DialogSum summarization, and English-to-Chinese translation, against random, loss, IFD, and ZIP selection. This supports cross-task data efficiency, but not a guarantee that an SAE feature discovered for one model, dataset, or layer transfers to a different architecture or a changing production corpus.
