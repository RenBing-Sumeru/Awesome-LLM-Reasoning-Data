The distinctive contribution is not simply using an LLM or CLIP as a scorer. DOSE treats their outputs as complementary axes in a record-selection distribution: text can be well formed but mismatch an image, and an aligned pair can still provide poor instruction content. It therefore makes the training-record decision depend on both qualities without training a new selector on the collection to be filtered.

Its second contribution is to replace deterministic ranking with a density-ratio sampling target. This explicitly preserves coverage in score space, which addresses the common failure of top-score curation to overrepresent similar records. The proposal is especially relevant when a training pipeline must lower selection cost and reduce target-corpus exposure, but still wants the subset construction to encode a quality-versus-diversity decision.
