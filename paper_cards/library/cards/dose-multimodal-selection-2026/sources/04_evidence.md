On nine general visual question answering benchmarks, a LLaVA-1.5-7B model trained on the 20% DOSE subset reaches 96.0% relative performance against full-data training. This is the best reported average among the compared selectors that do not first train on the entire target collection; it exceeds D2-Pruning at 94.8% and Self-Filter at 93.2%, while remaining below full-data-aware ICONS at 98.6%.

The authors also apply the procedure to MathV360K and continuously fine-tune LLaVA-1.5-13B. At a 20% subset, their reported average is 39.1 versus 36.9 for random selection at the same fraction and 39.4 for the reproduced full-data reference. The paper additionally finds that DOSE reaches 97.4% relative performance in 12 hours and 98.5% in 22 hours, but reports cases where random sampling is stronger on individual low-ratio math tasks.
