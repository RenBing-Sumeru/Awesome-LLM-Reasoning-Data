DOSE separates two judgments that are often conflated in multimodal filtering. A prompted language model estimates whether the instruction-and-answer text is coherent, informative, safe, and useful for instruction tuning; CLIP similarity estimates whether the image and text belong together. This makes a training record a two-dimensional object rather than a single scalar quality score.

The central choice is not to take a hard top-k set. DOSE shifts probability toward favorable score regions while preserving nonzero support across the observed score distribution, and intersects the samples preferred by the two dimensions. The resulting data consumer sees records selected for both quality and alignment, while the selection rule deliberately resists a narrow concentration on common high-scoring patterns.
