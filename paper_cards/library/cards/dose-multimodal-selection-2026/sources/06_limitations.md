The method inherits the blind spots of its frozen scorers: textual suitability is judged without the image, CLIP similarity is an imperfect proxy for task usefulness, and neither score establishes factual correctness. The paper itself observes that score-selected data can become semantically clustered and lose to random sampling on some small-ratio tasks. The main evidence uses LLaVA-1.5 and particular public collections, so it does not establish that the rule transfers unchanged to other architectures, languages, or proprietary corpora. It also declares no official implementation or released selected subset, and upstream data terms must be checked before reuse.
