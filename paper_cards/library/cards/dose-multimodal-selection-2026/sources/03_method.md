For every record, Vicuna-7B receives a yes-or-no prompt about whether the textual content is informative, well formatted, usable for visual instruction tuning, and free of harmful content; the probability of the yes token becomes the text-quality score. CLIP-ViT-B/32 embeds the image and text in a shared space, and their cosine similarity becomes the image-text relevance score. Neither scorer is fine-tuned on the target collection.

For each score dimension, the authors fit a kernel-density estimate, form a Gaussian target whose center lies between the dominant mode and the maximum score, and sample with weights given by the target-to-reference density ratio. They take the intersection of the two weighted random samples. In the main setup, this procedure selects 665K records, 20% of the LLaVA-1.5 visual instruction-tuning collection, before standard second-stage tuning of LLaVA-1.5.
