For continued pre-training of Llama-3.2-1B, FOT obtains a 22.39 average over general and code benchmarks, compared with 20.96 under random order, a gain of 1.43 points. In supervised fine-tuning, the reported average gains over random order are 1.74 points for Llama-3.2-1B, 1.69 for Llama-3.1-8B, 4.34 for Gemma-3-4B, and 1.60 for Qwen-3-4B across math and code benchmarks. The paper summarizes an average improvement of 2.4 percent across its experiments.

The ablations also support the two-phase decision. For Llama-3.1-8B, using only the curriculum phase lowers the average from 49.01 to 40.99, whereas curriculum followed by random order reaches 50.70. Reverse curricula and mixed easy-hard batches fail to match the result consistently. These comparisons indicate that the measured effect is not simply a benefit of sorting once: it depends on the model-relative difficulty definition and on returning to unconstrained random exposure after the initial transition.
