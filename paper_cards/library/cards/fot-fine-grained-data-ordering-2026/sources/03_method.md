For each example, FOT computes cross-entropy under the pre-trained model and an estimated pre-training-distribution component. Because the original pre-training corpus is unavailable, the latter is approximated with RedPajama token frequencies following a prior detection method. A weighted average of the two quantities gives the difficulty score. All data are ranked from simple to difficult; no record is filtered out, relabeled, or regenerated.

Training has two phases. In the first of two reported epochs, the sorted list is divided into blocks and shuffled only within each block, preserving a global easy-to-hard progression while keeping some local stochasticity. In the second epoch, the whole dataset is randomly shuffled. The paper applies this same schedule to a two-billion-token code/general continued-pretraining mixture and to MathInstruct and Magicoder supervised fine-tuning, then compares it with random, reverse, mixed, and alternative curriculum orders.
