The contribution is to make order a first-class property of a training record. Filtering asks whether an example may enter the corpus; mixing asks how many examples of each source are present. FOT instead attaches a difficulty rank to every retained example and specifies when it is consumed. The method therefore changes the optimization trajectory without reducing the data budget or introducing a new synthetic supervision source.

Its second distinctive choice is the hybrid schedule. A pure easy-to-hard curriculum is not presented as universally better; the authors show that retaining it alone can sharply harm an 8B model. The proposed first-epoch curriculum and later random phase make the training consumer adaptive in time while preserving the diversity of updates. This offers a specific, testable alternative to the assumption that random shuffling is a neutral default for all fine-tuning data.
