At each step, the method computes the update requested by scarce target data and builds a feasible set from gradients on general data. It projects the target update onto that set, then applies the projected direction. The paper derives subset, threshold, and greedy constructions as tractable ways to instantiate the feasible set and also gives group-wise and layer-wise variants.

The authors evaluate the scaffold in SFT, RLHF, and RLVR, keeping held-out evaluation data disjoint from regularization and training data and averaging results over five seeds unless otherwise stated. Practical reproduction must decide whether a rule decomposes across micro-batches: thresholding can be resolved locally, while top-k requires global statistics and may need two passes or more memory.
