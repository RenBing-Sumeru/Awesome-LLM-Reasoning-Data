The method requires gradient-level access to both target and general streams and can impose substantial memory or extra-pass cost, especially for rules requiring batch-global top-k statistics. Its guarantee is about the geometry induced by the available general data, not the truth or safety of that data; a biased or poorly matched corpus can constrain learning in the wrong direction. The reported code is public, but no dedicated new dataset is released, so exact data mixtures and their licenses must be reconstructed from the paper and upstream sources.
