The paper evaluates the framework across SFT, RLHF, and RLVR and reports consistent downstream-performance and convergence improvements against data-selection baselines. In SFT, it fine-tunes Llama-3.2-1B on four training/target pairs and reports that its layer-wise subset update outperforms the stated online data-selection baseline; the reported tests are held out from both training and regularization.

This is stronger evidence than a single SFT result because the same update principle is exercised under three post-training objectives. It is not proof that every general dataset is a helpful regularizer: the effect depends on the target/general pairing, gradient approximation, feasible-set rule, and system memory budget, all of which need to be matched in a reuse study.
