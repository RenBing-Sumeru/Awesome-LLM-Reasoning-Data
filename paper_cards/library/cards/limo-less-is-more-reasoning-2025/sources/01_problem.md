Reasoning fine-tuning is often assumed to require a massive set of demonstrations, even though larger collections can add repetition, noise, and teacher cost. LIMO asks whether a pretrained model can instead be activated by a very small set of difficult, diverse, high-quality mathematical examples.

Its proposed answer is to use selected solutions as cognitive templates. The work studies whether carefully chosen supervision can elicit reasoning behavior rather than attempting to reteach the model all mathematical knowledge through sheer data volume.
