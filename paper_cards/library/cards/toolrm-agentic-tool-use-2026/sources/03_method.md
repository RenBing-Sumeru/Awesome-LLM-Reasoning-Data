The authors collect and normalize trajectories from seven tool-use datasets, segment contexts and responses, validate calls against schemas and task rules, and sample alternative model outputs. Rule-based comparison produces chosen and rejected trajectories with controlled complexity and interaction intensity.

They train both generative and discriminative reward models on these records. The generative model can explain a judgement, while the discriminative model supplies a direct preference score; the paper then studies their use as reward signals for downstream agent training.
