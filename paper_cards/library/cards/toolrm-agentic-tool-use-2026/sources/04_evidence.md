The paper reports up to 17.94 percentage points of pairwise reward-judgement accuracy improvement over the stated frontier language-model and reward-model comparisons. It also evaluates the reward models in downstream tool-use optimization, linking preference quality to an agent-training consumer.

This evidence supports the tool-specific construction and the reported task mixture. It does not establish that a rule checker covers all semantically valid tool strategies, or that a trajectory judged preferable in one tool interface transfers to a different API, permission model, or environment.
