Instruction corpora are redundant, and difficulty or a single quality score does not directly identify examples that improve instruction following. wICI ranks instruction data by how much a candidate demonstration reduces difficulty for semantically related peers. In a real post-training pipeline, this uncertainty is costly because every retained example consumes tokens, optimization steps, and evaluation capacity. A useful intervention must therefore specify not only a desirable outcome, but also which record-level decision changes before training begins.

The paper asks which training records or reward signals should be consumed under a fixed post-training budget. The paper treats this as a decision about the training surface: the model sees a different subset, environment, or reward-bearing record, and its reported outcome should be compared with a matched alternative rather than with an unrelated model.
