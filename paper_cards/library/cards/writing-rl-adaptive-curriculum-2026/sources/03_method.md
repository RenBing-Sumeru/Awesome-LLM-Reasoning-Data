For every instruction from LongWriter or WritingBench, the initial policy, Qwen-Plus, GPT-4o, Claude-3.7, and DeepSeek-R1 produce candidate writings. A fine-tuned long-form judge gives pointwise scores. The method removes an instruction if all candidates score poorly, computes the best-candidate-minus-policy margin for the remainder, and selects the top 1,500 instructions from each source. Candidate responses are ordered by score to form a reference ladder for each retained instruction.

During PPO or GRPO training, Qwen-Plus compares a newly sampled policy response with the reference at that instruction's current pointer and returns one for a win, one half for a tie, and zero for a loss. The policy response is placed second in the comparison to make winning harder and to avoid doubled calls from swapping order. A win advances only that instruction's pointer to its next stronger reference, so difficulty is updated asynchronously rather than globally by epoch.
