The distinctive contribution is not merely using an LLM judge for open-ended writing. The paper defines data value relative to the learner by comparing the learner's initial writing with a pool of stronger candidate writings, then uses that margin to decide which prompts enter RL. This makes selection target a demonstrated opportunity for improvement rather than broad topical coverage, output length, or an absolute quality threshold alone.

It also makes the comparison target stateful at the individual-prompt level. Reference answers are not a fixed collection of preferred completions: they become a ladder that a policy climbs after observable wins. Combining margin selection, a deliberately asymmetric pairwise comparison, and asynchronous reference promotion gives a concrete way to turn heterogeneous, scored writing candidates into a curriculum without claiming that a single answer is objectively correct.
