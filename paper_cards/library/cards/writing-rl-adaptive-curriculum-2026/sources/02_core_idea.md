Writing-RL treats a useful writing example as a moving relation between a policy and a ladder of candidate references, rather than as a permanently labeled prompt. For each instruction, the relevant quantity is learning potential: the score of the best available candidate minus the initial policy score. A large positive margin means that the prompt has a credible improvement target; prompts where every candidate is poor are not useful targets.

The framework then turns that relation into an adaptive curriculum. Pairwise comparison against a current reference supplies a simple win, tie, or loss reward, while a prompt-specific pointer begins at the weakest available reference and advances after a win. The policy therefore practices against an attainable target before it is asked to match a stronger one, instead of optimizing against a single static teacher throughout training.
