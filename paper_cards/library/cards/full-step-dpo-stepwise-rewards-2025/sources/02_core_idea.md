Full-Step-DPO treats a reasoning solution as a sequence of supervised decisions with unequal value. The process reward model assigns a score to each step, so a chain is not reduced to a binary correct/incorrect response or to one first mistake. Higher-quality steps can reinforce the policy while lower-quality steps receive a different gradient contribution, preserving information that ordinary DPO and first-error-only construction discard.

The key is that the step scorer is self-supervised. Instead of requesting a human or a frontier model to annotate each transition, the method derives process-reward training data from generated reasoning and verifiable terminal answers. It then uses the scorer both to select high- and low-quality solutions and to weight all stepwise terms in the preference loss. Selection and consumption are consequently coupled through one learned process signal.
