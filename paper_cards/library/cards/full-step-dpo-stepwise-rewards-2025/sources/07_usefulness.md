Use this method when reasoning traces have verifiable end states but manual or frontier-model step labels are too costly. It provides a route from terminal correctness to process-scored pairs and a mechanism for using every step rather than throwing away information after the first error. Before adoption, validate PRM calibration on held-out traces, compare full-step weighting with first-error and outcome-only controls, measure the cost of sampling and scoring, and inspect whether selected low-scoring solutions contain useful errors rather than malformed text.
