The main novelty is to make the full process-reward trajectory part of a DPO-style training record. Earlier solution-level records retain only an outcome, and first-error records retain only one transition. Full-Step-DPO records a reward sequence and uses it to decide both which solutions form a pair and how each step inside that pair affects the gradient. This is a genuine change in supervision granularity and in the training consumer.

The self-supervised PRM is the other central choice. It is not merely an auxiliary evaluator: it replaces expensive external step annotation in constructing the data and supplies the weights that remain active in optimization. The method therefore ties data generation, filtering, and loss shaping to one source of evidence. Its value is strongest where final-answer verification can provide an initial trustworthy signal for process-reward learning.
