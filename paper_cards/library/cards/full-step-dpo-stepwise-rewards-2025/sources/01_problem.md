Solution-level DPO treats a long reasoning chain as one indivisible preference record. Step-DPO improves granularity by targeting the first erroneous step, but leaves later correct or incorrect steps unused and often needs humans or GPT-4 to identify that first error. This wastes process information and makes fine-grained data construction costly when many reasoning paths must be annotated.

The paper asks how to turn every useful step in a reasoning chain into preference supervision without an external step annotator. It trains a self-supervised process reward model from sampled solutions and final-answer signals, uses that model to score the full chain, and gives all steps a reward-sensitive contribution to optimization. The objective is therefore not merely to identify an error location, but to retain graded evidence from the entire solution trajectory.
