The paper reports that Full-Step-DPO exceeds solution-level DPO, Step-DPO, and other baselines on its in-domain mathematical tasks across several base models. It also evaluates out of domain on GK2023 and OCW and reports stronger performance in nearly all listed settings, with further results on five additional mathematical datasets. The released PRM achieves 84.3 percent accuracy on GSM8K and 33.2 percent on MATH in the reported process-scoring evaluation, showing that self-supervision yields a usable, though imperfect, step signal.

The analyses connect results to full-chain consumption. Compared with optimizing only the first erroneous step, weighting every step yields better preference-learning performance; the authors also show that the PRM can score and filter solutions for best-of-eight decoding. They report that self-generated process data lowers the construction cost relative to external step annotation. At the same time, analyses show PRM benefit diminishes for very strong base models and that later inference stages remain hard for the scorer, delimiting the evidence.
