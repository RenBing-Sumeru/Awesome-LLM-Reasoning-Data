The method first samples reasoning solutions for mathematical questions and constructs process-reward examples from their intermediate steps and terminal correctness. A self-supervised process reward model is trained to score each step, avoiding external step-level labels. It scores candidate chains, aggregates their process quality, and selects high-scoring and low-scoring solutions to form preference pairs. The PRM therefore supplies both a record-construction signal and a step-level value sequence.

Full-Step-DPO then replaces the whole-solution log-ratio with a sum whose step terms are dynamically weighted by those rewards. All steps in the chosen and rejected chains participate, unlike Step-DPO's first-error focus. The authors use the same Step-DPO training prompts for comparison, train with self-generated PRM data, and evaluate in-domain as well as MetaMath, MMIQC, GK2023, OCW, and other out-of-domain mathematical settings.
