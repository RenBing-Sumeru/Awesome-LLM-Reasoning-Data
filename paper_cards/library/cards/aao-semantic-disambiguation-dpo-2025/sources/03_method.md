For a chosen-rejected pair, AAO obtains token embeddings from the model and computes each token's maximum normalized similarity to the tokens of the other answer. An MLP predicts ordered thresholds a and b from output logits. Similarity above a yields an ambiguity weight of (1-S)^2, similarity below b yields a key-token weight of 1+S, and the middle region has weight one; a differentiable approximation permits threshold learning. These weights are applied to output logits in the preference optimization.

The authors run one-epoch AdamW training with batch size 16 and learning rate 5e-7 on Llama-3.1-8B and Mistral-7B, each in base and instruction-tuned form. Open-domain runs use UltraFeedback after base models are prepared with UltraChat-200k; safety runs use Anthropic-HH after Alpaca preparation. Comparisons include DPO, IPO, KTO, SimPO, TDPO, RTO, TIS-DPO, and a randomly weighted DPO control.
