The authors state that the ambiguity argument still needs a more rigorous analysis of gradient dynamics, and their piecewise similarity curve is empirically motivated rather than proven optimal. Semantic similarity is generated from the training model's own embeddings, so the grouping can inherit representation bias or fail when the same word has different task-relevant meanings. The helpfulness evidence relies substantially on automated LLM judges, and the paper reports no broad contamination or label-quality audit of the source preference data.
