AAO represents every response token with the language model's embedding and compares it with all tokens in the opposite response. The normalized maximum cosine similarity marks how likely that token is to be ambiguous across the preference pair. Highly similar tokens are treated as background, low-similarity tokens as preference-bearing keys, and the middle region as transitional context.

The method suppresses ambiguous-token weights with a squared complement of similarity, preserves transitional tokens at one, and increases the weight of key tokens. A lightweight adaptive module predicts the two grouping thresholds from model outputs, while auxiliary contrastive and chosen-reward losses train that module. The result is a DPO-compatible token-weighted record rather than a new preference label.
