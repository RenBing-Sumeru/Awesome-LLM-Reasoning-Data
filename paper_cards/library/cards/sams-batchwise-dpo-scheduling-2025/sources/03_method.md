At round t, SamS encodes each prompt, chosen response, and rejected response in the incoming batch as an arm context. A scheduler scores the arms and selects K of N pairs; the policy is then updated with ordinary DPO on only those pairs. The training feedback supplies a batch-level reward from the change in normalized DPO loss and a sample-level reward from preference margin and model uncertainty.

The scheduler is trained with a lagged update in the next round, allowing the method to collect the relevant loss feedback during normal policy training instead of running a separate evaluation loop. The scheduler contains exploitation and exploration components, and the authors report experiments with 64-pair batches and 32 scheduled pairs, across instruction-following evaluation and public preference datasets.
