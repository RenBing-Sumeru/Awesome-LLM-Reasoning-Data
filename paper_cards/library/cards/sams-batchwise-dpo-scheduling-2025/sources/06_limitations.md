SamS is sensitive to the quality of its fixed preference pool: the authors note that its relative advantage shrinks when abundant high-quality response pairs are already available, and noisy labels can still distort the scheduler's feedback. Its reward uses loss, margin, and uncertainty as proxies for future generalization, so a scheduler may learn dataset- or model-specific correlations rather than a portable notion of value. The added networks and stateful schedule also introduce tuning choices, while reuse still requires checking the licenses, splits, and possible benchmark overlap of every upstream preference dataset and automatic judge.
