The NeurIPS paper evaluates the scheduler on AlpacaEval 2 and MT-Bench, compares it with offline preference-optimization baselines, and separately tests transfer on Anthropic-HH and SHP. In the main instruction-following experiments, adding SamS to DPO improves AlpacaEval 2 win rate by 3.0 to 12.4 percentage points and length-controlled win rate by 5.5 to 8.4 points relative to the reported baselines.

The authors also test noisy preference labels and report that the scheduled variant is more robust than plain DPO. On Anthropic-HH, their table reports 67.1 test accuracy for DPO plus SamS versus 64.3 for DPO; on the same data, KTO plus SamS reaches 63.3 versus 60.2 for KTO. These results support a scheduling effect, though they are evaluations of the proposed policy-training recipe rather than proof that every chosen pair is intrinsically better.
