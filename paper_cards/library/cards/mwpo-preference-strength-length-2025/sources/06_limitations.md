MWPO assumes that a policy's current implicit reward margin is a useful proxy for preference reliability, yet that margin can itself inherit model bias and length effects. The paper reports that reward and length margins are correlated in its setting, and it fixes the length scale while searching only a small set of mixture coefficients. Its strongest alignment evidence also uses LLM judging or a proxy evaluator, so independent human evaluation and broader data-quality audits remain necessary before treating its weights as ground truth.
