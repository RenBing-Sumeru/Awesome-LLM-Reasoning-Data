Earlier DPO variants either treat offline pairs uniformly, add a length term directly to the reward, or weight pairs for a different purpose such as on-policy resemblance. MWPO makes two properties of the same stored record explicit optimization inputs: the model-relative confidence in its preference direction and the chosen-versus-rejected length margin. It therefore changes the training consumer without changing the label source.

The important design choice is geometric fusion rather than two independent penalties. A high update weight requires both a favorable preference signal and a favorable length signal, and the mixing coefficient exposes their trade-off. This gives the record a reusable audit question: whether its measured reward margin is meaningful rather than merely correlated with response length.
