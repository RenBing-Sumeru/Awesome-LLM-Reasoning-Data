For each instruction and preference pair, the method computes chosen and rejected implicit rewards from policy-to-reference log-probability ratios. Their sigmoid margin yields the reward weight. It computes a second sigmoid from the scaled difference between rejected and chosen token counts; this decreases the weight when the preferred response is longer. A mixing coefficient combines the two signals geometrically, and the resulting value scales the DPO loss without back-propagating through the weight.

The paper trains full parameters of Mistral-7B-Base, Qwen2.5-1.5B-Base, Llama-3.2-3B-Base, and Llama-3-8B-Instruct. Base models are first instruction-tuned with UltraChat-200k and then optimized on UltraFeedback-binarized; instruction-tuned models use PairRM-labeled Princeton Llama3 UltraFeedback pairs. It compares DPO, RRHF, IPO, KTO, CPO, R-DPO, SimPO, and WPO on AlpacaEval 2 and five downstream benchmarks.
