The metric assumes that an external reward model defines a useful target margin, so reward-model bias, calibration errors, and distribution shift can directly alter selection. It also needs policy and reference log probabilities, which adds preprocessing cost and ties results to model checkpoints and tokenization. In the self-generated setting, evolution, generation, annotation, and selection can form a feedback loop; reuse should evaluate whether this loop broadens capabilities or reinforces the reward model's blind spots.
