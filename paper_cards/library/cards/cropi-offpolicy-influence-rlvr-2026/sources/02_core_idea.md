CROPI uses trajectories sampled once from an initial behavior policy to approximate how the current policy would update on every prompt. It then treats the cosine similarity between a prompt's projected off-policy gradient and a validation-set gradient as practical influence. A high score means that optimizing the prompt is estimated to move the policy in a direction that also helps the chosen validation objective.

Selection is deliberately recurrent rather than global. At the start of a phase, the current checkpoint re-scores the entire training pool, ranks prompts for one or more validation sets, fuses those ranks, and retains only the top fraction for GRPO. The next checkpoint changes the influence landscape, so the next phase can choose a different subset without paying for new full-pool rollouts.
