The influence approximation is first-order, assumes the SGD setting in the analysis, and lacks a formal error, bias, or variance bound for off-policy gradients. Trajectories come only from the base policy, so prompts with uniformly correct or incorrect base-policy outcomes can have zero GRPO gradient and disappear from the available signal. Although it avoids fresh full-pool rollouts, it still computes gradients over the whole pool every phase, and its evidence is restricted to single-turn mathematics with models no larger than 7B and fewer than 1,000 training steps.
