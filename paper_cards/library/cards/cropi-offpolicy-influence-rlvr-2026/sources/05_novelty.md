The new decision is to score a training prompt by approximate policy-gradient contribution to an explicit downstream validation objective, rather than by an intrinsic property such as difficulty or pass rate. Offline trajectories make that objective-conditioned score feasible without performing full-pool online rollouts at every selection step. Sparse random projection is not only a storage device here: it is part of the practical ranking mechanism for full-parameter gradients.

The curriculum is also more specific than ordinary easy-to-hard scheduling. It re-evaluates influence after each policy phase and can move the selected set toward prompts that are semantically relevant to the validation objective and near the current learning frontier. This makes the validation set a declared consumer-side preference in the data-selection rule, while preserving a held-out evaluation split for the reported results.
