RLVR needs a policy-dependent way to decide which prompts should consume scarce rollouts and update steps. Difficulty, pass rate, and static quality scores can be useful heuristics, but they do not directly ask whether optimizing one prompt will improve the intended reasoning objective. A direct influence calculation would make that connection, yet it normally requires expensive online rollouts and full high-dimensional gradients for every prompt.

The paper asks whether the contribution of a training prompt can be estimated cheaply enough to drive repeated RLVR data selection. It frames the desired record as one whose policy gradient aligns with gradients for a small selection-validation set. The practical requirement is to refresh that decision as the policy changes, without regenerating a full prompt pool of trajectories at every phase.
