The reading takeaway is to treat selection as an intervention that needs uncertainty-aware evidence. Test on the actual pool scale, repeat random baselines, preserve cheap length and diversity controls, and report variance and significance alongside mean scores. The memorable counterexample is Qwen2-7B on 10K OpenHermes: entropy selection averages 54.02 while five random runs span 57.04–59.73. That gap does not prove entropy is always bad; it proves that a single comparison is too weak to justify a costly curation policy.
