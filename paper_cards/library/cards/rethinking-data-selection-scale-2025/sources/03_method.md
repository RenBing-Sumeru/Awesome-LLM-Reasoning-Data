The study starts with OpenHermes2.5 and an English-filtered WildChat pool, then constructs fixed-size instruction subsets using LESS, IFD, SelectIT, entropy, diversity, ZIP, token length, or random sampling. The methods are compared as selection policies, not as data sources: every policy is asked to spend the same subset budget. LLaMA3-8B and Qwen2-7B are fine-tuned for three epochs, so a reported difference is meant to arise from which records were consumed rather than from a changed optimization schedule.

The resulting models are evaluated on BBH, GSM8K, HumanEval, MMLU, and IFEval. Crucially, the authors repeat random selection five times and use right-tailed Mann–Whitney U tests when comparing a selector with the random distribution; this turns random sampling from a single baseline point into a measured uncertainty band. The repository and an English WildChat subset are released, while external scorer methods are deliberately not included because of the paper's stated cost constraints.
