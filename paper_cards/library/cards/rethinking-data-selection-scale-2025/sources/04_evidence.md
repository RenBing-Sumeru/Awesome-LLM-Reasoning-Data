For the 10K-example OpenHermes setting, none of the tested self-scoring methods obtains a right-tailed p-value below 0.05 when compared with five random runs. One vivid result is Qwen2-7B: entropy selection averages 54.02, whereas the random runs range from 57.04 to 59.73. This is stronger evidence than a single table maximum because it directly shows an allegedly informed selector falling below the variation seen from inexpensive random baselines.

The experimental design supports caution, not a universal theorem that random selection always wins. The pools, subset sizes, backbones, tasks, and self-scoring methods are all finite; an external scorer, a different source mixture, or a larger budget may behave differently. The durable evidence is methodological: a curation claim should report repeated random controls and statistical uncertainty before treating an observed improvement as a property of the selector rather than an accident of one subset.
