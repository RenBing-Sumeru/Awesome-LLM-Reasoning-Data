Translation preference optimization often ranks candidates with a quality-estimation score and trains on the single best-worst pair. This is brittle when a fluent translation omits source content or hallucinates a detail: quality estimators can prefer it, while the standard pair discards intermediate candidates that expose the subtle failure. The resulting supervision is both misranked and wasteful.

M2PO asks how a translation candidate set can be converted into preference supervision that values semantic faithfulness as well as surface quality. It treats partial omissions and hallucinations as a distinct hard region rather than noise to ignore. The paper therefore changes the data record from one reward-ranked pair into a confidence-aware, multi-perspective candidate ranking and multiple weighted contrasts.
