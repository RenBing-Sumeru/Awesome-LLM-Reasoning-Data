The reported M2PO-Prefer construction starts from WMT22, IWSLT, and FLORES-200 development sources in six English–Chinese, English–German, and English–Japanese directions. For each retained source, it generates eight candidates: four from GPT-4o-mini and four from the base translation model. A quality estimator, a semantic-alignment classifier, and policy confidence form the fused reward; after filtering, about 20,000 source inputs and about 160,000 candidates remain.

Candidates are sorted by the fused reward and coupled from opposite ends of the ranking, creating four preference pairs per eight-candidate set. Dynamic multi-pair CPO weights each pair by its normalized reward gap, and a listwise ranking loss regularizes the policy over all candidates. The paper fine-tunes Qwen3-4B-Instruct and GemmaX2-28-9B with LoRA, evaluates separately held-out WMT23, FLORES-200 test, and WMT24 data, and tests whether the construction also helps DPO, KTO, SimPO, and ORPO.
