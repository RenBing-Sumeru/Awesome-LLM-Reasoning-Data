On WMT23, the reported GemmaX2-28-9B M2PO model reaches 97.87 Coverage and 89.67 XCOMET, compared with 96.09 and 87.16 for the unadapted base model and 97.13 and 88.59 for the CPO baseline. The paper also reports a blinded expert study on 450 WMT23 samples: M2PO receives a 4.37 mean quality score and 89.11% full-match faithfulness, versus 3.53 and 72.67% for the base model and 4.33 and 87.11% for GPT-4o-mini.

The ablations tie the result to the data and objective choices rather than only to a stronger generator. Removing the multi-pair component lowers the reported WMT23 result to 97.42 Coverage and 89.16 XCOMET; removing the faithfulness filter gives 96.99 and 89.28. In a fully open-source pipeline, M2PO still exceeds CPO by 0.63 Coverage and 0.74 XCOMET. These results support the claimed translation setting, but quality estimators and semantic labels remain components whose reliability must be checked outside the reported benchmarks.
