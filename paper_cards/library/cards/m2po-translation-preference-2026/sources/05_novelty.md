Closest preference pipelines use a continuous quality proxy to choose a best-worst pair, or use a set-level objective without an explicit treatment of partial semantic errors. M2PO changes both stages. In construction, a discrete faithfulness signal corrects quality-score rankings and is progressively blended with policy confidence; in consumption, hierarchical pairs and a listwise term retain the information that a single pair would discard.

The new contribution is not the existence of quality estimation, preference losses, or candidate generation, all of which predate the work. Its distinctive decision is to make partial-error sensitivity and candidate-set ordering first-class parts of the training record and objective. A reuse should test whether its semantic-alignment classifier detects the target domain's failures, whether the confidence curriculum remains calibrated for another model, and whether API-generated candidates can be replaced under the intended cost and licensing constraints.
