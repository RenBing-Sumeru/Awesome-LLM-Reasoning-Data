The method separates two questions that a single quality score confounds: whether a translation is faithful to the source and how good it is once faithfulness is secured. It augments a continuous quality proxy with a discrete semantic-alignment penalty for omissions and hallucinations, then blends this external signal with the current model's confidence through a curriculum. Early training is more externally grounded; later training gives the policy's confidence more influence without removing the faithfulness guardrail.

Instead of reducing eight candidates to one extreme pair, M2PO ranks the complete set by this fused signal and pairs high- and low-ranked candidates hierarchically. Large score gaps receive larger local preference weight, while a listwise loss asks the policy to preserve the whole ordering. The candidate set thus supplies both easy contrasts and difficult partial-error contrasts, rather than treating the latter as disposable middle cases.
