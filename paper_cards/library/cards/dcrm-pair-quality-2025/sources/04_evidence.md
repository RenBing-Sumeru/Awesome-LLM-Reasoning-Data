Across the constructed datasets, the paper reports a moderate positive correlation between dataset-level DCRM and the AlpacaEval length-controlled improvement after training: Pearson correlation is 0.59. This is stronger than the reported correlations for token edit distance, reference-policy probability difference, or reward margin considered alone. The result supports the paper's narrower claim that the joint measure tracks downstream behavior better than its individual components in these settings.

For the operational test, Best-of-N-squared re-selection improves the reported results over the corresponding original datasets in the compared LLaMA-2 and LLaMA-3.2 settings. For example, LLaMA-3.2 trained on reselected same-source reference-policy pairs reaches 24.77 length-controlled AlpacaEval, 27.64 raw AlpacaEval, 5.10 MT-Bench, and 20.25 Arena-Hard, compared with 22.80, 25.65, 5.01, and 18.88 before re-selection. These results support pair re-selection under the paper's reward-model and benchmark setup, not a guarantee that DCRM transfers to every preference source or judge.
