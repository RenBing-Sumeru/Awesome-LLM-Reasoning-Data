For a prompt and a chosen-rejected pair, the method computes three quantities. The reward margin is the reward model's score difference; token-level Levenshtein distance measures textual change; and the absolute difference in reference-policy log probabilities measures how differently the policy already views the two answers. DCRM applies a sigmoid to the reward margin, centers it, and divides it by the sum of the two distance terms plus a stabilizing constant.

The experiments hold DPO fixed while varying how preference records are formed. They compare pairs sampled from the same source and reward-labeled, pairs from different sources and reward-labeled, and pairs whose preference is fixed by source-level ranking. For re-selection, the method searches candidate pairs in an existing response pool, uses the largest DCRM score, trains LLaMA-2-7B-Chat, LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct, and Gemma-2B-IT, and evaluates conversational behavior with AlpacaEval, MT-Bench, and Arena-Hard.
