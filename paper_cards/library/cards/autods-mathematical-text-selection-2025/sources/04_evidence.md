The clearest comparison holds the continual-pretraining recipe fixed and changes the way the mathematical corpus is selected. For Mistral-7B, AutoDS reaches 16.14 accuracy on MATH, compared with 14.26 for uniform sampling, 12.30 for DSIR, and 12.90 for QuRating. These are useful numbers because the baselines are also curation rules rather than unrelated model scales, and they show a gain under the paper's stated token and training settings.

The paper also evaluates several model sizes and uses ablations to connect the result to the proposed score. Still, the evidence establishes relative utility on the evaluated mathematical mixtures, not that an identical threshold will transfer to law, code, or multilingual data. Nor does an improvement on MATH prove that the score measures mathematical truth: it can also capture the preferences and blind spots of Qwen-72B, the model that supplied the selection signal.
