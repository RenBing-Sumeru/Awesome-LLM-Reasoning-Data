The authors draw candidate documents from OpenWebMath, RedPajama arXiv, and Algebraic Stack, so the pool includes web mathematical material, scientific writing, and question-answer content. They prompt Qwen-72B with two binary questions aimed at whether a passage contains useful mathematical knowledge and whether it has educational value. Rather than sampling a verbal judgment, they read the relevant target-token logits and combine them into the LM-Score used for ranking.

After scoring more than 11.26 million documents, the authors choose score ranges to create an approximately 200 GB AutoMathText release. They continually pretrain Gemma-2B, LLaMA2-7B, and Mistral-7B on selected mixtures and compare them with uniform sampling and existing selectors under matched schedules. The official repository and dataset expose the scoring and corpus artifacts; however, the paper does not provide a complete document-level overlap audit against every downstream benchmark, so reuse still needs a contamination check.
