AutoDS makes the selection signal an explicit companion to the text. Each candidate document is associated with an `lm_q1q2_score`, derived from the difference between the model's logits for the positive and negative answers to two natural-language questions about mathematical informativeness and educational value. A downstream curator can rank documents, retain a score band, or change the threshold, rather than treating every document that survived an upstream crawl as equally useful.

This is deliberately a model-relative usefulness signal, not a theorem prover and not a correctness label for every statement in a document. Its central claim is more modest: the score changes the composition of the corpus consumed by continual pretraining, and that changed composition can be evaluated under a matched training budget. The released scored corpus makes both the decision rule and the selected data inspectable rather than leaving quality filtering as an undocumented preprocessing step.
