Across the reported tasks, RLAIF reaches performance comparable to RLHF. The direct-RLAIF variant is reported to outperform canonical RLAIF, and the self-improvement experiment outperforms a supervised fine-tuned baseline under the tested conditions.

These results show that AI feedback can be a viable training signal in the studied settings. They do not establish that an AI judge matches people on every value-laden judgment, or that direct use of a changing external model remains reproducible without logging its version and prompt.
