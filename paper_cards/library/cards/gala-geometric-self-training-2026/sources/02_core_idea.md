GALA first maps candidate samples into a latent embedding space, clusters them, and chooses an anchor from each cluster by geometric distance. The selected anchors stand in for redundant neighborhoods rather than letting frequent patterns dominate the synthetic-data budget.

For each selected problem, strategic brainstorming and reflection prospect more varied reasoning trajectories. A lightweight dynamic validator then accepts only reliable mini-batches, turning data construction into a stream that can adapt during self-training.
