Test-time scaling is often associated with complex search or large-scale RL, but public methods rarely separate the contribution of training data from the contribution of extra generation budget. s1 asks whether a small, openly specified supervised set can yield a model whose reasoning improves predictably when its thinking budget is controlled.

The work therefore combines a small data intervention with an inference-time one. It does not claim that either component alone explains performance: the question is how curated traces and budget control interact.
