Use ASLEC when selecting multi-step reasoning traces by model likelihood and when the selection pipeline can expose step boundaries. Store the segmentation rule, target scoring model revision, source model, generation temperature, correctness verifier, selection ratio, and fitted CASL coefficients with the selected dataset. Compare selected and unselected step-length distributions in addition to downstream accuracy; a score that raises accuracy while still collapsing onto one source or one formatting style may remain biased. CASL is the safer default when first-step transitions may contain useful reasoning information, while DROP is a low-overhead diagnostic control.
