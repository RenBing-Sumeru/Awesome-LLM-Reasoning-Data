Naturalness-based reasoning-data selection ranks a generated chain of thought by average log probability, perplexity, local log probability, or entropy under the target model. These scores are intended to retain trajectories that the target can learn from. In long reasoning traces, however, each new step begins with a low-probability branch token; when steps are longer, that first-token penalty is diluted by more continuation tokens. A selector can therefore prefer verbose step formatting rather than better reasoning.

This paper asks whether naturalness selection is confounded by the number of tokens per reasoning step, and how to correct the score before it decides which generated trajectories are used for SFT. The record is a question, a verified long chain of thought, final answer, token probabilities, and a step segmentation. The desired change is to the pre-SFT ranking rule, not to the answer verifier, the model architecture, or the inference-time decoding policy.
