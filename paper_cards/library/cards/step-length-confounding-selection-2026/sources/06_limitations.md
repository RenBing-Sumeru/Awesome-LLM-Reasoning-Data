The analysis identifies first-token probability as a major contributor to step length confounding, but the paper explicitly leaves open whether deeper confounders remain. Its conclusions concern offline, teacher-generated and Math-Verify-filtered SFT data; on-policy generation and selection may have a different relationship to response length. Both the DROP rule and CASL regression depend on reliable step segmentation and a representative target-model probability calculation. Although the official release includes code and generated datasets, reuse should verify upstream LIMO-v2, AceReason, source-model, and benchmark licenses as well as whether manual supplementation of hard LIMO problems changes the selected-data distribution.
