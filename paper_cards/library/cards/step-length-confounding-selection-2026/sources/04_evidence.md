The paper first shows that four existing naturalness selectors pick a distribution with longer reasoning steps than the unselected pool. On LIMO-v2 for Qwen3-4B-Base, Local LP reaches 36.50 average accuracy, ASLEC-DROP reaches 44.64, and ASLEC-CASL reaches 47.54. For Qwen3-8B-Base the corresponding values are 44.06, 52.92, and 56.15. The selected-versus-unselected distributions become much closer in step length under both variants, which is evidence for reducing the stated confounder rather than only improving a benchmark score.

Across the two data sources and four target models, the paper summarizes average accuracy gains over Local LP of about 6.28% for DROP and 9.08% for CASL. On the separate GPQA evaluation, CASL improves Qwen3-4B-Base accuracy from 29.16 for Local LP to 35.35 and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct from 26.13 to 38.51. CASL also consistently exceeds DROP, suggesting that first tokens contain some useful information that should be debiased rather than removed entirely. These results rely on the reported segmentation, probability model, verified-answer collection, and math/science benchmarks; they do not establish that every length correlation is caused only by first tokens.
