For every candidate response, the pipeline segments the chain of thought into steps and computes model probabilities under the target model. ASLEC-DROP scores the response after excluding the probability of the first token in each step. ASLEC-CASL fits a regression using first-token and non-first-token probabilities plus the first-token ratio, then forms a deconfounded score by removing the ratio's fitted effect. Candidates with the highest corrected scores are selected for SFT; the regression has few parameters and the paper reports only seconds of additional fitting cost.

The LIMO-v2 experiment starts from 800 math problems, generates five correct responses per source model from QwQ-32B, Qwen3-32B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B, and gpt-oss-120b, then chooses five of twenty responses per question. AceReason-1.1-SFT uses 10K problems with one correct response per source model and selects 10K of 40K candidates. Correctness is checked during collection with Math-Verify; the study trains four target models and evaluates AIME 2024, AIME 2025, MATH-500, OlympiadBench, and GPQA.
