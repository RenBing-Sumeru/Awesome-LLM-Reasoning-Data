At every training step, TPO adds scheduled noise to the image embedding, creating a corrupted image. For each response token, it subtracts the corrupted-image raw logit from the original-image raw logit. The method applies a sigmoid and a margin of 0.5 to turn the difference into a calibrated reward. For chosen responses, the reward grows with visual sensitivity; for rejected responses, it grows as visual sensitivity decreases. If the score is zero, the calibrated factor is one and has no effect.

The factor multiplies each token probability inside a KL-constrained preference objective. Both policy and reference rewards are calculated, and the policy-side value is recomputed as training changes. The authors use RLHF-V's 5K preference pairs directly, without its fine-grained human annotations, on LLaVA-1.5 and Qwen2-VL backbones. They test reward assignment on one or both preference sides, noise-step schedules, and the margin parameter; the reported best image corruption is an intermediate 500 steps.
