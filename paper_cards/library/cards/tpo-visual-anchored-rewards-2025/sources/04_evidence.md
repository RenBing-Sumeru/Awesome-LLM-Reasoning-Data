On LLaVA-1.5-7B, TPO raises AMBER F1 from 74.3 to 85.0, MMHal score from 2.01 to 2.47, and HallusionBench average accuracy from 47.21 to 50.22 while keeping general benchmarks close to or above the base model. On LLaVA-1.5-13B, it reaches 88.0 AMBER F1, 2.72 MMHal score, and 50.93 HallusionBench average accuracy. On Qwen2-VL-7B, TPO improves MMHal score from 3.5 to 4.2 and lowers the reported hallucination rate from 29.0 to 18.8, while ordinary DPO changes those metrics only slightly.

The ablations connect outcomes to the reward design. Rewarding only chosen or only rejected tokens improves several hallucination measures, but giving the opposite visual correlation can fall below the untrained backbone. The best performance occurs at 500 noise steps, not at the smallest or largest corruption. Analysis finds noun and adjective tokens have higher visual-anchor scores than other tokens, and image-attention weight on MMHal responses rises from 0.14 before training to 0.17 afterward. These results support the claim that the signal changes visual grounding rather than merely adding a generic preference loss.
