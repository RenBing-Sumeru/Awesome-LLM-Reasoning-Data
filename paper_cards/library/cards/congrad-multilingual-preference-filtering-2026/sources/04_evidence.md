On the multilingual Aya instruction-following evaluation, the best Llama result from consensus-gradient selection reaches a 73.22% average win rate after five rounds, compared with 66.52% for multilingual self-rewarding on all data; for Gemma, the corresponding values are 69.22% and 59.53%. The paper also compares random filtering, minimum and maximum response-length margins, minimum and maximum reward margins, and two language-level schedules. Maximum reward margin is competitive, but the consensus-gradient choice is best in the reported main table.

The analysis extends beyond the ten languages used during training. On Hindi, Vietnamese, and Russian, the selected-data procedure remains stronger than full-data training in the reported rounds. It also improves four languages with limited Llama tokenizer support relative to the full-data and reward-filtering variants. Global MMLU and multilingual ARC Challenge do not show a material alignment tax, while the runtime table reports about 1.1 times the five-epoch cost of naive self-rewarding for Gemma 2-2B.
