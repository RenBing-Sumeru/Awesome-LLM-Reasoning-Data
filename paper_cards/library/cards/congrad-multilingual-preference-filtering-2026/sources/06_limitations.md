The authors note that low-rank compression introduces approximation noise into filtering, that the experiments use typologically varied but relatively balanced languages, and that severely imbalanced or very low-resource cases remain untested. The rule can also reject culturally specific or linguistically untranslatable examples even when they are locally valid, because it prioritizes transferability to a shared direction. Filtering is static at the round level rather than dynamically updated within training, and the data are translated from English prompts, so the results do not establish that the same behavior holds for naturally sourced multilingual instructions.
