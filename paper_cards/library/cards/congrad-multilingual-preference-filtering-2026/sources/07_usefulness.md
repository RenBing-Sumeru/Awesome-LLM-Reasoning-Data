This paper is most useful when a team already has multilingual prompts and a way to generate or label preference pairs, but observes uneven progress across languages. It supplies a concrete audit quantity—each record's agreement with a cross-language update—and a conservative selection point before DPO. It is especially relevant for self-rewarding loops, where poor records can recur across rounds, but the same principle can guide offline preference-data triage if per-record gradients are affordable to compute.
