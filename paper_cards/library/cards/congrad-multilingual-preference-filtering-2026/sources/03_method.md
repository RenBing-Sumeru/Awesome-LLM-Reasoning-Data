In each round, the current instruction model generates four answers for every prompt and self-evaluates them to form chosen-rejected pairs. The first round trains on all such pairs. From the second round onward, the algorithm maintains one compressed gradient EMA per language, projects away negative pairwise dot-product components across languages, and sums the de-conflicted vectors into a consensus direction.

It then computes each candidate pair's instantaneous length-penalized DPO gradient and ranks pairs within their language by cosine similarity to that direction. The main experiments retain the top half of every language's records, train for one DPO epoch, and repeat for five rounds. They start from Llama 3-8B-Instruct or Gemma 2-2B-Instruct, use one thousand AlpaGasus prompts partitioned into ten languages, and compare against full-data, random, length-margin, reward-margin, and language-scheduling baselines.
