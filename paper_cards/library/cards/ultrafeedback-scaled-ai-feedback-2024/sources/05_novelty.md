Earlier alignment datasets commonly supplied a small number of human comparisons or a single scalar preference. UltraFeedback changes the unit of collection by combining broad instructions, many model-generated alternatives, critiques, and multi-aspect scores in one feedback record.

Its contribution is therefore chiefly a data and supervision interface rather than a new optimizer. That interface makes the same AI judgment usable by several training stages and exposes information that would disappear if the collection were reduced immediately to a winning response.
