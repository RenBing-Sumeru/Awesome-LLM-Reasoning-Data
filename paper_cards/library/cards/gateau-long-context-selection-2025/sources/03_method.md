For HMG, the authors normalize response perplexity across LongAlign for LLaMA-2-7B-base-4K and its 64K homologous model, then take their difference. For CAM, the 64K model computes response-conditioned attention for equal-length input segments; segment importance comes from response perplexity when that segment alone is retained. Cosine similarity between normalized importance and attention vectors yields the contextual-awareness score. Both scores are normalized over the pool before weighting.

The main pool is LongAlign, 10K long samples synthesized from documents from nine sources with Claude 2.1; ShareGPT short instructions are mixed during final training to retain ordinary assistant behavior. The paper selects the top 10%, 30%, or 50% of long records and SFTs LLaMA-2-7B-base-64K, with additional 13B, ChatGLM3, and LLaMA3 analyses. It compares full LongAlign, raw perplexity, CaR, and Cherry Selection on LongBench, LongBench-Chat, and MT-Bench. Official code and selected long data are linked by the authors.
