With only 10% selected long records in the real-world setting, GATEAU-LLaMA reports 55.4 on LongBench-Chat and 58.6 on MT-Bench, versus 48.8 and 54.3 for full 100% LongAlign SFT. In the limited-short-instruction setting, the corresponding 10% scores are 58.0 and 53.4, versus 50.8 and 47.7 for full training. The 10% selected model also reaches a 56.1 LongBench average in the real-world setting, compared with 49.3 for full training.

Both components matter in the 7B and 13B ablations. For the 7B real-world 50% run, the full method reports LongBench single-document, multi-document, and summarization values of 38.9, 25.8, and 25.5, whereas removing CAM gives 38.4, 24.3, and 25.1 and removing HMG gives 38.6, 24.5, and 24.9. The paper finds 30% often gives the best LongBench balance and reports selected samples outperforming short-context selectors at equal ratios. These are benchmark and GPT-4-based evaluations of a particular synthetic pool, not a proof that every high score is a faithful long-dependency example.
