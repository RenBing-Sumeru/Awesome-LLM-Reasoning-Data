Conventional instruction selectors can rank difficulty, quality, or diversity without asking whether a long input is causally necessary for its response. GATEAU makes long-range dependence the selection target. It uses the gap between homologous context-window models to isolate context-sensitive generation, then separately asks whether a long-context model attends to segments that matter for the reference answer.

The method's novelty is the intersection of those two criteria before a record becomes SFT supervision. A long example with a hard response but no context-window benefit is not the intended target; neither is a long example whose attention is incoherent relative to segment relevance. This is therefore a data-consumption policy for long-context alignment, not an architecture change or a generic high-perplexity filter.
