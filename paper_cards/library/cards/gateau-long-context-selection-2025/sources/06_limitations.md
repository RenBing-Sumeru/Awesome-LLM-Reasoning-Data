HMG requires a homologous short-window and long-window pair, restricting use where such checkpoints do not exist or cannot be accessed. CAM depends on attention as a usable indicator of contextual use and requires repeated long-context computation, so selection is not inexpensive. The work focuses on overall instruction following and long-context understanding, rather than specialized task performance, and its central pool is Claude 2.1-synthesized LongAlign. The authors note that performance can decline when all high-scoring data are used or when data quality is mixed, so the selected fraction and source distribution need validation instead of assuming more selected records are always better.
