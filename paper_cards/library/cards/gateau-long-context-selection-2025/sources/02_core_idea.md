Homologous Models' Guidance compares response perplexities from two otherwise similar base models with short and long context windows. A large normalized difference is interpreted as evidence that the response becomes easier specifically when a model can use long-range context. This separates context-dependent generation difficulty from general instruction-following weakness better than a single model's perplexity.

Contextual Awareness Measurement divides the input into segments, estimates each segment's relevance to the reference response, and compares that relevance pattern with the long-context model's attention pattern. When attention is poorly aligned with important segments, the record is scored as difficult to understand through long-range relations. A weighted sum of the two scores ranks records; high-scoring records are selected for SFT as influential long samples.
