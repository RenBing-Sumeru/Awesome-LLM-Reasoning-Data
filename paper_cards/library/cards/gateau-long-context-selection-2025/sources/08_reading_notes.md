The paper's two scores answer different counterfactual questions. HMG asks whether a longer-window homologous model changes the likelihood of the target response; CAM asks whether the long model appears to place attention where the response-relevant segments are. Neither score alone defines data quality. The most informative evidence is the selected-fraction comparison, where 10–30% can outperform the full long pool, and the ablation showing each score adds value. Treat the result as guidance for this particular notion of long-context supervision, not as a general ranking of all instruction data.
