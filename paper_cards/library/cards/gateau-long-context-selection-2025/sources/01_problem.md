Synthetic long instruction data can have very long inputs without requiring a model to use them. A response may depend only on a nearby passage, or the input may contain unrelated concatenated material. Training uniformly on such records can spend a long-context SFT budget on examples that do not teach retrieval across distance, even when the underlying model has an extended context window.

GATEAU asks which long instruction-response records should be admitted to alignment training when the desired capability is modeling meaningful long-range dependencies. The data decision is selection from a 10K synthetic long-instruction pool, followed by ordinary SFT. The paper targets records where long context makes both response generation and input understanding genuinely difficult, rather than merely preferring greater sequence length.
