On Baize with LLaMA3-8B, MDS has the best average rank, 1.25, across twelve MT-Eval, ConsistentChat, and TopDial measures; the next strongest listed average rank is 4.58 for CC-Score. With Qwen3-8B it again obtains 1.25, ahead of 4.67 for DialScore. MDS also avoids the TopDial decline from all-data training: for LLaMA3-8B, all data lowers the LLM-EVAL score from 7.12 to 6.61, while MDS retains 7.12 and reports the highest entity F1 of 0.173.

On the Banking corpus, MDS reaches 6.72 G-EVAL on the in-domain test and 7.30 on out-of-domain ConsistentChat, the highest reported values, with entity F1 close to or above competing selections. Removing either global coverage or local structure lowers MT-Eval and TopDial performance; turning off semantic bins or the form filter particularly lowers TopDial entity F1. The paper further reports that long-turn entity F1 is 0.554 for MDS versus 0.538 for ZIP, and that MDS-only subsets contain fewer topic-drift and unsupported dialogues. These are evidence under LLM-judge and entity-based metrics, not human clinical or safety evaluation.
