The main distinction is that MDS has two different protections against bad data. The global stage protects dataset-level coverage of what users ask; the local stage protects whether a chosen conversation remains on topic, progresses, and answers in the requested form. Its strongest result is the consistent aggregate rank across diverse dialogue measures, not one isolated score. When reproducing, inspect the error-type and order-perturbation analyses as well as benchmarks, because the claim is about preserving cross-turn structure rather than simply selecting more fluent answers.
