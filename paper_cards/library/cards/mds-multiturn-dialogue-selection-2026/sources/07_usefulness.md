Use MDS when the unit of training is a conversation and preserving context across turns matters more than optimizing isolated reply quality. Preserve the dialogue pool revision, query encoder, number of semantic bins, coverage and redundancy weight, global retention fraction, entity-extraction and form-scoring prompts/models, threshold, bin quotas, selected dialogue IDs, turn expansion format, and LoRA training recipe. Compare against whole-dialogue random sampling and turn-level selectors under the same dialogue budget. The official repository and project page provide the release, but source-dialogue privacy, consent, licenses, and domain-specific safety need independent review before deployment.
