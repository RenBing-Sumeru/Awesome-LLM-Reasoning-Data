The global stage represents a dialogue with the aggregate of its user-query embeddings, rather than assistant responses that may carry stylistic noise. It clusters these trajectories into semantic bins, then greedily selects representative conversations while penalizing near-duplicates inside every bin. This prevents frequent templates from consuming the whole budget and preserves long-tail user intents.

The local stage asks whether a retained dialogue has usable structure. It scores each assistant turn for anchoring to entities introduced by the user and for informative novelty relative to earlier assistant turns, then separately checks whether the answer form matches the query form. A dialogue must pass form consistency and is ranked by its entity coherence–novelty score within its bin. Thus final SFT data couples global coverage with local multi-turn reliability.
