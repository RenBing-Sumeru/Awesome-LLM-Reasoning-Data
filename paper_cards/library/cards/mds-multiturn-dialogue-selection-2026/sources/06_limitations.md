The authors report that MDS does not substantially reduce contradiction errors, likely because the method deliberately retains longer, structurally richer conversations whose cross-turn dependencies make implicit conflicts difficult to detect. Entity extraction and form scoring rely on an instruction-tuned scorer and prompts, so errors or domain shifts in that scorer can change selection. The primary experiments cover Baize and a Banking corpus, two 8B backbones, and automated LLM-judge or entity metrics; results do not establish behavior for safety-critical conversations, other languages, or very different dialogue sources. Offline scoring also introduces a preprocessing cost that must be weighed against the benefit of a 10K-dialogue subset.
