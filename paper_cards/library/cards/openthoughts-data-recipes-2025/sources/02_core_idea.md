The project treats data construction as a sequence of choices rather than a single corpus. Problems from math, code, and science are paired with teacher-generated reasoning, then subjected to quality controls and filtering; more than 1,000 controlled experiments inform the released OpenThoughts3-1.2M recipe.

This makes the artifact useful for examining why a data pipeline works. The 1.2M count is not sufficient description by itself: teacher, prompts, filters, source mix, and training configuration are all part of the claimed recipe.
