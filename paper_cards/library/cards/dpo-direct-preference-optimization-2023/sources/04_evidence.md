The NeurIPS paper evaluates sentiment control, summarization, and single-turn dialogue. It reports that the direct objective matches or exceeds the compared RLHF methods while avoiding a separate reward-model fit and online sampling loop.

The evidence establishes DPO as a practical training consumer for pairwise preferences, which explains its broad later adoption. It does not prove that all preference datasets are reliable: label noise, response length, and reference-model choice still change what the objective learns.
