The benchmark inevitably inherits assumptions from its upstream datasets, GPT-4o judge, reward models, cleaning fractions, and selected base models. A method can benefit from a particular judge or reward model while encoding its bias, and choosing the best removal fraction after evaluation can overstate deploy-time gains. The paper tests broad combinations but not every domain, language, preference policy, or high-cost human-feedback workflow; teams must therefore preserve data splits, account for API and compute cost, and validate any cleaning rule against their own target behavior rather than treating the benchmark ranking as a universal prescription.
