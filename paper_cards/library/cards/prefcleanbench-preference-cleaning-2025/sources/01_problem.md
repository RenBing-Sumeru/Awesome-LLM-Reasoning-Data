Preference optimization assumes that a chosen response is genuinely better than its rejected counterpart, but human and model feedback can contain noise, inconsistency, and poor prompts or responses. Many cleaning proposals claim to repair this input, yet their results are usually tied to one dataset, one model, or one optimizer. Practitioners therefore lack a reliable basis for deciding whether a cleaning rule should change the records used for alignment.

PrefCleanBench asks how preference-data cleaning should be compared when the outcome that matters is downstream alignment. It defines a shared protocol that applies cleaning to several preference datasets, trains models with the cleaned and original records, and tests whether any improvement transfers across model families and preference-optimization objectives.
