The paper reports a controlled DPO comparison over four preference datasets and evaluates thirteen cleaning approaches. Its main table shows that no one technique wins every dataset and metric, while the choice to remove or flip a flagged label materially changes outcomes. The authors find that using multiple judges to identify unreliable records and removing those records often produces stronger win-tie rate and reward results than relying on a single judge or simply flipping labels.

The evaluation uses a GPT-4o judge for win-tie rate, a held-out reward model for average reward, alternative reward models in appendices, and a human comparison on a sampled Anthropic-HH subset. The code repository exposes the processing, cleaning, training, generation, and evaluation steps. The evidence is therefore strong for the benchmarked settings, but it does not establish a universally best cleaner or guarantee that its automatic judgments match every deployment's preferences.
