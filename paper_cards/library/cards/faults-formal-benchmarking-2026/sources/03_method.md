The authors analyze 13 benchmark variants containing about 10,000 problems. Corpus-scale Lean metaprograms surface defects such as counterexamples, vacuous theorems, and unsound axioms; some findings have machine-checkable certificates. A separate recall-oriented semantic-audit layer targets missing hypotheses, mistranslations, and evaluation loopholes that static tools cannot fully decide. For reuse, pin each benchmark revision, Lean/Mathlib environment, checker revision, theorem identifier, certificate, and the distinction between mechanically established and judgment-dependent claims. The official checker repository and corrected snapshots are the artifacts to inspect.
