This ICML 2026 paper asks what a machine-checked Lean benchmark actually certifies. The primary source is https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.29493 and the checker release is https://github.com/Shashi456/atp-checkers. The relevant data surface is a formal theorem plus audit output: static-check results, certified witnesses, fault category, and potentially a corrected version. It belongs in Programmatically Verifiable Outcome Data as a robustness study because it tests the reliability of the terminal predicate itself. The L4 decision boundary is explicit: Lean kernel acceptance proves a formal statement, not that the statement faithfully captures the intended problem or that the harness is ungameable.
