The paper replaces the blanket assumption of self-verifying formal benchmarks with a layered audit. It runs Lean-oriented static checkers across five commonly used benchmark families and their forks, distinguishes certified faults from semantic findings, and proposes release standards. The core data object is therefore an evidence-bearing defect record rather than an unqualified proof-pass bit. Closest comparisons are benchmark score reports and manual dataset cleanup; the contribution makes failure certificates, environment versions, and semantic fidelity first-class.
