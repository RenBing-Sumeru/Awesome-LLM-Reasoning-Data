Formal proof benchmarks are often presented as intrinsically trustworthy because Lean checks completed proofs. This ICML 2026 work makes the trust boundary concrete: checker-accepted proofs, formalization fidelity, evaluation harnesses, and maintenance drift are different claims. Lean checking and static analysis are not new; the direction signal is the corpus-scale release of a taxonomy, automated checks, and corrected snapshots that preserve provenance. It is especially useful as a counterweight to overconfident RLVR labels in formal domains.
