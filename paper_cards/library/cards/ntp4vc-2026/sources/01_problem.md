Neural Theorem Proving for Verification Conditions: A Real-World Benchmark asks how real-world verification conditions, prover-specific contexts, generated proofs, and acceptance results from Isabelle, Lean, or Rocq can provide a rigorous outcome-data surface rather than a subjective model judgment. Its decision boundary is programmatically verifiable outcomes: the result is a checker verdict, not merely a persuasive natural-language answer.

Source and audit record: Neural Theorem Proving for Verification Conditions: A Real-World Benchmark — https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18944. Venue/date: ICLR 2026; first public release: 2026-01-26. Deterministic verifier contract: Isabelle, Lean, and Rocq proof-checker acceptance for verification conditions. Data/evaluation object: real-world verification conditions, prover-specific contexts, generated proofs, and acceptance results from Isabelle, Lean, or Rocq. Actual pipeline: The benchmark presents a verification condition with its target-language context, a model synthesizes a proof, and Isabelle, Lean, or Rocq decides whether that proof is accepted. Quality/impact signal: ICLR 2026 conference paper; first real-world multi-language VC-proving benchmark. Reuse/audit caveat: Check prover versions, imported libraries, condition extraction provenance, and whether metrics compare equivalent search budgets across assistants.
