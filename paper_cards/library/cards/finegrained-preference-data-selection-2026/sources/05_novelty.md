Most preference filtering methods score a record using one global reward or difficulty estimate. This work makes disagreement among several annotated aspects the selection target, so a record is valued for cross-aspect consensus rather than only for confidence under its own label.

The paper separates record selection from the new fine-grained objective it derives. Its practical contribution is a portable data contract: aspect, pair, cross-aspect reward gaps, normalized divergence, and selection decision, followed by unchanged DPO.
