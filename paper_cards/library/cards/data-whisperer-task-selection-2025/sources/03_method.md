Each iteration samples disjoint demonstration and query groups from the task dataset and places the demonstrations after a fixed instruction. The pre-trained scoring model generates query answers; exact match scores GSM8K and ROUGE-L scores DialogSum and BioInstruct. The average query score is initially associated with every demonstration in that context, and repeated appearances are later averaged.

For each demonstration, the method sums self-attention from the selected layer and all heads between its tokens and all query tokens, then normalizes by the demonstration length. This attention weight refines its credited score. The main study uses ten demonstrations and five queries by default, selects a budgeted top-k subset, and LoRA-fine-tunes Llama-3-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, or Mistral-Nemo for five epochs; smaller same-family models can perform the scoring.
