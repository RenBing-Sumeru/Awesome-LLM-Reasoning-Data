The reported three-iteration Llama 2 70B model improves on instruction-following evaluation and achieves a strong AlpacaEval 2 result relative to several contemporary systems. The paper also reports improvement in the model’s own reward judgments across iterations.

These results support co-improvement in the reported setting, not an unconditional guarantee of self-correction. The core evidence relies on prompted model judgments and benchmark evaluations, so judge calibration, prompt choice, and contamination remain part of the interpretation.
