Reasoning distillation often fine-tunes a smaller student on a large pool of teacher-generated question-answer traces. Treating every trace as equally useful wastes the limited training budget: a student may repeatedly see skills it already solves while remaining weak on a few atomic operations. Selecting a question as one indivisible unit also hides that one mathematical solution can require several linked skills.

This paper asks how a distillation corpus can be selected for the current weaknesses of a particular student model. The training record is a math question, a teacher solution, and a path of skills attributed to that question in a hierarchy. The decision is both which records enter a 1,000-example SFT subset and whether the student sees the skill path before the solution; it is not a claim that the hierarchy itself is a universal mathematical ontology.
