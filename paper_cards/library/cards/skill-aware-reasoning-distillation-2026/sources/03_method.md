A Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct attributor traverses the Instruct-SkillMix tree top down for every candidate, repeatedly choosing relevant child skills until reaching leaves. The paper then evaluates the student over the 100K pool and computes leaf-skill accuracy. A skill's sampling probability is inversely proportional to that accuracy, capped to prevent a near-zero score from dominating; 1,000 examples are sampled from the resulting distribution. The authors manually inspect about 100 attributed pairs and report no observed missing or mislabeled skills, but this is a limited spot check rather than a full annotation audit.

For skill-aware SFT, the complete ordered chain is inserted before each reference solution. The main experiments use 100K unique OpenMathReasoning question-answer pairs, the predefined Instruct-SkillMix taxonomy, Qwen3-4B and Qwen3-8B students, and five SFT epochs. They compare full-pool, random, and skill-based 1K subsets with or without the skill-chain augmentation, then evaluate Avg@8 on AMC23, AIME 2024, AIME 2025, MATH Level 5, and OlympiadBench. The release states that code and the constructed data are available from the official repository.
