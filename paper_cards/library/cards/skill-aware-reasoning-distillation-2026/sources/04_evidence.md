The strongest reported comparison keeps the budget fixed at 1,000 records. For Qwen3-4B, random standard SFT averages 66.8, whereas skill-based selection plus skill-aware SFT reaches 68.4; for Qwen3-8B, the corresponding values are 68.1 and 69.5. Fine-tuning on all 100K records performs worse than the base models in both blocks, at 58.1 and 58.5 respectively, so the paper's evidence is not merely that added instructions help but that a targeted subset can avoid the full-pool degradation.

The ablation shows that selection and augmentation each contribute: skill-based selection alone reaches 67.3 and 68.5, while random skill-aware SFT reaches 67.6 and 69.4 for the two Qwen students. A further DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B test reaches 53.0 with the combined method versus 52.0 for random standard SFT. The paper reports that gains concentrate on emphasized weak skills and that the full hierarchy beats root-only or leaf-only prompts. These results support the specified math taxonomy and students; they do not establish equally reliable skill attribution or weak-skill estimation in non-math domains.
