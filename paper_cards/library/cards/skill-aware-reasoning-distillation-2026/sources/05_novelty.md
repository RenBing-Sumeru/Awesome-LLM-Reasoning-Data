Existing efficient-distillation methods can choose a small high-quality set, but often score a problem as one unit or use a fixed selection independent of the student. This work turns the record into a multi-skill object and bases sampling on the student's measured competence at the leaves. It also distinguishes selecting records from training on them: the skill chain is retained as explicit supervision rather than used only as an offline filter.

The novelty is the connection between a hierarchical attribution, a student-specific weakness profile, and a structured SFT target. It is not simply oversampling difficult questions. The full-chain ablation matters because the authors claim hierarchy carries useful dependencies beyond a flat list, while the skill-accuracy plot matters because it tests whether more sampling is actually directed to—and improves—the intended weak areas.
