The framework treats the student's per-skill accuracy as a demand signal. It maps each candidate solution from a 100K teacher corpus to one or more root-to-leaf chains in a predefined skill tree, evaluates the student on those leaf skills, and samples examples more often when their skills have lower accuracy. The selection target is therefore model-relative: a geometry-strong student should not receive the same mixture as a student weak in geometry.

The selected record is also structurally augmented. Before the teacher solution, skill-aware SFT prepends the ordered chains needed for the problem, such as mathematics to probability to Bayes' theorem. The paper's hypothesis is that this makes latent dependencies explicit instead of asking the model to infer skill organization only from question-answer pairs. Selection focuses the training budget, while the augmented chain changes what the chosen record asks the student to model.
