The approach relies on an existing skill tree that may not match the student's own decomposition of reasoning, and a per-skill accuracy estimated from limited examples can be noisy. One-time attribution of the 100K pool takes about 200 GPU hours according to the paper, so the cost is amortizable but not free. The principal experiments cover mathematics and two Qwen scales, with an additional distilled Llama model; broader domains and larger students remain unverified. Because weak-skill sampling can emphasize errors in the attribution or evaluation profile, reuse should audit the taxonomy, label quality, coverage, and whether short skill labels leak benchmark-specific structure.
