Self-consistency is not correctness: a model can repeatedly converge on the same wrong answer, especially when its errors are systematic. The procedure depends on reliably parsing a single final answer, restricting its immediate fit to tasks with canonical answers. A high threshold improves pair quality but sharply reduces quantity and diversity, while further iterations can make rejected answers harder to sample and may reduce response diversity. The paper explicitly leaves broader model families and non-parsable tasks for future study.
