Self-consistency is usually an inference-time vote that selects one answer after sampling. ScPO makes the whole vote distribution a training record: it determines whether a prompt is retained, which two responses become a pair, and the instance weight of the optimization loss. This is more specific than generic self-training because the confidence signal remains attached to the pair rather than being discarded after selection.

The method also distinguishes itself from self-evaluation and ordinary reward-model ranking. It does not ask the model to explain whether a response is right; it uses agreement among independently sampled final answers as the proxy. The contribution is therefore a constrained data-generation rule whose reliability can be audited by vote threshold, vote margin, parsing rate, and later gold-label comparison.
