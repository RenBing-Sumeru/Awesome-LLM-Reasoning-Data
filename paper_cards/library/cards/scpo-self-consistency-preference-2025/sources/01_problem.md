Complex reasoning data often has no gold answer, and model self-judging or an external reward model can be unreliable on difficult or out-of-distribution questions. Pure self-training can therefore reinforce responses that sound convincing but are not correct. Yet repeated sampling sometimes reveals a useful signal: independently generated correct solutions tend to agree on the same final answer more often than random errors do.

ScPO asks whether that agreement can be used to build a preference dataset without labels. It moves self-consistency from inference to training: for each unlabeled problem, the model's most-voted answer becomes the chosen response and its least-voted answer the rejected response. The record's vote margin determines how strongly that comparison changes the next model.
