For every chosen-rejected pair, γ-PO first obtains the reward quantities required by the selected base objective. It then calculates a dynamic target margin from their difference. The target rises for high-margin pairs, which strengthens their gradient signal, and falls for low-margin pairs, which smooths their contribution. The paper derives the equivalent dynamic-smoothing interpretation and keeps the rest of the paired optimization scaffold unchanged.

The method is evaluated as a plug-in for several margin-based objectives, including DPO and SimPO. The reported experiments use LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-Instruct, and Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct, then assess model outputs with AlpacaEval2, Arena-Hard, and MT-Bench. Reproduction must pin the underlying preference corpus, base objective, margin transformation, hyperparameters, evaluation prompts, judge versions, and the exact model checkpoint.
