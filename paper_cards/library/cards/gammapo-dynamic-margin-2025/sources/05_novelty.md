Prior robust preference approaches commonly smooth all labels with one setting or filter ambiguous pairs by a fixed rule. γ-PO moves the decision from corpus level to record level. The reward margin does not merely diagnose a pair after training; it changes the target imposed on that pair during every update, allowing two records from the same dataset to receive different degrees of sharpening or smoothing.

The contribution is not a new preference dataset, a new reward model, or an inference-time procedure. Its reusable novelty is a confidence-conditioned data-consumption rule for existing pairs. Before reuse, compare it with simple pair filtering and fixed label smoothing, verify that the chosen base objective yields a meaningful margin, and report whether margin adaptation changes calibration, diversity, and performance on underrepresented preferences.
