γ-PO interprets the pairwise reward margin as a confidence signal. A large margin indicates that the preferred and dispreferred answers are clearly separated under the objective; a small one indicates an uncertain comparison. Instead of applying a universal target margin, the method scales an individual target for each pair from this signal, thereby amplifying confident contrasts and reducing the pull of ambiguous labels.

The paper relates this rule to dynamic label smoothing. Small margins induce stronger smoothing, which prevents the optimizer from making a brittle, extreme distinction; large margins induce a sharper target. This retains all records while changing their effective supervision, and can be inserted into DPO-style methods that expose a reward-margin term rather than requiring a new response generator or a separate reward model.
