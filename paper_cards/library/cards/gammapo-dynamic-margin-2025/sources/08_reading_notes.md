The main memory hook is that γ-PO does not remove a preference pair; it changes how strongly the pair is allowed to specify the policy target. Its data-use decision is entirely driven by the current objective's margin, so margin calibration is the critical audit surface. The reported average 4.4% gain and plug-in experiments are useful evidence of practical viability, but the paper should be read alongside analyses of whether low-confidence labels are genuinely noisy or instead represent desirable diversity.
