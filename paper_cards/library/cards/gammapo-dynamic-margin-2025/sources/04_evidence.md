The paper reports that γ-PO improves the evaluated DPO-style baselines by an average of 4.4% across AlpacaEval2, Arena-Hard, and MT-Bench. It reports results across four base models and shows the dynamic-margin form as a plug-in rather than a model-specific replacement. The claimed efficiency boundary is also narrow but useful: the authors report minimal code changes and negligible effect on training efficiency relative to the base procedures.

The evidence supports the value of treating pair ambiguity differently within the reported datasets and judge-based benchmarks. It does not show that every small margin is mislabeled, nor that a reward margin is universally calibrated across domains. The paper's ablations and comparisons make the optimization change testable, but a deployment should separately inspect whether low-margin pairs represent harmless ambiguity, minority preference, or a systematic bias in the source labels.
