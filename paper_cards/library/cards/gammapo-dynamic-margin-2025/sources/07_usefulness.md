Use γ-PO when a preference-training run has a broad distribution of pairwise margins and retaining uncertain records is desirable, but treating them identically is not. Log the raw margin, the resulting target margin, the underlying loss, and the evaluation outcomes by margin bucket; without these records the behavior cannot be diagnosed. It is useful as a light-weight alternative to deleting ambiguous data, but it should not be used to declare the low-margin portion of a preference dataset unimportant without reviewing what those comparisons actually encode.
