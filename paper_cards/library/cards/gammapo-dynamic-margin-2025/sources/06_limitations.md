γ-PO makes the strong operational assumption that the objective's reward margin is a useful proxy for label confidence. A small margin may instead represent a meaningful but fine-grained preference, and smoothing it can systematically de-emphasize minority or difficult cases. The method also inherits the preference corpus's label quality and the base objective's calibration, while its dynamic mapping adds hyperparameters that may not transfer across model families or domains. It should therefore be audited with subgroup performance and pair-level label review, not only aggregate judge scores.
