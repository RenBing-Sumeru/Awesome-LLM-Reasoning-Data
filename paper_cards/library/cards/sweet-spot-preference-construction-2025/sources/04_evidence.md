The conventional max-min pipeline does not improve monotonically when responses per prompt increase from 5 to 200; the paper reports fluctuating or declining AlpacaEval 2 scores across all four LLaMA/Mistral base and instruct settings. In the 21-pair analysis with 200 samples, the best choices consistently use a rejected response near mean minus two standard deviations. For LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct, the pair of mean plus two standard deviations chosen and mean minus two standard deviations rejected reaches a 48.18% length-controlled win rate, about three points above the conventional construction.

The ablation heatmaps show small reward margins perform poorly: for LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct, pairing mean plus two standard deviations with mean plus one standard deviation gives 34.63% length-controlled win rate. Extending candidate positions to mean plus/minus three or four standard deviations does not yield a material improvement over the relevant extremes. The paper also reports that none of the evaluated preference-pair choices falls below the SFT checkpoint, but its main outcome is AlpacaEval 2, so the chosen reward distribution and evaluator remain central to how broadly the conclusion should be applied.
