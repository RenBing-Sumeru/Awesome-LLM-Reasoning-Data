For every prompt, the method fits the sampled reward scores with a normal distribution and represents the response pool by seven positions: minimum, mean minus two and one standard deviations, mean, mean plus one and two standard deviations, and maximum. It evaluates all 21 ordered chosen–rejected combinations rather than assuming the most distant observed pair is best.

The empirical sweet spot selects a high chosen response—typically maximum or mean plus two standard deviations—but uses mean minus two standard deviations as rejected rather than the absolute minimum. The proposal is therefore a construction rule over a reward distribution: preserve a substantial, controlled margin while avoiding an excessively pathological rejection. This distinction determines which two generated answers become the training record for every prompt.
