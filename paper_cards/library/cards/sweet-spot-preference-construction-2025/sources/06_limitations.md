The approach assumes reward distributions can be usefully approximated by a normal form and depends on the chosen reward model; skewed, multimodal, or poorly calibrated rewards can change which response lies near a nominal standard-deviation position. Generating 200 candidates and training 21 DPO models is an expensive analysis, even if the final construction rule is simpler. Evidence centers on UltraFeedback prompts, Armorm scores, four 7B/8B model settings, and AlpacaEval 2, so it does not prove that the same mean-minus-two-standard-deviation negative is optimal for safety, reasoning, multilingual, or highly constrained preferences. The paper also notes that the reward model's own limits and sampling temperature affect the pool.
