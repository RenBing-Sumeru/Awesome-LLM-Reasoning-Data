The authors start from SFT models and sample responses to UltraFeedback prompts with temperature 0.8, using the Armorm reward model to score each response. In the main distribution study, 200 responses per prompt are sampled, a mean and standard deviation are estimated, the seven representative responses closest to specified reward positions are identified, and 21 preference datasets are constructed. A separate DPO policy is trained for every pair type to isolate the effect of chosen/rejected positions.

The case study evaluates LLaMA-3-8B and Mistral-7B in base and instruction-tuned settings. Base models are first SFT-trained on UltraChat-200K; instruction settings begin from the public instruct checkpoints. The final scalable strategy samples a larger response pool, chooses the best high-reward response, and uses the response nearest mean minus two standard deviations as rejected. Evaluation uses AlpacaEval 2 win rate and length-controlled win rate. The paper releases code and constructed data at its official repository.
