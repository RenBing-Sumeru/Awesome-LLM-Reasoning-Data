The important result is not simply that reward margins should be large. The paper finds that the absolute lowest response becomes a worse negative as sampling reveals more extreme tails, whereas a response around mean minus two standard deviations retains a productive contrast. Read the seven reward positions as a diagnostic tool for a particular reward distribution, not as universal absolute scores. The most direct replication is to reproduce the 21-pair grid on a new policy and reward model before adopting the final pair rule at scale.
