Inputs are formal Lean theorem statements, available context, retrieved material, and a backend LLM. The pipeline alternates direct proof attempts, blueprint generation, subgoal decomposition, formal sketching, Lean code generation, compiler execution, revision, and backtracking. Outputs are candidate proofs, proof-graph states, compiler messages, and accepted Lean artifacts.

The verifier is Lean compilation/kernel checking. The paper evaluates the framework on IMO-LeanProofBench and reports Putnam 2025 results; the project page reports 70.0% overall solve rate across the benchmark. Reproduction must pin the Lean environment, theorem files, Mathlib/retrieval state, backend model, and search budget. The official paper, project page, and solution repository are the artifacts to audit.
