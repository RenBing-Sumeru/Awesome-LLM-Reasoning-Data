Across TinyLlama-1.1B and Alpaca-7B, three datasets, two alignment objectives, and PPO, GRPO, and REINFORCE++, InfOES improves the reported average test reward over the corresponding standard algorithm. In direct selector comparisons, it reaches 3.72 versus 3.39 for the strongest baseline on TinyLlama and 4.31 versus 3.97 on Alpaca, with the largest relative gain on the mixed-domain dataset.

The paper also measures saved optimization steps: across the listed settings, InfOES reaches the best standard-training performance with about 18.5–22.2% fewer steps on Alpaca and 19.4–21.8% fewer on TinyLlama. Ablations show that high-influence subsets train best while low-influence subsets can underperform the untrained base model. These results support objective-aware online selection, but use small models and reward-based alignment evaluations.
