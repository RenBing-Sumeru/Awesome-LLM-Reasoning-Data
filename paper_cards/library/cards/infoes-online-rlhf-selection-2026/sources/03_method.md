At the start of a round, the current policy greedily generates responses for a small validation set and computes an advantage-weighted log-probability surrogate return. Its average gradient is the objective feature. The policy then collects ordinary rollout experiences containing state, action, reward, log probability, value, and advantage; the RL gradient of each experience is paired with the validation feature to obtain its influence score.

Experiences above a threshold are retained for the policy update. The main experiments use harmlessness and helpfulness validation prompts from PKU-SafeRLHF, a TinyLlama reward model trained on seven public preference sources, and a threshold that discards the least influential 15% in each round. The selector is plugged into OpenRLHF implementations of PPO, GRPO, and REINFORCE++ with LoRA.
