PFP relies on an LLM to infer why a binary label was assigned, and on a fixed five-dimensional ontology that may omit culturally specific, task-specific, or conflicting preferences. System-prompt conditioning can reduce the response diversity needed to form useful pairs, while external feature extraction and prompt synthesis add cost and reproducibility dependencies. The target distribution comes from the seed preference data, so preserving it can also preserve the seed's biases. A deployment should audit feature assignments, monitor feature coverage by subgroup, and permit principled changes when the intended preference policy evolves.
