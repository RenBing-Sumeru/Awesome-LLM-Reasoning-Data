The paper reports that PFP improves AlpacaEval 2.0 length-controlled win rate by 7.66 percentage points over the SFT model and by 2.11 points over iterative DPO. It also reports improvement in harmlessness and honestness across online iterations on Anthropic-HHH, whereas the authors observe feature-distribution drift for the compared online methods. This connects the proposed data-control mechanism to both aggregate preference evaluation and a multi-aspect alignment evaluation.

The evidence also identifies a mechanism boundary: conditioning every response on one fixed prefix reduces response diversity and makes pair judgment harder, so PFP samples two prefixes per instruction. Its curriculum lowers prefix-sampling temperature to make comparisons harder gradually. These findings support the reported Mistral and UltraFeedback pipeline, but feature extraction is itself inferred by an LLM and does not prove that the five chosen feature classes capture all human preference causes.
