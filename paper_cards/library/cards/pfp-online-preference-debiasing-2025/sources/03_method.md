The method extracts feature labels from an offline seed dataset with an LLM feature extractor, then trains five feature classifiers. For an online instruction, the classifiers propose features and a distribution-preserving adjustment aligns their aggregate distribution with the initial one. A system-prompt synthesizer turns two sampled feature sets into prompts, the previous policy generates one response under each prompt, and SPA or a reward model selects the preferred response to form a labeled record.

Experiments start with 10,000 UltraFeedback seed examples and use four disjoint online batches of 5,000 prompts. Mistral-7B-v0.1 is initialized from an UltraChat SFT model, while PFP is compared with DPO, iterative DPO, and SPA on AlpacaEval 2.0, MT-Bench, and Anthropic-HHH. Reproduction requires the feature ontology, extractor and classifier versions, distribution target, prompt-synthesis template, temperature schedule, judge, batch order, and model checkpoints.
