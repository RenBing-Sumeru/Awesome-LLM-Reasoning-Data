Reliable labels are easier to obtain for simple reasoning problems than for difficult ones, while directly training on a large difficult unlabeled pool risks noisy pseudo-labels, reward hacking, and collapse. A static confidence filter either admits too much noise or leaves most hard data unused. The training question is therefore not only how to generate pseudo-labels, but when each uncertain record should become training supervision.

The paper asks whether a model can use a small easy labeled set to grow into harder reasoning data without assuming that every generated answer is trustworthy. It seeks a staged consumer of unlabeled records: accept cases already supported by repeated agreement, spend reflection effort on intermediate uncertainty, and postpone cases beyond the current policy's reliable frontier.
