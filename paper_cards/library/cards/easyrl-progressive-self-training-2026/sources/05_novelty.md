The distinctive choice is not simply easy-to-hard ordering. Easy examples are used as a trusted seed, while harder examples are admitted according to the current model's agreement and uncertainty structure. The method differentiates accepting, reflecting on, and deferring records, so the hard pool is a source of evolving supervision rather than an undifferentiated reward-free corpus.

It also makes pseudo-label quality a training-state variable. A record that is too uncertain in one round can become eligible after the policy is strengthened on easier selected records. This changes the data consumer over time and gives a concrete operational interpretation to a learning frontier, though the frontier is estimated by self-consistency and reflection rather than by a ground-truth verifier for the hard pool.
