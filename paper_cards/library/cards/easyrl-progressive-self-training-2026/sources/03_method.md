The reported training pool contains 20K DeepMath-103K problems: 2K labeled examples at difficulty 3.0–4.5 and 18K unlabeled examples at difficulty 5.0–10.0. A GRPO warm-up uses answer verification and boxed-format penalties. For each hard problem, the model makes two inferences; equal answers are selected immediately, while inconsistent answers with entropy at most 0.3 are passed to a reflection prompt that chooses or re-solves an answer.

Each round trains GRPO on the easy labels plus selected pseudo-labels, then repeats generation and selection on the deferred remainder. The paper uses a 4096-token maximum sequence length and evaluates Qwen2.5-Math 1.5B and 7B plus Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct. Its selection rule is therefore coupled to the current policy, but its training reward for selected hard data is agreement with a generated pseudo-label rather than an external answer key.
