Across the mathematical benchmarks, the paper reports that the third EasyRL iteration improves Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B's five-task average to 40.3 and Qwen2.5-Math-7B's to 50.6. With 10 percent easy labeled data, those figures exceed the reported full-label supervised GRPO averages of 39.5 and 49.5. The same table shows improvements over the base model on scientific biology, chemistry, and physics tasks, although those are transfer measurements rather than direct training data.

The ablation removes knowledge transfer, divide-and-conquer pseudo-labeling, or difficulty-progressive self-training and reports average drops across two models. The paper also tracks the selected data over iterations: selected average difficulty rises, while repeated self-training eventually converges and can slightly decline after the third round. These analyses support the staged selection claim, but they do not independently verify pseudo-label correctness for every selected record.
