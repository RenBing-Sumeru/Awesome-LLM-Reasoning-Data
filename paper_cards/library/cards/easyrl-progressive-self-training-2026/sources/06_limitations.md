EasyRL is tested mainly where answers can be checked or represented as discrete pseudo-labels; the authors explicitly leave open-ended, non-verifiable tasks for future work. Self-training can accumulate pseudo-label errors, and the paper observes convergence or slight decline after repeated rounds. Its acceptance thresholds, source difficulty labels, reflection behavior, and initial easy seed all affect which records become supervision, while the paper does not release the resulting pseudo-label sets for direct audit.
