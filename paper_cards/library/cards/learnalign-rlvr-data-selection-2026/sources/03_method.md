The pipeline first warms up the policy on a random subset. For every training question, it generates eight rollouts and calculates the fraction that satisfy the programmatic correctness condition. The success rate becomes learnability p(1-p). It also computes the GRPO gradient for the question, then uses random projection to store a lower-dimensional gradient representation.

For every pair of questions, the method multiplies their learnability values by the cosine similarity of projected gradients. Each question receives the average of its row in the resulting matrix, and the highest-scoring top-N questions are selected for later RLVR training. The reported settings use 300 warm-up GSM8K questions or 1,000 DAPO-MATH-17K questions, with GRPO-style training and binary final-answer rewards.
