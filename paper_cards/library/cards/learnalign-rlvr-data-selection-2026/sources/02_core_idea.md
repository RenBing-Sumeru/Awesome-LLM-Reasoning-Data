LearnAlign combines representativeness with learning potential. For a warmed-up policy, it computes a policy gradient for each question and compares its direction with other questions. A question whose gradient aligns with the broader training direction can reduce the loss of other records, so it is a representative candidate for a compact RLVR set.

Directional similarity alone loses magnitude information and does not distinguish a solved question from an impossible one. LearnAlign therefore weights normalized gradient alignment by p(1-p), where p is the success rate across rollouts. This quantity is largest at intermediate success, so the selection target favors questions with both usable correct trajectories and room for improvement, while avoiding length-driven gradient norms.
