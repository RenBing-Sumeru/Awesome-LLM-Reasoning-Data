RL with verifiable rewards can use many reasoning questions, but uniformly training on them wastes compute and can select examples that are already solved or almost impossible for the current policy. SFT selectors based on perplexity, raw gradients, or instruction difficulty do not match the RL objective, and raw gradient norms are biased toward short responses.

The paper asks which reasoning questions should reach RLVR post-training when no external quality labels are available. It scores a question by whether it is currently learnable and whether its policy-update gradient is representative of the wider data distribution. The desired subset should retain questions at the boundary of the model's capability, rather than merely the shortest, hardest, or highest-loss examples.
