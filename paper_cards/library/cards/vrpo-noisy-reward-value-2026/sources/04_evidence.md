Under rule-based noisy rewards, VRPO raises the reported Qwen3-8B average accuracy across four mathematics and three science tasks from 34.64% before RL to 38.14%, exceeding Reinforce++ at 36.60% and Dr.GRPO at 36.59%. In the weaker Qwen2.5-7B cold-start setting, it reaches 25.08% average accuracy, compared with 14.41% for GRPO and 20.60% for PPO. These comparisons support the claim that value-side regularization can stabilize optimization when reward noise is consequential.

The paper also reports that entropy and perplexity regularization of the value model obtains 42.41% average accuracy on a noisy math setting versus 39.51% for value-model cross-entropy, and that activating 80% of high-uncertainty tokens is better than activating all tokens. In learned-reward dialogue, it reports task-completion improvement from 72.1% to 75.9% and final average performance of 83.80%, while PPO and GRPO exhibit instability. The evidence is diverse but not a proof that every reward error can be corrected by a value model.
