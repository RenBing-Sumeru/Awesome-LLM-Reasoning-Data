Remember VRPO as a claim about where to place the noise filter: not only at the reward label, but at the value prediction that converts rewards into advantages. Its most concrete evidence is the value-model ablation, the multi-domain noisy-reward comparison, and the response-length behavior under dialogue reward hacking. The key audit question is whether the richer value model captures return-relevant semantics or merely learns another proxy that will fail under a new reward distribution.
