Robust RL work often modifies the reward model or filters noisy records before a policy update. VRPO instead makes the value function the explicit intervention point: a value prediction is trained to be compact and semantically aware, then supplies the advantage used by the policy objective. This changes the consumer of a noisy reward without claiming to make the original reward label clean.

The paper does not introduce a new reward dataset or a general verifier. Its reusable novelty is the combination of information-bottleneck value modeling and selective language-model-guided losses as a way to transform reward feedback into policy gradients. Before reuse, compare against direct reward denoising, inspect whether key-token selection tracks useful content rather than style, and measure whether it reduces reward hacking without suppressing legitimate long reasoning.
