Existing long-CoT distillation usually treats a long teacher trace as the single desired target, whereas generic compression can remove useful steps. LS-Mixture makes paired long and structure-preserved short traces a supervised design choice, allowing a student to learn length control from training data.

The novelty is the paired-record construction and mixed SFT objective for efficient reasoning. It is not the general fact that short answers are cheaper; its contribution is attaching a controlled short counterpart to a long reasoning record before the student is trained.
