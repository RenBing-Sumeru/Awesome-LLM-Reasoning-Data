With 50K training records, the reported 12B DORM reward model reaches 90.5 overall on RewardBench. The paper compares this with 90.4 for ArmoRM trained on 585.4K records and 90.2 for InternLM2-20B-reward trained on 2.4 million records. Its component study reports an average of 90.2 for the combined method, above 85.3 for unweighted SFT, 86.4 for prior weighting only, and 89.1 for bilevel optimization without the prior.

For a downstream test, the authors use the trained reward model to label UltraFeedback and align a Mistral-7B policy. They report a 61.2% win rate, above the listed ablations and public reward-model baselines. The evidence supports data efficiency, but it comes from the authors' mixture, a 5K validation regime, and an independent reward model used as the policy evaluator.
