DORM is essential for teams choosing among heterogeneous preference sources, especially when labels cannot safely be removed by a single heuristic. It gives a concrete way to document uncertainty, disagreement, a weight prior, validation signal, and the eventual training consumer. Its practical cost means it is better treated as an ablation-backed recipe than a default: first validate the source mixture and held-out set, then compare record-level, source-level, and simple filtering alternatives under the available compute budget.
