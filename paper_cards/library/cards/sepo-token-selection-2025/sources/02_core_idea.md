SePO uses DPO itself as a reward estimator. Given a reference model and an oracle obtained by DPO on preference pairs, the log probability ratio for a token under the oracle and reference provides a token-level quantity proportional to the token's reward contribution. High ratios in chosen responses and low ratios in rejected responses are treated as the most discriminative evidence for the pairwise preference.

The central move is to convert that estimate into a binary training mask. The target policy no longer receives loss on every token: it receives a contrastive objective over the top-scoring chosen tokens and bottom-scoring rejected tokens. A small oracle can label a much larger policy's training data because it identifies useful positions rather than directly generating the target response. This gives a concrete route from coarse preferences to sparse, model-consumable supervision.
