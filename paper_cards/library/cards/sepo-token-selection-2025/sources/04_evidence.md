Across Arena-Hard, AlpacaEval 2.0, and MT-Bench, the main setting selects only 30 percent of response tokens. The paper reports that this reduces GPU training hours by 39.94 to 62.34 percent relative to comparison methods while outperforming the principal response-level and token-level baselines on most tested policy models. For LLaMA-3-Base-8B, SePO reaches 25.0 percent Arena-Hard win rate and 25.6 percent length-controlled AlpacaEval win rate, compared with 20.6 and 21.78 for SparsePO and 15.9 and 18.2 for DPO.

The controls connect performance to selection quality rather than sparsity alone. Randomly selecting the same share of tokens sharply lowers results; on LLaMA-3-Base-8B it yields 6.2 percent Arena-Hard and 10.6 percent length-controlled AlpacaEval win rates. The study also reports that a TinyLLaMA-1.1B or Pythia-1B oracle can guide substantially larger policies, and examines out-of-distribution HH-RLHF supervision to show selective masks can reduce over-optimization on weaker data.
