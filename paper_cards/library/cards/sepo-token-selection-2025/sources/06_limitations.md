The key-token fraction is empirically set and may need to change substantially across data distributions and target policies; the paper leaves automatic ratio selection open. Evidence does not extend to stronger policy models such as 70B systems, and the token estimator inherits any coverage or preference defects in its sampled oracle-training data. The evaluation relies on public instruction-following benchmarks judged by GPT-4o and does not provide a dedicated human audit showing that every selected mask corresponds to a human-interpretable preference rationale.
