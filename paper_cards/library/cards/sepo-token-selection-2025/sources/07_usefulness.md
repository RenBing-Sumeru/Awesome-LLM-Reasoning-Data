Use SePO when pairwise preference data is available but full-response optimization is expensive or suspected to be noisy. The recipe is especially useful for studying which parts of a response are actually consumed by an alignment objective, for reusing an existing preference set with a smaller training budget, or for testing whether a modest model can identify useful learning positions for a larger policy. Before deployment, validate the token-mask distribution, selection fraction, judge sensitivity, and licenses of the underlying pairs rather than assuming the reported 30 percent is universal.
