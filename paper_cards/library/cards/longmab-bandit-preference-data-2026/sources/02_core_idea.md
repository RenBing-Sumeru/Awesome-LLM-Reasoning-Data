LongMab treats every equal-length context chunk as an arm in a multi-armed bandit. A rollout selects a small subset of chunks, generates an answer from that subset and the question, scores the answer against the known answer, and feeds the resulting reward back to the chunks selected in that rollout. This converts evidence selection into an iterative estimate of which chunks help create correct answers.

The exploration-exploitation balance is then used to improve the preference-data distribution rather than only to choose an answer at test time. After several rollouts, the highest- and lowest-reward generated answers become a preferred and dispreferred pair under the original context and question. The resulting DPO consumer receives answer pairs whose difference reflects learned evidence selection, not just random decoding noise.
