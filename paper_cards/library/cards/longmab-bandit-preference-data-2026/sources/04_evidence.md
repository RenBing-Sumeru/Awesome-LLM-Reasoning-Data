On five long-context question-answering tasks, LongMab improves the reported average F1 for Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct to 47.43. The strongest listed DPO baseline, LongFaith-PO, reaches 43.15; for Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct, LongMab reaches 42.26 while the strongest listed DPO baseline reaches 37.92. The paper therefore reports gains exceeding four points over its compared preference-optimization methods.

Ablations make the construction claim more specific. Removing UCB selection lowers average F1 from 47.43 to 44.19 on Llama and from 42.26 to 38.76 on Qwen; removing the initialization also lowers performance. The authors further report that the selected preferred answers improve by more than six points over the random-chunk and full-context variants, while response diversity remains comparable to random chunk sampling. These results support, but do not independently prove, the value of the bandit-built pairs.
