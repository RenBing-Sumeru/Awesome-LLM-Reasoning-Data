Long-context models can overlook evidence in the middle of an input, and synthetic long-context training pairs are often repetitive or factually inconsistent. Full-context response sampling also lets irrelevant passages disrupt generation. For preference optimization, this is a data problem: the quality and diversity of chosen/rejected answers depend on which parts of the context were used to produce the candidate responses.

The paper asks how to construct preference pairs that teach a model to locate and combine useful long-context evidence. It does not simply score completed answers from a fixed prompt. Instead, it makes the selection of context chunks an adaptive data-construction decision, so repeated rollouts can discover useful chunk combinations before the best and worst responses are converted into a DPO record.
