On the target-agnostic Alpaca setting, ProDS compares with IFD and other selection rules across Vicuna, Koala, WizardLM, Self-Instruct, and LIMA-style evaluations. The paper reports that its selected top 5% is closer to the WizardLM test distribution after t-SNE than IFD's top 5% (average Euclidean distance 67.13 versus 85.85), and analyses show its gains are not explained only by shorter preferred responses.

For targeted selection, the paper compares with BM25, DSIR, RDS, and LESS on MMLU, TyDiQA, and BBH while asserting no training examples are directly related to those target tasks. This supports the claim that preference-derived selection can add information beyond answer-generation similarity, but GPT-4 pair judgment and validation construction remain possible sources of target leakage and evaluator bias.
