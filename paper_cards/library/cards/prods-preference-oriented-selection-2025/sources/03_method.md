First, ProDS randomly takes 5% of the training pool for SFT warm-up. The warmed-up model generates target responses; when GPT-4 judges a generation different from the reference, the generation becomes a rejected response and the reference becomes a chosen response for a 5% DPO warm-up. The resulting DPO gradients define positive and negative preference directions on the target validation set.

For every candidate training triplet, ProDS computes an influence representation and cosine similarities to both direction sets. Simulated annealing sets an instance-level trade-off between positive alignment and negative alignment, producing a final rank; the top subset is used for SFT. The paper uses Alpaca's 52,002 triplets and also roughly 270K examples from FLAN V2, CoT, Dolly, and OpenAssistant, so a reuse must keep target-validation construction separate from final testing.
