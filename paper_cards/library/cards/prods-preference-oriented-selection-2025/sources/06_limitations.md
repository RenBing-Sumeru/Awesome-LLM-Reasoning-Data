ProDS adds offline SFT and DPO warm-up, GPT-4 comparison, influence computation, and annealing before the final SFT, so its data-efficiency claim does not mean the selection process is cheap. Its preference representation relies only on DPO gradients, as the authors note, and target preferences may depend on a judge or reference style rather than stable user values. Because validation samples are derived from test-set structures, a reuse must separate selection validation from the final benchmark and audit every model/API and dataset license.
