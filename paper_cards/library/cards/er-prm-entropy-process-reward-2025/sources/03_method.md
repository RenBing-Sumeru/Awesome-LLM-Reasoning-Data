For each math problem, the generator samples 15 stepwise solutions. For every partial step, a completer produces 16 continuations and each continuation receives one if its final answer is correct and zero otherwise. ER-PRM applies its entropy-regularized formula to these outcomes, yielding one continuous reward label per step; it reports about 260K examples and 1.5 million step labels for each source policy.

Llama-3.1-8B learns to predict a special positive token after every step, using cross-entropy and the generated labels. Candidate solutions are ranked by their lowest step score in best-of-N. For policy improvement, 16 answers per question are ranked by the PRM, the top rationale is retained, and rejection-sampling fine-tuning uses MATH plus 12,500 OpenMathInstruct-2 questions.
