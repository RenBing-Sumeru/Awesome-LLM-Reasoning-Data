The novelty is not using likelihood alone, but moving the likelihood's conditioning and aggregation boundary from a complete response to local steps. GRAPE-style global selection is a sensible baseline for short, single-teacher responses; LALP identifies its fluency trap when traces are long and teachers differ in style, then creates a student-aware score at the level where transfer is observed.

This makes the selected record auditable: a reviewer can inspect the segmentation, local window, per-step scores, and answer verifier separately. The method is not a semantic judge of reasoning truth and does not generate new traces. Before reuse, check the segmentation model, context-window fraction, candidate answer verification, and whether target tasks exhibit the same step-compositional coverage.
