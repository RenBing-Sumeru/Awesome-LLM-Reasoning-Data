LALP assumes that a step segmentation is meaningful and that immediate context is sufficient to judge a transition; the paper's segmentation relies on another model, GLM-4.5-Air. It costs more preprocessing than GALP because every step is rescored, and its evidence centers on answer-verified long traces from three teachers. It cannot protect against a wrong final answer that entered the candidate pool, nor does step plausibility prove that a full trace is globally valid.
