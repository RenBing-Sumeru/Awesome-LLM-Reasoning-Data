The failure case is concrete: under both 7B and 32B students, Qwen3-32B traces receive the highest global score but lead to the weakest downstream accuracy, whereas QwQ-32B gives the best SFT outcome without the highest GALP. LALP instead recovers the downstream teacher order. In the teacher-selection table, the 32B student reaches a seven-benchmark average of 0.726 with LALP selection, compared with 0.632 for GALP and 0.651 for random selection.

For per-prompt selection, the paper reports up to a 9.4% math-accuracy improvement over GALP. It also evaluates beyond math on GPQA-Diamond and LiveCodeBench. Its mechanism evidence supports the proposed granularity: on AIME 2025, nearest-neighbor coverage is 0.935 for steps but 0.760 for whole trajectories, so the claim is about the long mixed-teacher regime rather than every form of SFT.
