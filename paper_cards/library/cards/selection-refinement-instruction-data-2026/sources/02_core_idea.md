The method differentiates quality, then applies an evaluate-refine-review loop and output alignment to weak records. This changes the training decision rather than only describing a corpus. The important unit is not an abstract notion of “quality,” but a concrete object that can be consumed by training: an instruction pair, a generated task, a reference-derived requirement, or a verifier-backed trajectory. The method makes the signal attached to that object explicit enough to rank, revise, generate, or reward it.

The selection or reward rule is model- and setting-dependent, so it must be evaluated under a matched recipe. This framing also separates the claim from generic model scaling. If the same backbone, budget, and optimizer are held fixed, any difference can be attributed more directly to how records are selected, structured, or rewarded. That is the connection between the paper and a reproducible training-data decision.
