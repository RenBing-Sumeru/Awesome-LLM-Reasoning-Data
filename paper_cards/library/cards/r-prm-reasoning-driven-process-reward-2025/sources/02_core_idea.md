The central record is a mathematical problem, a partial reasoning trace, a target step, and a process judgement. In the released resource, supervised records teach reasoning-style evaluation and preference records compare alternative evaluations without requiring another round of human step annotation.

This changes the feedback object from an opaque scalar into a generated assessment that can be inspected or used as a reward. It remains a model judgement rather than a formal proof: the explanation makes the reward easier to audit, but it does not itself verify mathematical correctness.
