The paper reports F1 gains of 11.9 points on ProcessBench and 8.5 points on PRMBench over its stated strong baselines, plus more than 8.5 points of accuracy improvement across six mathematical datasets when used to guide reasoning.

These measurements support the proposed generated-judgement and preference-training pipeline in its mathematical setups. They do not show that a fluent explanation is faithful, or that a judgement trained from synthetic seed data will remain calibrated for another model family, subject area, or notation convention.
