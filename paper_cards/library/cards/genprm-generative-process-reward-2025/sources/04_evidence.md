Using roughly 23K MATH conversations, the paper reports that a 1.5B GenPRM exceeds GPT-4o and a 7B version exceeds Qwen2.5-Math-PRM-72B on ProcessBench. The comparison supports the paired design of generated reasoning, verification, and test-time aggregation.

It does not establish that every rationale is correct. Executable checks cover only what is encoded, and the result depends on candidate rollouts, benchmark construction, and the chosen aggregation budget.
