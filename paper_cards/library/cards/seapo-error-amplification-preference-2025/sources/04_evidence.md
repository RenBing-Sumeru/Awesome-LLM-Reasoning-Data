The paper evaluates models from 1.5B to 14B over reasoning, code, knowledge, translation, and truthfulness settings. It reports that its constructed negatives improve overall performance and gives truthfulness gains of five to ten percentage points in the reported comparisons.

Ablations compare error types, mixtures, severity, and objectives. They show that frequent task-relevant errors help related tasks, while mixed errors give broader gains; results still vary with data source and task difficulty.
