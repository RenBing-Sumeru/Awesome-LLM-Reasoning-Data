Single-dimensional scores such as perplexity or one reward value miss the varied properties that make synthetic instruction data useful. CrowdSelect selects synthetic instruction pairs with complementary signals from multiple LLM responses and reward-model assessments. In a real post-training pipeline, this uncertainty is costly because every retained example consumes tokens, optimization steps, and evaluation capacity. A useful intervention must therefore specify not only a desirable outcome, but also which record-level decision changes before training begins.

The paper asks which training records or reward signals should be consumed under a fixed post-training budget. The paper treats this as a decision about the training surface: the model sees a different subset, environment, or reward-bearing record, and its reported outcome should be compared with a matched alternative rather than with an unrelated model.
