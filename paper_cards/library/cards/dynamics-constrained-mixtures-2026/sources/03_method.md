Fine-tuning sources are separated from evaluation domains. Evaluation domains are partitioned into targets and constraints, and need not be included in the training mixture. At every update, DynaMiCS probes each available source with the current optimizer for a small number of steps, evaluates the temporary checkpoint on all domains, and uses the finite differences to fill the slope matrix. It then solves a probability-simplex problem with a squared-hinge penalty for predicted constraint violations; when no feasible solution exists, the least-violating mixture is selected.

The paper evaluates fifty scenarios with one to three targets and three to ten constraints, covering math, code, instruction following, medical QA, function calling, multilingual data, safety, commonsense reasoning, and general knowledge. It uses Qwen2.5-3B at LoRA ranks 8, 32, and 64, Qwen3-8B, and Gemma3-12B, each trained for 2,048 steps with batch size 8. Geometric schedules re-estimate the mixture more often early in training; the paper also tests accuracy constraints, showing that the finite-difference signal does not require a differentiable evaluation metric.
