The evaluation spans fifty scenarios, five model configurations, and three random seeds, totaling more than 7,000 fine-tuning runs. On Qwen2.5-3B, light and dense warmup schedules reach median target perplexity reductions of 8–9%, compared with 0.4% for best-of-ten fixed mixtures. On Qwen3-8B, DynaMiCS reaches 11% while the fixed baselines are at 0%; on Gemma3-12B, it reaches about 24%, versus 18% for the stronger fixed baseline. Infeasible runs contribute zero, so these measurements include both target improvement and the ability to satisfy all constraints.

The paper reports that a single adaptive run costs about 1.4–1.7 times one fixed run, while a ten-run mixture search costs ten times the baseline and still often fails to find a feasible checkpoint on Qwen models. Early updates matter: for Qwen3-8B, adding early probes raises median reduction from 5.8% to 11%. In four accuracy-constrained Gemma scenarios, the dense schedule finds feasible solutions in all four, although the tightest case has high seed variance. This evidence supports local, constraint-aware adaptation in the reported settings; it does not prove that the chosen constraint batches represent every capability a deployed model must retain.
