Static mixtures decide data-source proportions before training, and many dynamic methods change those proportions from a reward, gradient, or controller signal without enforcing preservation. DynaMiCS changes the decision boundary by naming protected evaluation domains and treating their base-model performance as explicit constraints. It can use a benchmark that is never sampled as training data, so the training source and the capability being protected are deliberately separate objects.

Its distinctive mechanism is finite-difference probing followed by constrained mixture optimization. It is not simply a curriculum: temporary source-specific updates produce a matrix of transfer and interference estimates, and a penalty program converts that matrix into weights. The non-differentiable accuracy experiments matter because they show the signal can be defined by any repeatable evaluation, while the constrained formulation makes preservation a first-class part of the data-consumption decision.
