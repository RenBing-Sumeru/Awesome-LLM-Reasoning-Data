The key insight is that a data mixture is a constrained control decision, not merely a balance of domains. DynaMiCS uses evaluation-only domains to decide how to consume training sources, so the quality of that evaluation contract is at least as important as the candidate corpora. The most informative replication checks are the slope-prediction error over an update horizon, feasibility under held-out constraints, sensitivity to early updates, and whether a source that initially helps a target later becomes an interference source. The method is especially valuable when safety or retention must be measured alongside specialization rather than assumed from an average score.
