With 10% selection, the reported mathematics average for Qwen2.5-7B is 31.63, compared with 27.05 for full 10K-data SFT; the AIME 2024 score is 7.71 versus 5.00. In the general setting, the selected subset has a 12.09% average over AlpacaEval and Arena-Hard compared with 8.15% for full-data training. The reported medical average is 56.42 versus 53.14, and code at a 20% budget is 45.1 versus 43.0.

Ablations support both parts of the selection rule: removing bidirectional NLL filtering lowers the mathematics, general, and medical figures, and selecting the lowest entropy-difference bin outperforms the middle and highest bins. On a 100K mathematics pool, selecting 10K reaches 29.80 against 27.16 for full training and 27.94 for random selection. The work also reports sensitivity: a 1% warmup is weaker, same-family calibration produces more overlap and better downstream selection than cross-family calibration, and two iterations give the strongest cost-performance trade-off before gains diminish.
