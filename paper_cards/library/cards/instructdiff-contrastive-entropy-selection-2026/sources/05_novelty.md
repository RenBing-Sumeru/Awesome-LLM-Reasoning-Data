Many earlier selection methods evaluate one model state, use a static quality scorer, or specialize to either generic instruction data or verifiable reasoning data. This paper makes the selected record depend on a before-versus-after calibration comparison. It therefore treats the distributional response of a particular base model as part of the selection signal rather than asking a separate judge to assign a universal record quality.

Its contribution is not entropy regularization during optimization. Entropy is an offline measurement used to decide which response records are admitted to SFT, coupled to a symmetric learnability filter. The domain-adaptive aspect is also specific: the ranking direction stays fixed at lowest contrastive entropy, while the observed direction of uncertainty change differs between reasoning and general data. This separation of a common ordering from different underlying regimes is the paper's central design claim.
