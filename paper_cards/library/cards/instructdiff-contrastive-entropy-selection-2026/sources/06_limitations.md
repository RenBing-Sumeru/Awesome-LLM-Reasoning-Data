Selection quality depends on a sufficiently good warmup calibration; the paper shows that a 1% warmup produces noisy distribution gaps and recommends about 1,000 warmup examples. The selected subsets are model-family specific: same-family models overlap far more than cross-family models, so reuse of a subset for another family is not assured. The reject and selection ratios are fixed at 0.1 in the main experiments, and automatic ratio choice remains open. Finally, the causal reason that entropy expansion appears useful for reasoning while compression appears useful for general tasks is not established, so the reported criterion should be revalidated when data, models, or task formats change.
