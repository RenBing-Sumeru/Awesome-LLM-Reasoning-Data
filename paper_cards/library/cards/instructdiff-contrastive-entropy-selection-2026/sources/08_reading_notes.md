The easiest way to read this work is to keep its two scores separate. NLL difference is a gate for records at a plausible learning frontier; contrastive entropy is the ordering inside that gate. The headline result is therefore not that low-entropy text is always good—absolute entropy baselines are tested separately—but that the change in uncertainty caused by a small calibration step identifies a useful training subset. The cross-family analysis is operationally important: the chosen records describe a relationship between a data pool and a model state, not an intrinsic ranked dataset.
