InstructDiff compares two states of the same model family: the untouched base model and a copy lightly fine-tuned on a random warmup subset. For every candidate record, it measures the difference in response negative log likelihood and the difference in token-distribution entropy. The first quantity brackets a middle range of examples that are neither nearly redundant nor pathological; the second ranks the surviving examples.

The notable empirical claim is that the lowest contrastive-entropy records work best in every tested domain, even though the sign has different interpretations. Reasoning examples tend to show an entropy increase after calibration, described as cognitive expansion, whereas general instruction examples tend to show uncertainty compression. Thus the method does not hard-code a separate reasoning rule: it uses the relative state change to decide what data the next SFT run will see.
