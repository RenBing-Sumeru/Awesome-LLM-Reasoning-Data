The procedure begins by randomly drawing roughly 10% of an instruction pool and fine-tuning it to form a calibration model. For each candidate, the authors compute length-normalized NLL and entropy under the base and calibration models. They discard the lowest and highest 10% of NLL differences, treating the former as low-signal near duplicates and the latter as potentially incomprehensible patterns, then select the lowest contrastive-entropy 10% of the original pool; code experiments use 20% for code.

The paper can repeat this loop after SFT, using the improved model as the next calibration state. It evaluates 10K-record pools of NuminaMath, Alpaca, MedCAQA, and BigCode with Qwen2.5-7B for mathematics and Llama3-8B elsewhere, and also tests 100K mathematics and 52K general pools. Comparisons include random, perplexity, absolute entropy, length, IFD, SelectIT, ZIP, Superfiltering, and full-data SFT. The paper explicitly links an official implementation repository.
