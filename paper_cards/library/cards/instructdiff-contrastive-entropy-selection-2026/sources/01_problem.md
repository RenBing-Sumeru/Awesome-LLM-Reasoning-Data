Supervised fine-tuning is increasingly performed on large instruction pools, yet training on every record can be expensive and can yield diminishing or negative returns. Existing selection rules are often tuned to one setting: heuristics that identify good conversational instructions need not identify useful mathematical reasoning traces, while difficulty or correctness filters designed for reasoning need not transfer to general instruction following.

The paper asks whether a single model-relative measurement can decide which instruction-response records should enter a limited SFT budget across domains. Its object is not generation at inference time: it changes the subset consumed by training. The intended decision is to retain records whose change in uncertainty between a base model and a lightly calibrated model signals useful learning, while rejecting records that are too redundant or too anomalous.
