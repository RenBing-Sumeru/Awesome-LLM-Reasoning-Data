Earlier direct preference objectives make the comparison between a winner and loser the primitive training event. KTO instead elevates the individual labeled response to that role, while retaining a principled reference-dependent loss rather than falling back to ordinary positive-only supervised fine-tuning.

The novelty is consequently both conceptual and operational. Prospect theory motivates the asymmetric treatment of good and bad feedback, and the resulting objective changes what annotators or automated judges need to emit for an example to become usable by the trainer.
