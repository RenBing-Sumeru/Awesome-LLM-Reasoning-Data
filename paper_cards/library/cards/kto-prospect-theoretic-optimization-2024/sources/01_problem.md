Many alignment objectives assume that feedback arrives as a preferred and rejected response for the same prompt. Such paired data is costly to obtain and discards the far more common operational signal that an individual response is simply acceptable or unacceptable.

KTO asks whether an alignment objective can consume those binary judgments without reconstructing preference pairs. The intended change is to make the training objective fit the available feedback record, rather than requiring data collection to imitate the format expected by an existing optimizer.
