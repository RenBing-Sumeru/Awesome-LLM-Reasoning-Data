GUI agents need trajectories that connect a user goal, changing observations, intermediate reasoning, and grounded actions. Such records are scarce online because they must be collected in a live interface; relying on human annotation makes browser-agent training expensive and difficult to scale.

AgentTrek asks whether public web tutorials can become a source of executable trajectory supervision. It treats tutorials as imperfect procedural knowledge that must be turned into tasks, replayed in an environment, and checked before the resulting trace is used to train an agent.
