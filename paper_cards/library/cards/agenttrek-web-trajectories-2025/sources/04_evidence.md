The paper evaluates agents trained on synthesized trajectories on textual web browsing benchmarks such as WebArena and visual grounding and browsing benchmarks including ScreenSpot Web and Multimodal Mind2Web. The comparison is meaningful because it tests whether the generated trajectories improve an agent’s subsequent interactive behavior rather than only whether a tutorial was parsed correctly.

The release describes 52,594 dialogue turns in the training dataset and the paper reports state-of-the-art results across the stated textual and visual settings. These results support guided replay in those environments, but they do not by themselves demonstrate robustness to changing websites, private workflows, or evaluator errors that accept a superficially plausible completion.
