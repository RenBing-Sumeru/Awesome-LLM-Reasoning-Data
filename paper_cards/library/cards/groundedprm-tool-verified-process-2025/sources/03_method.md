For each MATH problem, MCTS selects a partial state by UCT, expands it with candidate LLM steps, verifies steps through a math tool, and rolls paths to a terminal answer. Final-answer correctness and verified step values are aggregated into rollout rewards, which update node values along the tree. The construction filters incomplete, inconsistent, and tool-unverifiable traces, leaving roughly 40K records with a problem, full trace, step labels, and explanations.

The reported model is Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct fine-tuned generatively with LoRA. The paper specifies rank 128, six epochs, batch size four, eight gradient-accumulation steps, and learning rate 3e-5. It evaluates step classification on ProcessBench and uses the PRM to choose steps during reward-guided greedy search, comparing against automated, mixed, and human-labeled PRMs.
