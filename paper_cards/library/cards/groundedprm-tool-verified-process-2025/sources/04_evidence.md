On ProcessBench, GroundedPRM reports average F1 39.7 across GSM8K, MATH, OlympiadBench, and Omni-MATH. The strongest automated-label baseline shown, Math-Shepherd-PRM-7B, reports 31.5 average F1 using 445K samples, while GroundedPRM uses 40K; this is the paper's 26% relative-improvement claim. Its F1 is 47.0 on MATH and 34.4 on Omni-MATH, compared with 29.5 and 23.8 for that baseline.

For reward-guided greedy search with a Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct policy, the reported six-set average is 42.4. The displayed automated and human/mixed comparators range from 37.7 to 41.9, with the best automated EurusPRM stage reaching 41.1. These comparisons support the narrow conclusion that the constructed reward records work as an inference-time selection signal in the reported mathematics settings.
