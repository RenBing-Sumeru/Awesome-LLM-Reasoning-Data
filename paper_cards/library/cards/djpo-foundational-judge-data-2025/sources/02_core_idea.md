DJPO treats a judgment label as a seed for several distinct preference records. A teacher generates a critique and final decision; if the decision matches the annotated ground truth, the full trace is preferred, otherwise it is rejected. Removing the critique from the same record yields a direct-outcome pair that concentrates the signal on the final judgment. Reversing the task yields a deduction pair in which the real judged response is preferred over a weaker model's reconstruction.

These are not redundant rephrasings. The critique record trains explanatory reasoning, the direct record prevents the long critique from diluting outcome tokens, and the deduction record forces the judge to connect a judgment to the response content that earned it. Mixing them creates a multi-task preference-data consumer. The design keeps ground truth at the point of selection but allows generated traces to supply richer contrastive supervision than an annotation alone.
