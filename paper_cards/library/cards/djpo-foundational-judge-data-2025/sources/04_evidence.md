The paper evaluates seven pairwise, four single-rating, and two classification benchmarks. It reports that the 8B judge has stronger aggregate pairwise performance than GPT-4o and specialized judge baselines, while the 70B judge exceeds the next strongest listed 70B pairwise-only baseline by nearly two percent in aggregate. The judges are top two on six of seven pairwise benchmarks and also handle ties in Auto-J better than comparison models. On MetaCritique, the 70B model records 93.10 precision, 70.54 recall, and 77.60 F1 for critique quality.

The ablation connects the broad result to the three-record mixture. Removing any task reduces overall coverage; direct judgment gives sizable pairwise gains, while response deduction contributes to pairwise and classification performance even if it can slightly reduce single-rating performance. In a downstream experiment, the 70B judge scores generator outputs for UltraFeedback so that the highest score becomes a positive response and the lowest a negative response for another DPO run. This demonstrates that the constructed judge can itself become a training-data producer.
