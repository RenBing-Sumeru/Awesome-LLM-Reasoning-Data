The authors assemble human- and model-annotated evaluation datasets across single rating, pairwise comparison, and classification. They preserve available rubrics or write aligned rubrics when needed. Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct samples 20 critique-and-judgment outputs per input at temperature 0.7. Matching the final judgment to the ground truth labels a candidate positive; disagreement labels it negative, forming critique preference pairs. Removing the critique produces standard-judgment pairs with the same correctness test.

For response deduction, the model receives a protocol, input, and correct evaluation and must reconstruct the original response. The true original response is positive and a Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct deduction is negative. The curation balances label distributions and mixes 680K pairs at 70 percent critique, 15 percent direct judgment, and 15 percent deduction. DPO together with SFT trains 8B, 12B, and 70B instruction models; all three pair types are ablated separately.
