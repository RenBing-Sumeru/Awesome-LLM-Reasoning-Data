Earlier work proposes methods that generate or optimize preference pairs, but it often treats pair attributes as incidental. This paper changes the evidence standard by holding corpus size fixed and isolating structural prefix sharing, contrast composition, response scores, and prompt difficulty. It therefore supplies selection rules with controlled empirical boundaries instead of assuming that a larger score gap or a more elaborate search procedure is automatically better.

The contribution is not a general instruction dataset or a new preference loss. Its reusable change is a programmatically scored experimental surface for deciding which record characteristics should be retained in a preference corpus. Before applying its conclusions outside instruction following, test whether the target preference can be reliably verified, whether the model scale alters the difficulty optimum, and whether the same contrast mix preserves desirable diversity and safety behavior.
