Use this paper as a design-of-experiments guide before committing to a preference-data generator. Record whether pairs share a prefix, their verifier-score contrast, absolute response scores, prompt difficulty, construction cost, and retained-pair yield; these are training inputs rather than incidental statistics. It is particularly useful for instruction-following data with a programmatic checker, but it should not be used to transfer its exact difficulty or contrast rules to subjective preference data without a controlled pilot.
