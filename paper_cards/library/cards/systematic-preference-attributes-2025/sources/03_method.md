The pipeline starts from seed instruction prompts, removes their original constraints, generates and semantically deduplicates new base prompts, samples non-conflicting constraint combinations, and writes final constrained prompts with Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct. It creates about 48,000 prompts, roughly 16,000 for each of four, five, and six constraints. Programmatic checks score how many constraints a response satisfies, making correctness of a chosen and a rejected response observable.

For rejection sampling, the policy independently samples answers and a high-low score pair is retained. For MCTS, sibling rollouts with high and low reward share their earlier tokens and form a pair. The paper controls dataset size and unique-prompt count while varying the chosen-rejected scores and prompt difficulty, then measures generalization on IFEval and synthetic evaluations. A reproduction must preserve the constraint ontology, prompt generator, verifier, pair sampler, search budget, model, and exact difficulty mixture.
