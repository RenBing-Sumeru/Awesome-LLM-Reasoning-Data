The analysis is restricted to instruction-following prompts with 23 computationally verifiable constraints, so it cannot directly establish how the same choices behave for abstract helpfulness, truthfulness, or value conflicts. The experiments use 8B-scale models and compare only rejection sampling with MCTS, leaving both scaling behavior and other curation procedures unresolved. The synthetic constraints also make score correctness unusually clean; a deployment should measure whether its reward or judge has enough reliability to support the same conclusions.
