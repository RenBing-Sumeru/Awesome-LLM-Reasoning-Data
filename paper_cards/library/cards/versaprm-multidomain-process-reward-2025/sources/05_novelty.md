Prior process-reward work made mathematical traces the default substrate and often treated the trained verifier as the primary contribution. VersaPRM moves the design attention to the training record: it combines domain-balanced question selection, multiple generated traces, reference-conditioned earliest-error judgments, and prefix truncation into an openly released construction recipe.

It also evaluates the consequence of that record design rather than only reporting one model. Random multi-domain subsets outperform equally sized math-only subsets, and held-out-domain experiments remain close to the full model. Those tests support the narrower claim that diversity changes the learned process judgment, rather than merely exposing the reward model to the benchmark's question format.
