The authors manually inspect 64 randomly sampled questions, selecting one all-correct trace and two error-containing traces per question. The automatic labels agree with their judgments 80 percent of the time for traces marked correct and 71 percent for traces marked incorrect; they estimate roughly 75 percent label accuracy overall. Removing both label definitions and reference answers drops the correct-trace agreement from 80 percent to 7 percent on their re-evaluated subset.

With 16 Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct candidates and minimum-score weighted majority voting, VersaPRM reaches 61.22 accuracy overall on MMLU-Pro-CoT-Eval, a 4.07-point gain over majority voting. The non-math aggregate rises by 3.68 points, and law rises from 35.93 to 43.86, a 7.93-point gain; Qwen-2.5-Math-PRM improves law by only 0.35 points in the same table.
