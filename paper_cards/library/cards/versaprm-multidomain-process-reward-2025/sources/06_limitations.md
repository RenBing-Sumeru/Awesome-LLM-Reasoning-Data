The supervisory judge is itself a Llama model and the manual check estimates imperfect labels, so errors or style preferences can be inherited by the released process data. The model still misses factual and arithmetic mistakes in the paper's examples, and the authors report that one-step PRM-guided refinement reduces pass-at-one from 51 percent to 45 percent. The evaluation is largely multiple-choice MMLU-Pro and requires multiple sampled traces at inference, so it is not evidence that the method universally improves open-ended reasoning or reduces serving cost.
