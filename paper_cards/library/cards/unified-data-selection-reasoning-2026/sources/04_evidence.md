Experiments cover mathematics, STEM, and code, with Qwen3-8B-Base for the main supervised study, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B for rejection fine-tuning, and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B for reinforcement learning. On the mathematics SFT table, the highest-HES 20% subset obtains 31.34 average accuracy, close to the 31.83 full-data result and above the 23.86 random-20% result; removing the lowest 20% gives 34.61. The lowest-HES 20% subset falls to 11.75, providing an adverse control rather than only a favorable comparison.

The paper also reports that a Qwen3-0.6B proxy selects a 20% subset for an 8B model at 30.20 average accuracy, versus 31.34 when the 8B model selects its own data. In rejection fine-tuning, the highest-HES option exceeds random selection across the reported candidate budgets. For RL, high-HES positives with random negatives reach 19.26 average accuracy, compared with 17.51 for random positives and negatives and 18.34 for the full batch, while using half as much data per update. These are results in the listed settings, not proof that entropy rank identifies quality for every task.
