The paper limits validation to math, code, and STEM and explicitly leaves agentic planning untested. Its rationale assumes long reasoning paths contain sparse, critical forks; when uncertainty is more uniformly distributed, as in open-ended generation, its score may lose discriminative value. HES is computed once before training, so it cannot react to a model whose weaknesses change during optimization. The checked primary sources provide the paper and an ICLR submission record but do not declare official code or a standalone data release, making independent reproduction depend on reconstructing the stated datasets, models, and training details.
