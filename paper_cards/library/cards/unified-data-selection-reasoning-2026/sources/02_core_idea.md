The central claim is that the informative part of a long reasoning path is concentrated at a small number of uncertain decision points. High-Entropy Sum (HES) sums token entropies only at the highest-entropy positions in a response, rather than averaging uncertainty over every token. The paper interprets a larger sum as evidence that the path negotiated more consequential branching points and is therefore a more valuable positive training example.

The same scalar score is used as a selection interface across three consumers. For supervised fine-tuning it globally ranks completed responses; for rejection fine-tuning it ranks correct candidates for a question; and for reinforcement learning it favors high-HES successful rollouts while retaining randomly sampled failures. The contribution is a common decision rule for records that otherwise enter different optimization stages in different forms.
