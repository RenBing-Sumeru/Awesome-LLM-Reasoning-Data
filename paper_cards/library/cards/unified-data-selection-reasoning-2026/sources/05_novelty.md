The filters compared in the paper either score a whole response with a global statistic, select by a proxy such as length or difficulty, or require an additional learned or large-model judge. HES changes the target to the cumulative entropy of only the response's most uncertain tokens. It keeps this record-level score unchanged while changing the consumer: a curated SFT subset, a correct-candidate subset for rejection fine-tuning, or an asymmetric positive-negative RL batch.

The new element is not entropy itself, nor the general practice of selecting data before training. It is the claim that a sparse token-level statistic can be reused as one training-free selection interface across three post-training regimes. The relative HES variant outperforms the fixed-threshold and average-entropy alternatives in the reported SFT ablation, so the percentile aggregation—not merely any uncertainty score—is the component that should be tested when reusing the idea.
