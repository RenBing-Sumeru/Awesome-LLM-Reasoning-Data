The key distinction is between a quality label and a training-selection signal. HES does not verify that a chain of thought is correct; in the rejection and RL experiments, correctness is supplied before or alongside HES, while HES prioritizes among available records. Read the 20% result with the low-HES control, proxy-model transfer, and asymmetric RL experiment: together they support a practical ranking hypothesis, but they do not remove the need to audit source-data correctness, domain transfer, model dependence, and the absence of a released implementation.
