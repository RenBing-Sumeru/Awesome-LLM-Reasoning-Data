Long chain-of-thought post-training needs many reasoning responses, but retaining every response is expensive and can preserve unhelpful or noisy paths. Existing filters based on length, perplexity, or average token entropy score a whole sequence coarsely. A long response with only a few decisive reasoning turns can therefore resemble a routine or flawed response, while learned selectors or stronger-model judges add another costly component to the pipeline.

This paper asks whether a model can rank reasoning records with a cheap signal already available during generation. The training object is a prompt paired with a long reasoning response or rollout. The decision is which records enter supervised fine-tuning or rejection fine-tuning, and which successful versus failed trajectories enter a reinforcement-learning update; it does not introduce a new benchmark or an inference-time search procedure.
