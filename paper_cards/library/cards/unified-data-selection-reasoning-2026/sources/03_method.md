For a response, the method computes next-token entropy at every position and forms a relative HES score by summing the top p percentile of entropies; the main setting uses p = 0.005. A fixed-threshold variant and alternatives—average entropy, average high-entropy-token entropy, and the sum over all tokens—are also evaluated. The percentile form is intended to avoid directly rewarding length and to adapt the cut-off to each response instead of assuming one global entropy scale.

In supervised fine-tuning, records are ranked and either the highest 20% are retained for an efficiency test or the lowest 20% are removed for a de-noising test. In rejection fine-tuning, 32 responses per question are generated, incorrect responses are removed, and the top 2, 4, or 8 correct trajectories are selected per question or from a global pool. In reinforcement learning, the principal asymmetric batch keeps high-HES correct rollouts but draws incorrect rollouts at random; the reported RL setting uses GRPO on DeepScaleR.
