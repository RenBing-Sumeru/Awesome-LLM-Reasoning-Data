The EMNLP Findings paper evaluates three LLMs on Math, Code, and general instruction-following tasks. It reports that the consistency-based construction improves multilingual alignment and also generalizes to languages outside the construction set.

The paper compares against English-reference baselines that use translation probability, direct translated answers, or an additional aligned model for cross-lingual rewards. This supports the claimed construction choice in those settings, but the actual value of a consistency metric remains task-, language-, and model-dependent.
