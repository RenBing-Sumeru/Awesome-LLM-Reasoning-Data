At every iteration, the current policy regenerates three responses for each sampled prompt, producing pairwise candidates. ActiveDPO computes LoRA gradients for the policy's implicit reward, normalizes them to avoid length bias, randomly projects them to 8,192 dimensions, and greedily selects 50 pairs with the largest diversity-aware gradient uncertainty. The selected pairs are then assigned preference labels and used for DPO.

The authors use batch acquisition so gradients and policy updates need not be recomputed after every individual label. They evaluate Llama-2-7B, Gemma-2B, and Qwen3-4B on TLDR summarization and WebGPT question answering; for feasible repeated experiments, reward models trained on the original human feedback emulate the labels for newly generated pairs.
