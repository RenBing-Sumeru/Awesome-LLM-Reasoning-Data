LIMR introduces Learning Impact Measurement to score samples by their alignment with the model’s learning process. A record therefore includes the prompt, answer, source, and selection consequence; rule-based final-answer correctness supplies the online reward, while LIM determines where training effort is spent.

The central claim is that selection can be more important than count. The released selected subset is useful only alongside the model, impact-estimation procedure, reward parser, and rollout budget that produced the ranking.
