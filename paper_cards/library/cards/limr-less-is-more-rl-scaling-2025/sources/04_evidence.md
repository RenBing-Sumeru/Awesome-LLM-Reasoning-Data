The paper reports that 1,389 selected samples match or exceed the 8,523-sample set, including a 16.7% AIME24 improvement over the full set, and reports gains over LIMO and s1 on MATH500. This directly supports the authors’ selection comparison under their 7B-scale math-RL setup.

The evidence does not show that the ranking is stable across base models, training stages, or domains. Impact estimates are themselves model- and budget-dependent, while final-answer reward does not observe flawed reasoning that happens to end correctly.
