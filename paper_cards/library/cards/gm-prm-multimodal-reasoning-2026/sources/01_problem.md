Multimodal mathematical solutions can fail because a model misreads a diagram, performs invalid algebra, or carries an earlier visual mistake into later text. A final-answer reward cannot identify which step failed or whether the repair should revisit the image evidence.

GM-PRM asks how a process reward model can judge image-grounded reasoning and provide a correction when it finds the first error. It treats visual alignment and mathematical logic as separate but jointly necessary parts of a step judgement.
