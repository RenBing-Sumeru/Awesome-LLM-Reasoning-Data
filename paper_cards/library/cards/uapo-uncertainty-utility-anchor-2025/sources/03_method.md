The paper first analyzes pairwise Bradley-Terry assumptions from data, optimization, and annotator-behavior perspectives. It then defines a utility anchor associated with a prompt and reformulates preference reward around that anchor. The model estimates a reference anchor of uncertainty and uses the resulting quantity as an uncertainty-related reward penalty. This construction is designed to make the reward representation less dependent on a single observed pair and more robust to distribution shift.

For data use, the authors construct multiple forms from three Princeton NLP preference datasets and compare conventional pairwise training with multiple-dataset settings. UAPO can process unpaired records, whereas ordinary pairwise objectives would need a partner response. A simplified SimUAPO variant fixes the reward anchor to zero for a multiple-dataset experiment. The reported implementation counts three forward/backward passes for three data pairs, and analyzes the relation between learned anchors and rewards across prompts.
