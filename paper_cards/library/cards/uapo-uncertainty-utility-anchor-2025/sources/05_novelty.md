The distinctive move is to add a third reference object to offline preference learning: not only a policy and a reference model, but also a prompt-conditioned utility anchor. This changes how incomplete preference records are interpreted. Instead of insisting on a direct comparison between two responses, the objective can relate a response to an anchor that represents uncertain utility, allowing unpaired records to contribute.

The work also makes uncertainty part of the reward contract rather than a post-hoc confidence score. Its data-efficiency claim comes from admitting records that pairwise DPO would leave unused, and its robustness claim comes from penalizing uncertainty around the learned anchor. This does not establish that every unpaired response is helpful, but it offers a concrete mechanism for testing record reuse rather than assuming pairing is the only valid preference format.
