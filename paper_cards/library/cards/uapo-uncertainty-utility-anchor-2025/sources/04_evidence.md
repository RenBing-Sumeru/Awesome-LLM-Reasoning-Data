The paper compares UAPO with IPO, CPO, KTO, ORPO, R-DPO, and other offline objectives on Mistral-Instruct, Llama-3-Instruct, and Gemma-2-Instruct using pairwise datasets. It reports competitive results without a strict pairing dependency and analyzes robustness under distribution shifts. The central empirical claim is not simply a single benchmark peak: the method can use multiple data constructions and preserve performance while changing the assumed form of supervision.

The multiple-dataset study gives the most direct evidence for the data-use claim. UAPO and its simplified variant improve when they are trained with multiple datasets, while the paper reports that this benefit is not uniform across every model distribution. Visualizations place the learned utility anchor between winning and losing response rewards, and prompt examples show the anchor differs across inputs. These analyses support the interpretation of the anchor as prompt dependent rather than a static scalar, while also revealing dependence on source mixture.
