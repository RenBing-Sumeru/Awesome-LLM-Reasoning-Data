For each task, the pipeline supplies few-shot input-output demonstrations to GPT-4o and samples candidate hypotheses. The teacher then follows each candidate rule on the demonstrations, producing a noisy fitness score. The authors retain hypotheses satisfying at least half of the examples for rule-generation and rule-following SFT; pairs separated by a score threshold form the preference corpus.

The student is first fine-tuned with LoRA on both kinds of SFT examples and then aligned to prefer stronger rules. The paper compares preference objectives and reports ORPO as best overall in this setting. Its published data statistics include 39,736 rule-generation SFT examples, 88,369 rule-following examples, and 108,768 preference examples; held-out task instances provide the final accuracy test.
