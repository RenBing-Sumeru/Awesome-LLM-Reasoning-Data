Prior distillation commonly teaches a student to imitate answers or long traces. ReDis changes the training record by making a candidate rule and its demonstration-consistency score first-class objects, then uses them for both corpus filtering and preference construction.

Its novelty is therefore the rule-level supervision interface and the link from a noisy teacher hypothesis to a measurable consistency test. Generic SFT, hypothesis search, and preference optimization are components; the contribution is how they are connected to decide which reasoning records enter training.
