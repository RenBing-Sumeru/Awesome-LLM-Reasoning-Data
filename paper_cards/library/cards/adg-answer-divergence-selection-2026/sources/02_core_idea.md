The final training object is still an instruction-response pair, but selection first creates a five-answer behavior record for the intended backbone. Rather than treating one target response as the sole observation, ADG pools representations from several sampled answers and asks how far apart, and in which directions, they lie. This makes selection explicitly model-relative: a prompt can be informative for one backbone and uninformative for another.

The score combines dispersion magnitude with anisotropy, so high value requires both meaningful variation and a multi-directional answer shape rather than one elongated cluster. Examples are then placed into semantic bins and selected under proportional quotas. Coverage is part of the decision: without it, a high-scoring but narrow topic region could consume the 10K budget and reduce the variety of supervision seen during fine-tuning.
