The main experiments use Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct to write principles, then use Qwen2-7B or Llama3-8B SFT or instruct models as the policy and generator. For a query x, the good and bad principles are system messages forming two conditioned queries; the generator samples their answers and an unconditioned answer. The good loss combines a base preference loss with SFT on the good answer.

The rejected-side loss is deliberately not the symmetric bad-answer loss. It applies the base loss to the bad query, original answer, and good answer, plus SFT on the original answer, so bad conditioning does not force an unnatural response. The authors optimize the generator on 8K UltraFeedback queries, sample with temperature 0.7 and top-p 0.8, and align the policy on the full 60K-query collection with DPO in the main setup.
