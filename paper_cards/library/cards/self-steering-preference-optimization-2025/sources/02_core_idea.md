For each query, a principle generator writes contrastive good and bad principles. The policy-derived generator answers the query with each principle and also without a principle, producing a good response, a bad response, and an original response. The original answer is important because it anchors the rejected-response objective near what the policy would naturally produce.

SSO trains that generator with two linked terms. One separates good and rejected responses so the pair is accurate; the other makes the original response preferable to the good response under the bad-principle query, preventing the rejected distribution from being pushed into an implausible low-probability region. Only after this data-generation stage are the resulting pairs given to DPO or another xPO consumer.
