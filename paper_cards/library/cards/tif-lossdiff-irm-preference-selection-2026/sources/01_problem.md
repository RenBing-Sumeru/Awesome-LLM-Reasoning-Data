Preference cleaning often assumes a pair is universally good or bad. The paper argues that its value depends on the current model: the same chosen/rejected pair can improve one policy and harm another, while external rewards and generic judges miss that dependence.

It asks how to identify pairs that actually improve validation behavior for a particular alignment run. The authors use a truncated influence function as the reference notion of value, then seek a tractable score that can select records before DPO or SLiC training.
