SparsePO starts from a token-level preference formulation and inserts a mask value between epsilon and one for each token. The reward mask multiplies the policy/reference log ratio in the chosen and rejected responses; the KL mask multiplies next-token KL divergence. The resulting objective compares the masked reward totals while subtracting the difference between masked KL terms. The paper permits the two masks to be identical or to be learned separately, so reward emphasis and reference regularization need not follow the same positions.

For activation-based masks, the method standardizes and averages feed-forward, residual, and attention activations across layers of the reference model. For learnable masks, small ReLU networks read each layer's hidden states and a final projection combines them; ReLU induces sparse values automatically. The masks are trained alongside preference optimization and ignored after training. Experiments use synthetic sentiment preferences, Anthropic HH dialogue preferences, and Reddit TL;DR summary preferences, comparing dense, common sparse, and independent sparse configurations.
