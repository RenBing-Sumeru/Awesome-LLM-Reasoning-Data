SparsePO's main novelty is to make both sides of preference optimization selectively consumable. Earlier objectives may change the response-level reward, or selectively attend to tokens for one signal, but this work exposes separate per-token choices for reward and KL regularization. A token can be important for moving toward a preference without being equally important for staying close to the reference model, and the independent-mask formulation encodes that distinction directly in the loss.

It further avoids a separate external selector by deriving masks from the reference model or learning them on the fly. The sparse mask is not a preprocessing label attached once to a dataset; it evolves during optimization with the policy and target preference proxy. This makes the contribution a method for changing the optimization surface of fixed preference pairs, rather than a new preference-data collection or a generic inference-time diversity technique.
