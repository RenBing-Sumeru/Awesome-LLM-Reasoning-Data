SparsePO treats the contribution of a token as a learnable control variable. One mask weights the token's reward-related log-ratio, and another masks the token-level KL divergence from the reference policy. A small mask means that a token contributes little to the respective part of the loss, permitting the model to concentrate alignment pressure on positions that signal the target preference and to relax unnecessary reference anchoring elsewhere.

The method offers two ways to obtain these weights. A dense variant aggregates standardized activations from the reference model and uses one common mask. A sparse variant learns feed-forward mask heads from hidden representations; ReLU naturally creates many low-weight positions and the reward and KL masks can either be shared or independent. These masks exist only while training and are not required at inference, so the change lies in how existing preference records are consumed.
