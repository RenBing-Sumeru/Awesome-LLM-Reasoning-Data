Mathematical-reasoning post-training often fixes a dataset or relies on a teacher-defined difficulty score. Both decisions can become stale as the learner changes: an item that initially provides useful challenge can later be trivial, while an item that is difficult to a teacher or a human may be irrelevant or unlearnable for the current student. Continuing to optimize on either extreme wastes preference-training samples.

This work asks how the current model can determine which mathematical prompts and generated answer pairs should form its next offline preference-optimization set. The intervention is data acquisition during iterative training, not merely a different DPO loss. The authors seek a mixture aligned with the model's weak knowledge areas and its present difficulty frontier, so that the records used for the next update remain useful as competence evolves.
