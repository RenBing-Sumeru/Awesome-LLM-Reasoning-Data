Use this study when constructing iterative preference data for a reasoning learner whose weaknesses can be measured repeatedly. Record the candidate-pool version, correctness verifier, rollout count and decoding settings, embedding model and clustering parameters, error-cluster statistics, pass-rate and path-complexity formulas, extreme filtering rule, selected prompt IDs, and preferred/rejected solutions. Compare against random and fixed-difficulty selection with equal numbers of prompts and iterations. The paper is especially useful for avoiding the common shortcut of treating teacher-defined difficulty as the learner's difficulty; however, its resource claims and mathematics-only scope should not be extrapolated to online RL or other domains without new experiments.
