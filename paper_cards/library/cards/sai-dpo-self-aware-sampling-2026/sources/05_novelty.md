Prior dynamic reasoning training can iterate model updates, but it need not make the training mixture explicitly dependent on a measured map of the learner's knowledge weaknesses and current solvability. SAI-DPO makes the data record's eligibility conditional on both. It distinguishes a prompt that is genuinely informative for this model from one merely assigned a high external difficulty label.

The contribution is not to claim that all hard examples should be oversampled. Its filter removes both solved and unsolved extremes before ranking the remaining prompts, while semantic clustering supplies a second axis for coverage of weak areas. This combines an error-conditioned knowledge distribution with a learner-conditioned difficulty estimate and turns the combination into concrete preference pairs for DPO. The resulting object is a changing training dataset, not just a post-hoc analysis of benchmark errors.
