The central distinction is between an external label of difficulty and a student-relative estimate of trainability. SAI-DPO needs both a knowledge dimension—where the learner fails—and a difficulty dimension—whether the learner can productively improve on a particular prompt now. Its preference triples are produced after this decision, so the work belongs with training-data usage rather than with a standalone DPO algorithm. When reading the results, distinguish the gain over the random iterative baseline from the remaining gap to PPO, and inspect whether the cost of repeated rollouts and annotation is acceptable for the intended setting.
