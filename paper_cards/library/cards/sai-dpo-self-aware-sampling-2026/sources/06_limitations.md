The authors evaluate sampling only with offline methods such as iterative DPO and do not test the policy on mainstream online methods such as PPO; their final offline score also remains below the PPO result in the efficiency table. Experiments are limited to mathematical reasoning, so transfer to code or general instruction tasks is unknown. The approach depends on generated correctness labels, semantic clustering, and repeated rollouts, each of which can introduce cost or error; it also benefits from a stronger knowledge-tag annotator. No official implementation or newly released dataset is linked by the paper, so exact reproduction requires reconstructing the pipeline and verifying the upstream data and evaluation settings.
