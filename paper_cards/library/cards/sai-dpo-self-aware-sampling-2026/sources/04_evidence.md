On Qwen2.5-7B-Math-Base, SAI-DPO reports a 53.7 average over eight math benchmarks, compared with 52.0 for iterative DPO; its AIME24 score is 23.7 versus 19.4 and AMC23 is 63.3 versus 60.4. On Llama3.1-8B-Instruct, the average is 28.7 versus 26.9, and on Qwen3-8B it is 59.2 versus 58.4. The strongest improvements are concentrated on more difficult competition-style tests rather than saturated GSM8K results.

The efficiency table compares 48K selected offline-RL records with 67K for iterative DPO and 400K samples for PPO, yielding 53.7 versus 52.0 and 54.6 respectively for the specified Qwen base model. Ablating semantic alignment reduces the average by 2.3 points, removing self-aware difficulty by 1.2, and removing pass rate by 1.6. The paper also finds that an externally defined difficulty schedule underperforms the internal measurement, with a 49.9 average versus 53.7, and that the selected subset can improve rejection-sampling SFT by three average points.
