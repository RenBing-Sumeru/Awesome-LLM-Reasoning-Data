The change is to make two granularities of the same reasoning process coequal supervision targets. Long-CoT SFT normally treats detailed narration as the target, while brevity methods often compress only after training. This approach inserts a structure-preserving short trace into the training distribution, so the learner repeatedly sees that the same solution can be expressed at different costs.

That is different from simply asking the model to be concise at inference time. The claimed efficiency is learned from target design and checked with output length. It does not establish that every long trace has a faithful short equivalent; reuse should inspect whether the rewriting preserves decisive operations, whether mixture proportions match the target domain, and whether length is measured consistently.
