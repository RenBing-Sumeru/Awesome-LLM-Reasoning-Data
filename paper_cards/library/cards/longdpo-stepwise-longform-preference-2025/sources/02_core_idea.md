LongDPO searches a tree of possible next writing steps instead of sampling complete documents once. A global memory pool stores verified facts from earlier selected steps and screens new candidates for contradiction. At each layer, the best consistent sibling becomes the chosen step and a random sibling becomes the rejected step, turning local alternatives into preference supervision.

Some selected steps are still low quality. For those below a reward threshold, an external critic compares the chosen step with a stronger sibling under the relevant writing principle and produces improvement suggestions. The generator rewrites the selected step using up to three high-confidence suggestions. Stepwise DPO then trains on these localized, consistency-aware pairs while ordinary DPO consumes the accompanying UltraFeedback examples.
