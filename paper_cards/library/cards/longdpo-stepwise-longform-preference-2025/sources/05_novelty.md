Existing long-form DPO commonly compares complete responses, even though factual and structural mistakes are often localized. LongDPO moves the pair-construction decision into a tree at each writing step. It joins reward-based selection to a persistent factual-memory check, so a high local reward is insufficient when the candidate contradicts previously accepted content.

The critique stage is also selective rather than universal: only low-reward chosen steps are refined, and the critic grounds suggestions in a better sibling and a specific writing principle. This combines search, data filtering, targeted revision, and a prefix-conditioned DPO objective into one data-to-training pipeline. The novelty is the control of local training records, not simply the use of MCTS or critique by itself.
