On the 100K pool, ActPRM reaches ProcessBench F1 of 0.673 using half the annotation budget, matching full-data tuning and outperforming random selection by 3.3 points at the same 50 percent budget. With 62.5 percent of the budget, it reaches 0.680.

At scale, ActPRM reports average ProcessBench F1 of 0.750, and ActPRM-X reports 0.760, compared with 0.735 for Qwen2.5-Math-PRM-7B and 0.743 for Universal-PRM in the reported table. On PRMBench, ActPRM-X reports 0.667; the ablations also show that combining aleatoric and epistemic selection works better than using either alone.
