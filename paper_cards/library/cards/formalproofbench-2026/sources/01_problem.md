FormalProofBench: Can Models Write Graduate Level Math Proofs That Are Formally Verified? asks how graduate-level mathematics statements, Lean 4 theorem contexts, submitted proof terms, and kernel verdicts can provide a rigorous outcome-data surface rather than a subjective model judgment. Its decision boundary is programmatically verifiable outcomes: the result is a checker verdict, not merely a persuasive natural-language answer.

Source and audit record: FormalProofBench: Can Models Write Graduate Level Math Proofs That Are Formally Verified? — https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26996. Venue/date: ICLR 2026 VerifAI-2 Workshop / arXiv; first public release: 2026-03-02. Deterministic verifier contract: Lean 4 kernel acceptance of a submitted proof. Data/evaluation object: graduate-level mathematics statements, Lean 4 theorem contexts, submitted proof terms, and kernel verdicts. Actual pipeline: A model receives a formal theorem and its imported context, writes a Lean proof, and the benchmark records compilation success or failure from Lean 4. Quality/impact signal: ICLR 2026 VerifAI-2 paper; graduate-level Lean benchmark from Vals AI and EPFL authors. Reuse/audit caveat: Pin the Lean/mathlib version, theorem imports, split provenance, and statement licenses before treating accepted proofs as reusable training data.
