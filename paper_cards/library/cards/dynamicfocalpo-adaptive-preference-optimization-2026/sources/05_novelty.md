FocalPO already suppresses poorly ranked pairs, but it applies that decision with the same strength for the entire run. DynamicFocalPO's new decision is temporal: a pair's current ranking and the training stage jointly determine its influence. This creates an optimizer-level curriculum without needing a separate difficulty annotator, a data ordering pass, or a new batch of preference examples.

The paper additionally derives how the schedule changes entropy regularization and gradient suppression, then verifies the intended shift in gradient-contribution ratios. This is more specific than simply annealing a generic hyperparameter: the schedule changes the effective consumer of the preference dataset from early reliable pairs to a broader set of pairs later. Its novelty is therefore about the training objective's evolving treatment of the same records.
