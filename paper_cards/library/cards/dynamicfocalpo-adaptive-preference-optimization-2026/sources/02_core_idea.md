DynamicFocalPO extends FocalPO by replacing its single focusing parameter with a schedule. A high early parameter sharply suppresses gradients from pairs whose implicit rewards are currently misranked, so optimization concentrates on pairs the model can already order correctly. As the parameter declines, the suppression weakens and those difficult pairs increasingly participate, bringing the objective closer to ordinary DPO.

This produces a curriculum without changing the stored preference records. The same record has different effective weight at different training times, determined by its current margin and the scheduled parameter. The authors compare linear, exponential, and cosine decay, and argue that cosine annealing yields a smoother transition from reliable signal to challenge examples than a fixed focal setting.
