The loss retains DPO's implicit reward margin for a chosen and rejected response, then multiplies its contribution by a focal term with a time-dependent exponent. The authors set the exponent high at the beginning and reduce it toward a small value by the end; when it approaches zero, the modulating term approaches one and the update approaches DPO. Their gradient analysis identifies this as early selective suppression followed by later recovery of hard-pair gradients.

For the main experiments, they train Mistral-Base-7B on UltraFeedback and Llama-3-Instruct-8B on Llama3-ultrafeedback-armorm for one epoch with batch size 128. The reported shared schedule bounds are 0.10 and 0.01, with cosine, linear, and exponential variants; cosine is strongest. Baselines are DPO, SimPO, FocalPO with its published value, and a grid-tuned fixed FocalPO value, so the test isolates scheduling rather than a different data source.
