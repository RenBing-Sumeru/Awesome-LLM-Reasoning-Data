The procedure first constructs a safety-degraded reference and a utility-oriented reference, then computes each target token's loss under both models. Their difference becomes the token-risk score, allowing target positions across the whole training pool to be ranked together instead of making a separate keep-or-drop judgment for each response. The global top-risk cutoff makes the masking ratio an explicit design choice rather than a side effect of whole-example filtering.

The selected high-risk tokens are masked before LoRA supervised fine-tuning, while lower-risk targets remain available for learning. TOSS-Pro repeats the harmful-reference refinement with selected examples and reruns the scoring process. The reported setup draws from REDORCA, including 90K SlimOrca instances and 22K harmful Anthropic Red Teaming instances; official code and checkpoints are public, but the method does not release a new standalone dataset or make its mask a universal safety label.
