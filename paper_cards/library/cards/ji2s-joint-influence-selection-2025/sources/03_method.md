JI2S first LoRA-fine-tunes the target model for four epochs on a random 5% warmup of Alpaca and retains intermediate parameter states. At every state, it extracts gradient features for all Alpaca candidates and LIMA reference examples in the LoRA parameter space, then randomly projects features to 8,192 dimensions to reduce cost. The first-order term aggregates the candidate update's relation to proxy-test gradients, while the second-order term aggregates pairwise candidate interactions through the proxy Hessian.

The authors sum these estimates over training steps and combine marginal and joint terms with weight lambda, selecting the lowest-scoring top-K records. The main setting uses lambda=0.1 and K=1,000 from roughly 52K Alpaca instruction-response pairs. Final LoRA SFT evaluates LLaMA2-7B, Mistral-7B, and LLaMA2-13B against full Alpaca, LIMA, AlpaGasus, and DEITA, using Open LLM Benchmarks, MT-Bench, and GPT-4 pairwise evaluation. The paper says code is available, but its current official primary sources do not expose a verifiable repository URL, so this Card does not invent one.
