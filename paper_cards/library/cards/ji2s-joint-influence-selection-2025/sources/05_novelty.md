Influence-based selection usually scores a record by its marginal effect on a target loss. JI2S changes the selection target from an additive list of per-record utilities to an approximation that includes pairwise interactions. This is material for SFT data use because the final decision is not just whether one record looks useful, but whether it remains useful in the set being assembled.

The method also makes the proxy preference corpus part of the selection contract. Instead of asking a large judge to directly score every Alpaca prompt, it uses gradients on LIMA to define desirable downstream movement. Its novelty is therefore the conjunction of a human-preference proxy, LoRA-space trajectory features, and a second-order redundancy-sensitive ranking. It does not release a new base corpus or change the SFT objective itself.
