The method models only pairwise interactions; higher-order interactions could capture richer dependence but are computationally impractical. Its selection inherits the topical and stylistic coverage of LIMA, so bias or narrowness in that proxy can be amplified in the final subset. Influence estimates are approximate, depend on a 5% LoRA warmup, random projection, Hessian approximation, and the chosen joint weight, and are expensive compared with simple heuristic filters. Evidence is limited to Alpaca-style English instruction tuning and three older open models. Although the paper states code is available, no verifiable author repository URL is surfaced by the official paper page or extract, which limits immediate artifact reuse.
