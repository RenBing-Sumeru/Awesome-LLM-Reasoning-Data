The method evaluates a candidate record against LIMA, a 1,000-example human-curated instruction set used as a proxy for the intended behavior. It estimates first-order marginal influence from the record's parameter update and second-order joint influence from its interaction with another training record through an approximate Hessian on that proxy set. The final score combines the two quantities, allowing redundancy or non-additive effects to affect selection.

A low combined score is ranked as more influential, and the top 1,000 records from Alpaca form the final SFT corpus. The key claim is that the selected set should not be understood as 1,000 independently good prompts: its quality partly comes from avoiding overlapping records that a marginal-only ranking would retain. LIMA is therefore not copied into the output corpus; it is the lens that defines which Alpaca records the target model will consume.
