The evidence is centered on three-way NLI and cannot establish that the same selection rules improve open-ended generation, tool use, or broad instruction following. Difficulty, uncertainty, and benchmark categorization are model- and task-dependent, while synthetic labels can introduce errors not visible from aggregate accuracy. API fine-tuning hides the internal mechanism and limits reproducibility across provider versions. The authors also note that other tasks and more challenging datasets need study; generated data, candidate pools, and source benchmarks require license and contamination checks before reuse.
