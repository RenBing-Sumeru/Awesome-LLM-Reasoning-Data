The paper reports that ordinary fine-tuning improves in-distribution NLI while reducing challenge-OOD accuracy substantially; it cites drops of 10.95 percentage points for Gemini-2.0-Flash in its main comparison. Under the fixed 10K budget, uncertainty sampling is the strongest reported selection approach for challenge-OOD settings, while generated data consistently helps standard-OOD datasets.

The results also show the intended trade-off: simple synthetic examples can harm challenge-OOD performance, but increasing generation complexity improves both standard and challenge OOD results. The evaluation spans twelve datasets and more than 2,400 model-dataset evaluations, but remains a single task family and depends on how the authors categorize benchmark difficulty. This is good evidence for the record-mixture mechanism, not a general proof for all instruction fine-tuning.
