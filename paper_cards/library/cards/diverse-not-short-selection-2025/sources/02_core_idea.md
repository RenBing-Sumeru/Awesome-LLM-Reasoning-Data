Diverse-NS makes length a control variable in preference-data construction. A generated rewrite should be preferred not just because it has a higher entropy score, but because it is more diverse and higher quality than the ordinary response at comparable length. This changes the meaning of a preference label: it denotes diversity gain that cannot be explained away by giving the model fewer words to repeat.

The method also uses self-generated contrast rather than collecting a separate human-written target. An initial response represents the policy's natural tendency; a second call rewrites it under constraints intended to reduce repetition. Filtering then transforms the two responses into a quality-checked, length-matched learning signal. The resulting DPO record therefore attaches the training preference to a specific behavioral contrast—ordinary continuation versus a more varied continuation—rather than to a generic reward ranking.
