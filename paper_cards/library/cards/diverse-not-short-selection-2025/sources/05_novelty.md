The central novelty is to treat length matching as part of the preference-labeling contract, not merely as an evaluation correction. Earlier diversity-oriented selection can find high-entropy completions by favoring responses with fewer opportunities to repeat. Diverse-NS requires an improvement to survive a direct, same-prompt comparison under an explicit five-word length bound, so the selected record encodes a different training objective.

It also demonstrates a compact self-learning construction with a clear consumer: candidate generation, filtering, and DPO are linked in one record-level pipeline. The method does not claim that all creative answers have an objective ordering. Instead, it records a carefully bounded local preference between a default answer and a rewrite. The result is useful evidence that even a small set of pairs can change open-ended behavior when their selection criteria match the intended property.
