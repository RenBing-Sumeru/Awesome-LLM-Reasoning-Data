The authors create creative-writing prompts by sampling three-word sets, then use Llama-3.1-8B and Olmo-2-7B to produce a first five-sentence story and a second, constrained rewrite. In the final collection, each generator produces 200,000 prompt/first-response/second-response tuples from 20,000 prompts. The first response is provisionally rejected and the rewrite provisionally chosen, giving every record a naturally aligned contrast for preference learning.

A pair is retained only if the rewrite scores at or above the median first-response quality, exceeds the first response in ArmoRM quality and entropy, and differs by no more than five words. The authors sort retained pairs by entropy gain and use the top 3,000 for DPO, with the same budget applied to baselines. They then train the generating model on its own pairs and separately test whether Olmo-2-7B pairs can teach the larger Olmo-2-13B model.
