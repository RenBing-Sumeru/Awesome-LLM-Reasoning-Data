Preference tuning can make language-model responses less varied even when it improves alignment. Existing diversity scores and reward models are negatively correlated with response length, so a pair-selection rule that simply maximizes a diversity metric can reward a shorter chosen answer rather than a genuinely more expressive one. The paper demonstrates this failure in the closest baseline, where chosen responses are roughly fifty words shorter on average.

The paper asks how to construct preference records that teach a model to produce more diverse creative responses without silently changing the target into brevity. Its solution is to generate an ordinary response and a controlled rewrite for the same prompt, then keep a pair only when the rewrite improves diversity and quality while remaining nearly the same length as the original.
