The paper evaluates the variants on twelve agent benchmarks. Its unified variant reports 43.7% on GAIA and 46.2% on WebWalkerQA, results the authors use to argue that analysis, critique, and score can work together as a useful training signal.

These numbers are evidence for the reported agents, benchmarks, and evaluation protocol, not proof that an LLM judge is reliable in every environment. They support the value of the proposed feedback interface while leaving transfer to different tools, tasks, and judges as an empirical question.
