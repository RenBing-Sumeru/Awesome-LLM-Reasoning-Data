Earlier agent reinforcement-learning systems commonly retain only task success, a terminal reward, or a single numerical process score. Such representations provide little reusable explanation of why a trajectory should be changed.

The new contribution is to make analysis, corrective critique, and scalar assessment one reusable training record, then to compare text-only, score-only, and joint consumption of that record. The novelty is therefore in the feedback object and its use in optimization, rather than in an isolated optimizer or inference heuristic.
