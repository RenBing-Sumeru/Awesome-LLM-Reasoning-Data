Reinforcement fine-tuning commonly draws batches uniformly from a full reasoning dataset. This gives equal budget to already solved problems, redundant near-neighbors, and examples too difficult to yield useful feedback. Static curricula can have the same failure: a threshold fixed before training cannot follow a learner whose competence changes during optimization.

SPaCe asks two coupled data-use questions: which compact subset should remain available for reinforcement fine-tuning, and which part of that subset should be sampled at each moment. Its intervention is a training-data policy rather than a new reward model. The aim is to keep semantic coverage while concentrating updates on clusters that are challenging, learnable, and still making progress for the current model.
