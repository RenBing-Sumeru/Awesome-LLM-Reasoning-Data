Before training, SPaCe extracts embeddings with Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B, reduces dimensions with PCA, standardizes the embedding and one per-example attribute, and applies k-means. It chooses a fixed quota from each cluster using greedy farthest-point sampling. The clustering is intentionally performed only once, preserving a stable identity for each bandit arm; changing clusters online would invalidate accumulated solve-rate statistics.

At each training step, Thompson sampling selects one cluster from its hardness and pull-count statistics. A batch from that cluster receives binary correctness rewards and updates both GRPO and the selected arm's cumulative reward. If a cluster's solve rate fails to improve by a tolerance for consecutive updates, its sampling mean is reduced. The main experiments use DeepScaleR subsets, GSM8K, MATH, and Knights and Knaves; with at most ten clusters and ten representatives per cluster, several settings train on no more than 100 examples.
