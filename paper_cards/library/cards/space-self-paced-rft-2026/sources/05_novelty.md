Earlier data reduction can select a one-off subset, while curriculum methods can order examples through a fixed or threshold-based difficulty rule. SPaCe combines the two at the object that training consumes: a semantically and difficulty-aware reduced pool is prepared once, then its clusters are scheduled online using rewards from the learner. This makes data coverage and temporal allocation explicit parts of one training policy.

The distinctive decision is not simply to select difficult questions. A cluster remains attractive when it is hard and still improving; stagnant hardness is penalized. Equally, its reduction stage is not a global top-k difficulty filter: every cluster supplies representatives, preserving semantic variety before the bandit decides frequency. The claimed contribution is therefore a progress-sensitive curriculum over a deliberately compact record set.
