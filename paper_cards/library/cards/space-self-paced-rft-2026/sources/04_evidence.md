On DeepScaleR-Uniform, Qwen3-0.6B with SPaCe reaches 79.8 on GSM8K, 78.2 on MATH500, 20.0 on AIME24, and 18.9 on AIME25. The corresponding base scores are 78.0, 75.4, 10.0, and 16.7; the standard GRPO baseline is below SPaCe on all four. For DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, SPaCe reports 72.4, 81.1, 25.6, and 26.7, respectively, and is best on three of the four tests.

The paper averages three seeds for the main learned baselines and tests mathematics, logic puzzles, several data distributions, and models from sub-billion to 8B scale. On Knights and Knaves, it reports 9.9 average accuracy versus 8.1 for GRPO and 7.8 for AdaRFT. Removing the reduction stage lowers Qwen3-0.6B AIME24 from 20.0 to 12.2, supporting the claim that diversity-preserving reduction matters. The authors also show that the scheduler increasingly samples medium-hard clusters with rising solve rates, rather than becoming trapped in easy regions.
