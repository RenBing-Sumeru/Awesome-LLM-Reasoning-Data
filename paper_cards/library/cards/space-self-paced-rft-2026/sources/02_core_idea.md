The framework first makes a stable, reduced training pool. It represents every example by a semantic embedding and a scalar attribute such as difficulty, clusters the concatenated representation, and chooses diverse representatives inside every cluster by farthest-point sampling. Each cluster becomes a persistent curriculum unit, so selection can reduce redundancy without discarding an entire kind of problem.

It then treats the clusters as arms of a multi-armed bandit. During GRPO-based training, online solve rates update each arm; low solve rate denotes hardness, while a stagnation penalty prevents the scheduler from repeatedly choosing a cluster that remains hard but no longer improves. Thompson sampling balances uncertain clusters against difficult ones. Thus the same reduced records are not uniformly consumed: their sampling frequency changes with observed learning progress.
