The experiments cover models smaller than 8B, so scale behavior for larger systems is not established. The method needs a suitable per-example attribute and an embedding model before training; noisy difficulty annotations or poorly chosen clusters can make the arms less meaningful. It also relies on outcome correctness rewards and is demonstrated on mathematical and logical reasoning, not open-ended tasks with subjective feedback. The paper reports use of the Open-R1 codebase and upstream datasets but does not provide an author-declared standalone implementation or new data release, so reproducibility requires reconstructing the selection and scheduling details from the paper and respecting each upstream artifact's terms.
