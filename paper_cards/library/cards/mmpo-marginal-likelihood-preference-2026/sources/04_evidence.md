Across smaller models, the paper reports a more favorable alignment-capability trade-off for MMPO than DPO. At each method's peak general-capability point, MMPO's AlpacaEval 2 win rates are 61.86% for SmolLM 135M, 66.47% for 360M, and 64.03% for Llama 3.2 1B, versus 6.21%, 57.06%, and 46.57% for DPO. The 8B result is mixed: DPO reaches a higher peak win rate, 64.53% versus 63.37%, and a higher accompanying LM Harness accuracy.

The paper reports that MMPO varies little across the three tested beta values, while DPO and SimPO are more sensitive. On SmolLM2-135M, its mean win rate rises from 47.7% at epoch one to 67.3% at epoch five. Ablations show that removing in-batch normalization or the DPO-style auxiliary can raise preference win rate slightly but harms peak LM Harness accuracy; adding the tested token-level entropy term degrades both reported metrics.
