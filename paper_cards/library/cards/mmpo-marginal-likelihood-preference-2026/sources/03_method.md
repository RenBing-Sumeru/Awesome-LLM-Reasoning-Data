The paper starts with an MML formulation in which an output is produced through a partially observed process, then approximates the marginalization with the two responses in a preference pair. In the practical loss, model and reference log probabilities form chosen and rejected scores, those scores are normalized, and log-sum-exp plus a DPO-style logistic auxiliary loss are minimized. The derivation shows why the resulting weighted gradient favors the chosen response without an explicit reward or entropy-maximization term.

Experiments combine 12,359 Orca DPO Pairs training examples and 61,135 UltraFeedback-Binarized pairs. The authors train SmolLM2-135M, SmolLM2-360M, Llama-3.2-1B, and Llama-3-8B variants, tune on validation reward accuracy, then study five epochs under beta values 0.01, 0.05, and 0.5. DPO and SimPO are the direct baselines; the 8B setting uses LoRA on attention projections and an 8-bit Adam optimizer.
