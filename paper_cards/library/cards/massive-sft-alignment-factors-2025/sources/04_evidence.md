The primary evidence is breadth under control: the paper reports more than 1,000 trained SFT models rather than a comparison between two isolated recipes. This scale permits the authors to compare data, layer, and training effects across eleven model families, which is the necessary design for claims about factor sensitivity rather than a single winning checkpoint.

The accompanying repository makes the evaluation record inspectable. Its dataframe includes downstream scores and the configuration variables needed to slice them, while the README identifies the ten-data merged condition and distinguishes full tuning from LoRA. The paper's conclusions should still be read as evidence about its model families, datasets, and benchmark protocol, not as a universal ordering for all alignment work.
