Existing self-instruction approaches commonly start from an instruction and synthesize an answer. Here, high-quality human text is the fixed side of the record, and the model synthesizes the instruction around it, which changes what is inherited from the web and what is generated.

The paper also treats curation as a necessary companion to augmentation. Its contribution is a pipeline in which synthetic prompt generation, model-based filtering, and later fine-tuning all determine whether an unlabeled document becomes a training example.
