After two iterations, the reported LLaMA model outperforms other LLaMA-based systems on the Alpaca leaderboard that do not rely on distillation data. This establishes that the constructed and curated pairs can provide useful instruction-following supervision.

The evidence concerns a particular seed set, web corpus, curation procedure, and leaderboard regime. It does not establish that automatically inferred instructions preserve every intended meaning of the source document or that web-derived data is safe for every deployment.
