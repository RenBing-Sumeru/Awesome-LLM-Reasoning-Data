At each training step, the scheduler samples a dataset according to a probability that combines three terms: a Boltzmann transform of that dataset's exponentially smoothed reward, the original mixture proportion, and a uniform exploration floor. The sharpness parameter controls how strongly high-reward datasets are preferred, while the floor controls the minimum chance that every dataset remains available. Examples are then sampled uniformly within the selected dataset to form the SFT batch.

At a scheduled update interval, the method draws a small batch from every dataset and performs a temporary one-gradient-step probe. Its 1-Step Look-ahead Reward is the normalized reduction in loss from before to after that probe; these rewards are min-max normalized and accumulated with an exponential moving average. The paper trains LLaMA3.2-1B and LLaMA3.1-8B for two epochs on the 16-source TÜLU-2 mixture and the 19-source TÜLU-3 mixture, using batch size 128 and updating the sampling policy from those probes.
