DYNAMIX SFT treats every dataset in an instruction-tuning collection as an arm of a multi-armed bandit. Instead of committing to a static ratio, it estimates how much a short update on each source currently reduces loss, smooths that estimate over time, and raises or lowers the source's sampling probability. The feedback is model-relative and dynamic: the same dataset can receive different weight at different phases of fine-tuning.

Adaptation is deliberately constrained by a prior. The prior-scaled Boltzmann policy multiplies each reward-based weight by the original dataset proportion, and a nonzero uniform floor prevents any source from disappearing entirely. This makes the central decision a balance between exploiting data that currently teaches the model and preserving the diversity and coverage represented by the original mixture.
