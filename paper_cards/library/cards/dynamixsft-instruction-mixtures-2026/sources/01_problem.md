Instruction-tuning collections increasingly combine very different sources, such as general dialogue, mathematics, code, safety, and instruction-following data. A fixed mixture can preserve coverage, but it assumes that every source should be sampled at the same relative rate throughout training. Uniform sampling can distort source coverage, while manually designed ratios and many adaptive mixers require extra models, validation supervision, or costly per-source calculations.

The paper asks how to change the sampling probability of each dataset as a model learns, without abandoning the coverage encoded by the original collection. Its training record is still an ordinary instruction-response example, but the intervention is the source-level sampling policy that decides which collection contributes the next batch. The objective is supervised fine-tuning mixture optimization, not sample-level quality labeling or a new corpus release.
