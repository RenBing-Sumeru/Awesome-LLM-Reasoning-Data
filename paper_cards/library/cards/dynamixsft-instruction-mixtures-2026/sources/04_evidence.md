Across ten evaluations of knowledge, reasoning, coding, mathematics, and instruction following, the dynamic policy beats proportional Full Coverage in the reported averages. On TÜLU-2, it reaches 27.70 versus 26.67 for LLaMA3.2-1B and 52.57 versus 50.00 for LLaMA3.1-8B. On TÜLU-3, it reaches 33.07 versus 31.42 for the 1B model and 63.11 versus 61.55 for the 8B model. The paper summarizes the relative gains over proportional sampling as up to 5.1% and 5.3% for the two collections.

The evidence also tests the costs and components of the policy. Removing the prior drops the TÜLU-2 1B average from 27.7 to 25.9, and the method's reported overhead is 12.7% above naive sampling, compared with 760% for MultiDDS, 380% for MultiUAT, and 139% for HBO. Results are averaged across three runs; the appendix reports standard deviations below 0.25 for DYNAMIX SFT and a t-test below 0.05 against the primary baseline. These findings support the stated setups rather than showing that one mixture rule is optimal for every model, source collection, or training budget.
