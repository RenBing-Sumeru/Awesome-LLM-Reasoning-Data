Earlier work commonly uses a coarser record-level filter or terminal signal; this work makes the listed training decision explicit. The distinction from adjacent work is operational. A paper that only proposes a new optimizer, inference trick, or evaluation metric need not alter the records consumed during training. Here the contribution changes the data-selection boundary, the generated training object, or the reward contract attached to a rollout.

Its contribution is the changed consumer, selection rule, or reward interface, not the generic optimizer alone. This novelty should be evaluated by asking whether another practitioner could implement the changed decision and observe its effect under controlled training. It is not a claim that the surrounding model architecture or every auxiliary component is new.
