Human-human agreement averages 67.5% and human-AI agreement 66.5% across the two ranking protocols, which the authors regard as comparable to prior preference-collection studies. In joint comparisons where both records were either conditionally chosen or conditionally rejected, annotators still made a decisive choice 71% of the time. When one record was conditionally chosen and the other rejected, annotators did not prefer the conditionally chosen record in 48% of comparisons.

On TL;DR and Anthropic Helpful, JPO exceeds DPO by 5.2 and 3.3 win-rate points, respectively, averaged across sampling temperatures. It is 1.8 points higher than DPO on AlpacaEval 2 length-controlled win rate. A JPO model trained only on non-identical joint preferences reaches 71.7% on TL;DR, and a size-matched fifty-fifty joint/conditional mix exceeds DPO by 4.2 points.
