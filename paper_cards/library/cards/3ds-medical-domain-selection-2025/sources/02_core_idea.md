The first stage uses the target model itself as a prompt-based quality rater. Records whose model-generated score clears a high threshold are retained, with the stated purpose of eliminating noisy, redundant, or conflicting information before difficulty selection. This explicit alignment makes compatibility with the target model a prerequisite for admission rather than treating quality as a universal label.

The second stage decomposes difficulty into instruction understanding, confidence in a generated answer, and likelihood of the reference answer. It retains a middle percentile range for every metric rather than only the hardest records, then uses k-center selection over instruction embeddings to preserve diversity. Attention-derived token weights calibrate the two response metrics, making uncertain but semantically important tokens contribute more to the decision about which records enter the 5K SFT budget.
