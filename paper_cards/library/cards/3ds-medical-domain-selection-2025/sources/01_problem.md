Specialized instruction corpora can be large, heterogeneous, and noisy. Training a general model on every medical record may reinforce facts it already knows, introduce conflicts, or expose it to language outside its usable knowledge distribution. Selecting only externally judged high-quality or maximally difficult data does not solve this mismatch, because usefulness depends on the target model's state.

3DS asks which domain instruction-response records a specific model should consume for supervised adaptation. It is a selection policy over a 1.9-million-record medical pool, not a new medical generator. The paper seeks data that is first compatible with the target model's knowledge and then moderately challenging along several stages of solving, so the SFT subset supplies learnable domain signals rather than indiscriminate scale.
