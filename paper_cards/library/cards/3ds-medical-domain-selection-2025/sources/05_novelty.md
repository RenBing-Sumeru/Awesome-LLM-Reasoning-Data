Many selection pipelines use external quality judges or a single notion of difficulty. 3DS makes both decisions model-centric: the same target model first gates quality and then provides three different signals about what it understands, can answer confidently, and can answer correctly. The important change is not simply a more elaborate perplexity score; it changes the admission rule for domain records according to the learner's distribution.

Its second novelty is to reject two extremes on every difficulty dimension. A record can be linguistically difficult yet have a confident hallucinated answer, or easy to parse yet hard to match to the reference. Decomposing these cases lets the selector seek a middle, learnable region rather than a universal hard set. Diversity is added after the compatibility and difficulty decisions, making the final record set a combination of model alignment, multi-axis trainability, and coverage.
