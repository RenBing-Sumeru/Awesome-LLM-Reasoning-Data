The method rates the entire training corpus before fine-tuning, which is costly for a 1.9M-record pool and may limit use on even larger datasets. It is a pre-training selection rather than an online adaptive schedule, so the model's changing capability after SFT is not used to revise the subset. Thresholds are tuned on a medical holdout set and differ across model families, which reduces out-of-the-box portability. The central evidence uses Chinese medical data and automatic lexical metrics for one open-answer task; although law and general-domain tests are included, broader languages, safety requirements, and subjective expert-quality judgments remain insufficiently established.
