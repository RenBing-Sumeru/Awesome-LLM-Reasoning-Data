For a record with instruction and reference answer, 3DS measures instruction-understanding difficulty by prompt perplexity. It generates an answer and measures conditional perplexity for response confidence, then measures conditional perplexity of the reference answer for response correctness. The response scores weight token log probabilities by aggregated attention received from later tokens. These are model-state measurements, so the same record can occupy a different difficulty range for another base model.

Stage one retains target-model quality scores at least 90. Stage two sets per-model lower and upper percentiles on a medical holdout set, keeps examples in the specified middle ranges, and applies k-center sampling to form 5K examples. The main source pool has more than 1.9M Chinese medical records from five named sources. Experiments fine-tune Baichuan2-13B-Chat, Qwen1.5-7B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, and LLaMA3-8B-Instruct, comparing random, full-data SFT, external scorers, difficulty methods, and gradient similarity selection. The authors release the pool and implementation in their official repository.
