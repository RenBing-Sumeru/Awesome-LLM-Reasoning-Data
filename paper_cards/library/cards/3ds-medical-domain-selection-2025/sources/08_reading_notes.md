The two stages answer different questions: first, whether the target model should trust a record enough to consider it; second, whether that record lies in a productive rather than trivial or overwhelming difficulty region. The three difficulty scores should not be collapsed mentally into ordinary response loss, because one is prompt-only and another uses the model's own generated answer. The full-data degradation is a useful caution, but the selected 5K performance also depends on a medical holdout used for thresholds, so a reproduction should separate the general selection principle from its domain-specific calibration.
