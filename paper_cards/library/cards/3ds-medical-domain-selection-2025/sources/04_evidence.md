Across CMB-Exam and MMCU-Med, the reported average for 3DS is 60.61, above the base average of 58.47, random selection at 57.96, and the strongest listed baseline LESS at 58.86. For Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, it reaches 79.06 on CMB-Exam and 85.70 on MMCU-Med, compared with 78.28 and 83.43 before adaptation. Full SFT on the 1.9M pool drops the average to 51.00, providing direct evidence that more unfiltered domain data is not beneficial in this setting.

On the open medical analysis set CMB-Clin, 3DS reports an average of 35.21 across the listed BLEU and ROUGE metrics, compared with 26.15 for IFD and 26.01 for MoDS. Ablations show that removing the prompt-driven stage or any of the three difficulty components lowers results, and the paper reports low correlations and limited overlap among the three difficulty metrics. Additional experiments report improvements in a legal CaseHOLD setting and on MMLU, but the main evidence remains Chinese medical adaptation with selection thresholds tuned on a CMB holdout set.
