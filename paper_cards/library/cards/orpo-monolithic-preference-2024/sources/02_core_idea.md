ORPO retains ordinary supervised likelihood for the favored completion, then adds a small penalty based on the odds ratio of the favored and disfavored completions. The policy is therefore encouraged to imitate good answers while becoming less likely to produce the undesired response style.

The pair is not delegated to a later reward-model or direct-preference stage. It is a single training record with two roles: the chosen response supplies the positive instruction target, and the rejected response supplies contrastive evidence about what the same policy should avoid.
