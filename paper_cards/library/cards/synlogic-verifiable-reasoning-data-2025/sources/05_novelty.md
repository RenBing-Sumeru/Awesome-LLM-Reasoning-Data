SynLogic differs from a fixed logic benchmark by exposing a controllable generation space with its verifier. Synthetic logic and rule-based checking are established techniques; the contribution is to make 35 families, adjustable difficulty, and RL-ready reward contracts available as one scalable data interface.

Its novelty is therefore about controllable data construction, not a claim that a verifier proves human-like reasoning. The generalization value depends on whether training across families transfers to tasks that were not generated by the same templates.
