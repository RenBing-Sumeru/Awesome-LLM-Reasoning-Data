A conventional process reward model judges one reasoning step at a time. That local view can penalize a step that is later repaired, or reward a plausible-looking step even though the surrounding multi-step argument has already lost coherence.

The paper asks how process supervision can represent both local quality and consecutive reasoning context without relying only on costly new annotations. It answers with a hierarchical reward model and a data-augmentation procedure that derives additional multi-step supervision from reasoning trees.
